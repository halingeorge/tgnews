Headteacher struck off for swindling school funds to pay for staff parties

18 Nov 2019, 17:30

A primary school headteacher who used school accounts to pay herself extra money has been struck off.

Wendy Rowlands also fiddled funds to splash out on a staff party with booze, a teachers’ trip to Chester and a hotel room without approval.

An Education Workforce Council panel heard Ms Rowlands wrote cheques to herself for being in charge of a playgroup at Ysgol Swn y Don, Old Colwyn, Wales.

She denied this but the committee found the allegation to be true and heard how she tried to hide her mismanagement from council auditors.

The disgraced teacher did own up to a number of other offences including spending money intended for pupils on the staff party – which was approved by school governors.

The morale boosting trip to Chester – which governors did not approve – was also paid for with funds meant to benefit schoolchildren.

She was given the sack last year when an audit revealed failings in her financial management.

Her misspending is thought to have dated back to 2005, back when she was head at another school which merged to form Ysgol Swn y Don in 2015.

Ms Rowlands did not attend the hearing and argued her behaviour did not amount to unacceptable professional conduct.

The panel found she had acted dishonestly enough to hand her an order banning her from the teaching register.

She can make an application in two years to be considered for reinstatement.

Committee chairman Robert Newsome said they felt the punishment was necessary given how serious the offences were.
