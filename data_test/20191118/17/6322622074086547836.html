Pahang tables 2020 budget with RM4.13 mil in surplus

18 Nov 2019, 17:08 by Bernama

KUANTAN (Nov 18): The Pahang state government today tabled its 2020 Budget with a surplus of RM4.13 million.

Menteri Besar Datuk Seri Wan Rosdy Wan Ismail said the budget allocated RM739.89 million as operating expenditure and RM120.75 million for development expenditure.

"It is an expansionary budget that focuses on infrastructure spending with the development expenditure increased by RM22.7 million, compared with the previous year.

"For 2020, the state government is expected to collect RM744.02 million, an increase by RM40.22 million or 5.71 per cent, compared with 2019 estimates,” he said during the tabling at the state legislative assembly here.

Wan Rosdy said the highest contribution was expected from land revenue of RM417.79 million, followed by forest revenue (RM165.50 million), water revenue (RM3.77 million), federal government grant (RM94.2 million) and other revenues (RM62.76 million).

He also said as at Nov 15, the state government had collected RM15 million in entertainment duty.

Meanwhile, Wan Rosdy said Pahang was expected to collect RM26 million in revenue from sand mining activities under a combined temporary occupation licence and permit next year.

He said the state government was also expected to receive higher revenue from local authorities following the establishment of two sub-districts, namely Daerah Kecil Gebeng and Daerah Kecil Genting, which would see a review on quit rent in both areas.

"For 2020, Daerah Kecil Gebeng is expected to contribute RM200 million a year while Daerah Kecil Genting to contribute RM2 million,” he added. — Bernama
