Gunman kills four and leaves six injured in horror NFL Garden Party shooting

18 Nov 2019, 17:25 by Dan Grennan

Four men were killed and and six others were injured in a mass shooting that took place during a football garden party.

The shooting happened in Fresno, California in the U.S. at 6pm local time on Sunday night.

It is understood that gunmen entered the back yard of a private residence and used a side entrance to get into the party before opening fire.

There were a number of children present at the party, although, none were injured.

There were between 35 and 40 family and friends watching a football game at the party, according to NBC News.

Three men died at the scene and a fourth died in hospital, all of the 10 men that were shot were Asian and between the ages of 25 and 30.

Police said there are five other victims in hospital who are all expected to survive.

‘It is very likely that it was targeted — we just don’t know why,’ said Lt. Bill Dooley during.

‘Somebody picked that house and came up and shot several times on the backside of it. It looks like there was a target,’ the Lt. Dooley said.

‘What we do know is that this was a gathering, a family gathering in the backyard,’ Dooley continued.

‘Everyone was watching football this evening when unknown suspects approached the residence, snuck into the backyard and opened fire’, Lt. Bill Dooley said.

There have been no suspect or suspects identified, according to early reports.

‘Thank God no kids were hurt,’  said Deputy Police Chief, Michael Reid. ‘Luckily, most of the women and children were inside,

‘We’ll do everything we can to find out who the perpetrators are and bring them to justice.’

The police declined to comment on the type of weapon that was used in the shooting.
