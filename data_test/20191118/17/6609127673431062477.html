DEEP FREEZE

Scottish weather: Last night coldest of year so far as -8.6C recorded in Highlands with temps to plummet again tonight

18 Nov 2019, 17:30 by Ewan Mowat

SCOTLAND suffered the coldest night of autumn - and it will be even COLDER overnight.

Temperatures plummeted to -8.6C in the Highlands and there will be another deep freeze tonight with subzero temperatures across the country.

Mercury dropped to its lowest at Tulloch Bridge in Inverness-shire.

And tonight could see temperatures fall even lower in the area, with -9C predicted in the north of Scotland by the Met Office.

The central belt is also expected to freeze with subzero conditions overnight.

There is some good news though, as temperatures are expected to rise slightly through the week.

On Wednesday night, mainland Scotland is predicted to have chills of between -2C and -4C, while the Hebrides and Orkney will see temperatures of 3C and 4C, respectively.

And while the north of the country will still be -1C overnight on Thursday, mercury will have risen to 0C in the central belt.

The cold snap comes as part of an 'Arctic swell' which has brought the earliest winter snow for 11 years in some places.

Britain will be colder than Helsinki this week as the 400-mile wide cold front hits the UK.

We told last week how heavy snowfall had already hit at popular Scottish ski resorts.

Images shared online showed snowy slopes in the Cairngorms and Glenshee resorts.

The coldest night of the winter so far was recorded on November 8 as the mercury dropped to a bone-chilling -7C in Braemar, Aberdeenshire.

Experts predict it will be the coldest November of the decade.
