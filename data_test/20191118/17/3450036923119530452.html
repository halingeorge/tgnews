Heartland woman receives Lifetime Environmental Education Service Award

18 Nov 2019, 17:08 by Matt Gunn

KIRKSVILLE, Mo. â€” Karen Armstrong has always seemed to have a beat on anything environmental science-related.

Armstrong is currently the Missouri Department of Conservation (MDC) Environmental Educator a position that she has served in for the past nineteen years.

Prior to working for the MDC, Armstrong was an environmental science teacher at Kirksville High School for 7 years.

Armstrong has had an interest in environmental science ever since her time as a student-teacher at Fairfield, Iowa middle school.

As a student teacher at Fairfield middle school, Armstrong developed a curriculum for the Environmental Science class that she taught at Kirksville High School.

Even today at her job at the MDC, Armstrong continues to demonstrate contributions to environmental education by helping children connect with nature and help them develop a healthy respect for the world they live in.

Armstrong has done a lot for the environmental science field which is why she was chosen for the Outstanding Lifetime Environmental Education Service Award.

KTVO spoke with Armstrong Monday to get her thoughts on how she feels about receiving this award.

"I've been involved with the Missouri Environmental Education Association for almost since its inception and as when I was a teacher at the high school here in Kirksville. And then when I started working for the Department of Conservation I appreciated the opportunity to be able to share nature and the environment with children and so this is a culmination for me I would say," Armstrong said.

Everyone at KTVO would like to send their sincere congratulations for to Karen Armstrong receiving this much-earned award.
