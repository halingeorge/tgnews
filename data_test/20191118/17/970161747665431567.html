Ex-Corrie star Lindsay on why working class women can feel 'invisible'

18 Nov 2019, 17:05

Sally Lindsay has spoken out about working class women being ‘ignored’ by society.

The actress stars in new thriller Cold Call about as carer June who seeks revenge after her mother is scammed out of a large amount of money.

Sally, 46, feels her ‘she-wolf’ character’s actions in the drama are justified as working class older women are so often overlooked.

‘What happens to her mum spurs her on to feel so bereft and so lost and awful and guilty, so she feels she has to correct it,’ she told Metro.co.uk.

‘She feels she has to make this right. She needs some sort of revenge and retribution.

‘It is a very twisty and turny thriller so it’s quite a difficult thing to explain, but I found all of her reasons justifiable. Especially as a mother and as a woman and as a working class woman, and as a working class woman of a certain age.

‘Not so much me but you do sometimes feel invisible. If you’re stood in a shop or you’re at a bar, you’re last person to be served because you’re a middle-aged woman. And I think that’s what happens with June and they completely underestimate her because she’s invisible.’

The former Coronation Street star hopes that the show will be educational as well as entertaining, so viewers learn how they can protect themselves from falling victim to fraud.

‘If you have a phone and a bank account they can get you. I got emails from Natwest bank saying you’re overdrawn or this or that. I don’t bank with Natwest so obviously I know it’s not a genuine email. If I did bank with them I would be horrified and try and get back to them because you want everything to be right.

‘I can’t even stand getting a parking ticket! I’m crying me eyes out and there’s this bloke knocking on the window. I absolutely could afford it but I’m like, “no it’s wrong. I didn’t know you couldn’t park here.” I was heartbroken.

‘If that’s how you’ve been brought up, it’s completely alien when people tell you well you’re gonna get this, they might take all your money out and it’s fraud. It’s about fear.’

Cold Call starts tonight at 9pm on Channel 5.
