TIME TO SHINE

New Hibs boss Jack Ross wants to give Easter Road fans a team to be proud of again

18 Nov 2019, 17:31 by Aidan Smith

JACK ROSS wants to build a Hibs team that the fans can be proud of again.

The new Easter Road boss was officially unveiled as new Hibees gaffer today and he is targeting being at the club for many years to come.

The 43-year-old has signed a three-and-a-half year-deal at the Leith club and he's hoping to deliver success to the green half of Edinburgh in that time.

Speaking to Sky Sports, Ross said: "Part of the attraction for me in the early discussions we had was the want for me to be here for a number of years.

"I’m not here to use it as a stepping stone or a platform, I want to be a successful Hibs manager and I want to be here for the long term.

"I suppose my other answer for that is I went to a club where there was a high frequency in changes of managers and I think I was the longest serving since Steve Bruce so I did okay in that respect.

"I could have been there for longer but certainly the ambition here is to be here for a long period of time.

"You have to win football matches as a manager and as a club. Supporters want that but equally there are demands at certain clubs as well for how you do it.

"It's not like I'm not familiar with this club I have been here often enough as an opposition player, coach and manager.

"I've seen some really good sides so it's up to me to make sure I can provide a side that can satisfy the demands of the supporters as well."

Ross replaced the axed Paul Heckingbottom at Hibs, but he did have offers from other clubs.

He could have made the choice to be based across the city when Hearts came calling.

Ross added: "From the initial conversations with Leeann (Dempster), Graeme (Mathie) and Ron (Gordon), I got a good feel from them and I think that's important.

"We then progressed to the more formal discussions in terms of what I could offer the club.

"There's a lot of good things in place at this club whether it be the training ground or stadium.

"There's a lot of good things behind the scenes that people don't see but are very good and very positive but I feel it is a good fit.

"Ultimately the proof will be in the pudding in terms of what I can produce on the pitch but it felt very natural from very early on."
