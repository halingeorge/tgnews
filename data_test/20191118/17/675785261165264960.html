Hero bodyguard throws himself in front of client after motorbike hitman opens fire

The bodyguard has been praised for his quick-thinking when an attacker dressed as a pizza courier tried to shoot his client at a hotel in Mexico City.

18 Nov 2019, 17:14 by Lila Randall

A quick-thinking bodyguard was captured on CCTV protecting his female client from an attacker posing as a pizza delivery courier.

The attempted attack took place at around 2pm in the entrance of hotel Santa Fe in Mexico’s central capital Mexico City.

In the footage the bodyguard can be seen shadowing the woman as she walks around a black SUV and unloads her bags.

A man on a motorbike cruises into the hotel grounds and pulls up close behind the SUV when the bodyguard - already on high alert - places himself between the woman and the attacker.

The attacker quickly moves to get closer to the woman and pulls something out of his pocket, prompting the woman to run inside and the bodyguard to draw his gun.

 

The pair both fire shots but the courier is forced to turn on his heels and run when the bodyguard fires rounds at him.

Netizen ‘Edgaraponte’ said: “Excellent bodyguard, his training is very good. He is alert, he protects his client with his body, pulls out the gun and shoots with both hands.”

‘Santo99082430’ commented: “Crime is continually rising in our beloved Mexico. None of our leaders seem interested in solving the issue.”

Other social media users praised the bodyguard.

One person commented:  “As soon as the bodyguard saw the motorbike pull in, he already knows what is happening and does not lose sight of it.”

Another raised concerns over the gun crime in Mexico and impact it has on children in schools.

Though the motivation of the attack is unknown, Mexico City has a bloody history.

Drug-related violence in Mexico has increased in recent years, while many fatalities are suspected gang members slaughtered in turf wars.

The country’s War on Drugs, declared in 2006 by the then president Felipe Calderon, saw around 250 thousand murders at the end of the presidency of Enrique Peña Nieto in 2018, according to local media, Infobae.
