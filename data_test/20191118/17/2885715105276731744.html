Millionaire buys UK football team, signs 15 new players and makes himself manager

For his first game as manager, Tamplin - a steel businessman - promised supporters free entry and a drink. However, his first outing against Coggeshall Town didn't go as expected for the new manager as his side lost the contest 3-2.

18 Nov 2019, 17:23 by Sports Desk

Months after parting ways from football club Billericay Town, a millionaire returned to England’s non-league football for a new stint.

The 47-year-old Glenn Tamplin now owns Romford FC, and after taking over the club he appointed himself as the manager by dismissing the long-serving Paul Martin.

In addition, he recruited 15 new players for the squad, promising to lift the team from the bottom of the league.

“I could’ve got involved at Macclesfield or Welling but I like a challenge – I had to go somewhere with potential, that was lower than where I went before because I need to do it bigger than I’ve done before,” Tamplin told the BBC while explaining his decision to buy the club.
