'Catastrophic' hail storm hits Australia with stones 'the size of cricket balls

18 Nov 2019, 17:26

A hailstorm in Australia yesterday is estimated to have cost £21 million of damage.

The ‘catastrophic’ storm saw hailstorms ‘the size of cricket balls’ pound the Sunshine Coast and Brisbane, leaving 20,000 homes without power.

Thousands of cars were left with smashed windscreens, while windows of people’s homes were also affected.

The Insurance Council of Australia declared the storm a catastrophe, saying they had received 5,000 claims in relation to it including from 3,000 vehicles.

One company alone was working on 200 cars wrecked in the storm.

Mike Clayton from Clayton’s Towing Service said: ‘It was just total mayhem out there. When it hit, it hit hard and the cars were just destroyed so it was all hands on deck to get them off the highway.’
