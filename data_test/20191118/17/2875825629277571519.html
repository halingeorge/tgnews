Four Liverpool stars make Alan Shearer's team of the season so far as Man Utd miss out

Alan Shearer has selected four players from Liverpool, three from Leicester City, two from Sheffield United and one from Man City. No Man United nor Arsenal player makes the cut

18 Nov 2019, 17:20 by Irishmirror.ie

Liverpool are well-represented in Alan Shearer's Premier League team of the season so far with no fewer than four players being named.

Things aren't so rosy for Manchester United and Arsenal though, with not one player from either club making the cut.

Leicester City are the second best-represented club in the XI, with three players being including in Shearer's team.

While Manchester City have just one player representing the champions.

In goal Shearer has selected Dean Henderson as the man who's been most impressive between the sticks so far this season.

The Sheffield United keeper, on loan from Manchester United, has upstaged the man ahead of him at Old Trafford, David de Gea, according to Shearer's selection.

The Blades have been punching well above their weight this year and sit fifth in the Premier League - above both Arsenal and Man United - and have conceded just nine goals, bettered only by the Foxes.

Liverpool's Trent Alexander-Arnold and Andy Robertson patrol the flanks in Shearer's team, while another Blades star in the form of John Egan stands at centre-back.

Joining him in defence is the man who has replaced Harry Maguire at the King Power Stadium, Caglar Soyuncu - another blow for United.

Moving into midfield and Shearer has selected Liverpool's "lighthouse" Fabinho.

In front of him stands James Maddison, Sadio Mane, and the only Man City player selected in the XI, Kevin de Bruyne.

Finally, up top, Shearer has named two strikers: Jamie Vardy and Tammy Abraham.

Things looked like they were slowing down for Vardy last season now that he is in the twilight years of his career, but he's enjoying a new lease of life under Brendan Rodgers.

Vardy has netted 11 times so far this season, and, as of right now, is the Premier League's most lethal marksman.

Hot on his heels is the new kid on the block, Chelsea forward Abraham, who has taken the top fight by storm this campaign.

Abraham has 10 goals to his name and given that he's brimming with confidence, is showing absolutely no signs of letting up.
