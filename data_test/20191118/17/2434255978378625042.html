All Lidl workers in Ireland set for pay rise to meet Living Wage of €12.30 per hour

Good news for workers across Ireland

18 Nov 2019, 17:05 by Blathin de Paor

Lidl Ireland have confirmed they are set to give a pay increase to around 800 Lidl employees.

The retailer revealed on Monday that they are commited to matching the latest Living wage in Ireland which now stands at €12.30 per hour.

Therefore anyone working for the company who does not currently earn this, will be in line for a wage boost.

It is expected to benefit around 15% their employees.

Confirming the news Maeve Mc Cleane, HR Director of Lidl Ireland said: “We are delighted to be the first major nationwide food retailer to commit to the latest Living Wage in Ireland.

"Already, 85% of our team earn in excess of the Living Wage, however it is important for us to ensure that those whose hourly pay is below €12.30 benefit from an increase.

"This would bring them to a level that is more reflective of enjoying a better standard of living.”

 

The Living Wage is an independently assessed and recommended rate of income considered necessary to have a socially acceptable standard of living. Lidl Ireland currently employs over 4,300 employees in its 162 stores across the country.

The minimum wage in Ireland currently sits at €9.80 an hour but workers under the age of 18 can be paid even less.
