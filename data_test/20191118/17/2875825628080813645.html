Arsenal midfielder Lucas Torreira "no longer at ease" under Unai Emery

Lucas Torreira has started just four Premier League games under Unai Emery this term and his long-term future is far from certain

18 Nov 2019, 17:25 by Irishmirror.ie

Lucas Torreira's agent has declared that the Arsenal midfielder is "no longer at ease" under Unai Emery, putting his long-term Emirates future into doubt.

The Uruguay international was signed from Sampdoria in a £26million deal in 2018, and was seen as something of a coup after impressing at the World Cup.

But after being a regular last term, he hasn’t been first choice for Emery so far in 2019-20.

Torreira, 23, has often seen Matteo Guendouzi, Dani Ceballos and Joe Willock preferred by the Spaniard, as well as former skipper Granit Xhaka.

Torreira is yet to complete 90 minutes in the Premier League, and has started only four times - albeit rarely in the deep-lying midfield role he prefers.

As such, once again, speculation over his future has flared - having been targeted by AC Milan during the summer.

Torreira’s ex-Sampdoria boss, Marco Giampaolo, was interested in taking him to San Siro, but Arsenal had no desire to sell.

Giampaolo has since been dismissed by the Rossoneri, but now Torreira’s representative, Pablo Bentancur, has stated that a number of clubs are monitoring his situation in north London.

And he says Torreira isn’t currently pleased with his situation under Emery.

“I must say that the change of role has penalised him sharply,” Bentancur told Calciomercato.

"He is no longer at ease and we hope that things can change.”

However, with Xhaka looking likely to depart in January after his fall out with fans, Torriera won’t be departing mid-season.

That is a situation Bentancur understands and he added: "We have no problem with Arsenal but only words of thanks. Arsenal has allowed Lucas to compete in the Premier League, he has always shown great confidence.

"Clearly the interest for Torreira are all right, especially by Spanish clubs, but I have never sat down with any club to talk about his transfer. This is because of the great respect we have for the Gunners and general manager Raul Sanllehi.

"I don't want to name the teams out of respect, but I can tell you that Atletico [Madrid] tried to take Lucas from Sampdoria two years ago.

"There was an interest [from AC Milan] as well as other clubs [this summer]. But we got together with the Gunners' general manager, Raul Sanllehi, and he immediately told us that for Arsenal, Torreira was not for sale - an adamant position.

"I also have to say that Milan did not present a real concrete offer."

Nevertheless, he also admitted that Torreira continues to follow Serie A, and won't rule out a return in future.

"Lucas follows Italian football with great interest. I can't rule out anything.

"In Italy he grew up as a player and as a man. He brings to heart his experiences at Pescara and Sampdora."
