Wales suffer fresh injury blow with Tomas Francis set to miss the Six Nations

The Wales star suffered a nasty shoulder injury in the Rugby World Cup semi-final loss to South Africa

18 Nov 2019, 17:39 by Simon Thomas

New Wales coach Wayne Pivac has received more bad news with Tomas Francis the latest star name to be ruled out of the Six Nations.

Exeter tighthead Francis will be sidelined for up to four months due to surgery on the shoulder he damaged during the World Cup semi-final defeat to South Africa in Yokohama.

He was hurt while attempting to tackle Springboks No 8 Duane Vermeulen, taking a heavy blow to his right shoulder which forced him to leave the field.

Now the full extent of the injury has been revealed by the Chiefs, with the 48-cap front rower having to go under the knife.

Director of rugby, Rob Baxter, said: “After two or three scans it became clear that Tom needs an operation to repair the shoulder injury he picked up with Wales at the World Cup.

“It’s not great news for us, but at least we know where we stand now.”

This means the 27-year-old Francis will be out until late March, effectively ruling him out of any involvement in the Six Nations.

He joins Jonathan Davies (knee), Rhys Patchell (shoulder) and Gareth Anscombe (knee) in that crocked category.

It leaves Pivac with a squad vacancy to fill in the tight-head department as he looks ahead to the Championship.

Dillon Lewis, of Cardiff Blues, will now be the favourite to wear the No 3 jersey, having had a fine World Cup.

He played in all seven matches out in Japan and showed up well, both in the loose and with his improving scrummaging.

As for other tight-head options, the 41-cap Samson Lee provides plenty of experience, while the Dragons’ Leon Brown is a developing talent.

Pivac could also look at the uncapped WillGriff John, who has been a consistent performer for Sale over the past couple of years.

The former Cardiff Blues and Doncaster prop, who also had a spell playing out in New Zealand, is a real physical presence at 6ft 3ins and 20st 7lbs.
