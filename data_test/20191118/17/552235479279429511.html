Voice star Craig Forsyth lined up for Ayrshire Christmas lights show that will rival Glasgow

Organiser Sylvia Mallinson is promising a ‘sea of lights’ inspired by the city's Royal Exchange Square.

18 Nov 2019, 17:00 by Toni Lennox, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/toni-lennox/

Irvine is set for a spectacular £25k Christmas lights show to rival Glasgow’s Royal Exchange Square.

Organiser Sylvia Mallinson is promising a ‘sea of lights’ inspired by the city’s famous festive illuminations.

She said: “We’ve really concentrated on the lights this year, I would say it’s a show stopper.

“We didn’t have any lights at all at the beginning of the year because they were broken.

 

“Looking at how far the committee has come in the space of a year is something we’re all really proud of."

"It'll be like the sea of lights over Royal Exchange Square. We'll have that all over The Cross."

This year there will be no celebrity guest, instead the Irvine Special Event Forum have decided community stalwart Jim McHarg will do the honours.

Jim, who is set to retire, worked with the Community Learning Development department for 30 years.

Sylvia said: “Jim has worked in communities across North Ayrshire for three decades and we thought it would be a nice send off for him.”

The Irvine Special Events Forum have secured a fun fair and The Voice singer Craig Forsyth to entertain the crowds.

Organisers have also confirmed there will be a nativity scene on display at Caledonia House – the balcony above Iceland – and a special photo prop for families to capture a magical moment together.

 

Sweet treats and refreshments will be available to purchase on the night.

There are 25 volunteers offering a helping hand on the evening, but anyone wishing to take part are encouraged to reach out to the Irvine Special Events Forum.

Sylvia said: “This is not a council-driven event, this is put on by the community for the community.

“Let’s just spread a bit of love and Christmas cheer. The volunteers who help us give up their own time to try and do something for the town.

“All the time and effort people give, lets try and be festive about it and be happy we have got something going up.”

More acts are encouraged to get in touch to perform on the big night.

Sylvia explained: “If anyone would like to grace our stage, it could be comedy, singing, dancing, magic, a school class, anything at all let us know and we’ll organise a slot for you to come on stage.

“It’s a night for us to show off what great talent we have within the community. The Chritsmas light switch on is a great platform only available for one night.

“All are welcome to perform.”

Sylvia started organising the Christmas lights from 2013 and this will be her final year.

She said: “It’s been a great six years. A big thank you to the committee; Margaret, Ronnie, Annie, David, Carmen, Tracey and Bill. That’s everyone on the committee and this annual evening wouldn’t be possible without them.”

As of 2020 a new committee could be taking over, although it has not been confirmed. Sylvia said: “Our main concentration make sure we had lights. This will be my last year organising the Christmas lights and we are hoping to pass the event on to a new committee. This way if a new committee comes on board they have something to start them off and they wont have to hire anything.”

 

Sylvia added:“We encourage everyone to come along and just have a great night.

“If any local businesses would like to sponsor this event please reach out as we offer unique packages.”

The lights will be erected this weekend ahead of the big night which is set for Sunday, December 1.

The festivities begin at 3.30pm and Jim McHarg will switch on the lights at 6pm.

To put your name forward to volunteer or book a slot to perform on stage, contact the Irvine Special Events Forum on their Facebook page or call Sylvia on 07707744360.
