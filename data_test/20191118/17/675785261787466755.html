Google Stadia adds 10 more games to its launch list

Exciting times for Stadia fans. With the release tomorrow, Google has revealed its launch catalogue of titles.

18 Nov 2019, 17:13 by Matthew Osborn, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/matthew-osborn/

Stadia is Google's new game streaming platform that they say is "set to revolutionise the way gamers access, play and enjoy their favourite games."

Stadia is a cloud base gaming platform boasting up to 4K at 60 Frames Per Second with the Pro subscription.

The base Stadia can run at 1080p at 60FPS. Users will be able to play on any Chrome browser, Pixel 3/3a and 4.

The Stadia controller is the real mastermind of the show, connecting directly to the game running in Google's data centres.

Not only does this allow you to play games with no download, but also switch seamlessly from browser to phone using the controller's smart capabilities to pause the state of your game whilst loading it up on another device.

You can pick up a second controller and choose from three different colours: Just Black, Clearly White or Wasabi. The controllers themselves are comfy to hold in hand and have a good weight to them.

Running dual-band wifi for best connection at home and the low energy Bluetooth for better battery life. You can also quickly capture video and screen captures directly from the controller allowing you to view your clips via the Stadia app.

If you pre-ordered a Google Stadia they are starting to ship from November 19 to all buyers. You can check your order status by going to 'My Orders' on the Google store.

You can order Google Stadia exclusively via the Google store, retailin at £119.00 including delivery.

At launch the Stadia will be available in 14 countries through the Founder’s Edition: US, Canada, UK, Ireland, France, Germany, Italy, Spain, Netherlands, Belgium, Denmark, Sweden, Norway, and Finland.

More regions will be added in 2020.
