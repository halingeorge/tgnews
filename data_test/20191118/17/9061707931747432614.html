Brewers' home game against Southend rearranged for Tuesday, December 3

International call-ups caused it to be called off on Saturday

18 Nov 2019, 17:05 by Colston Crawford

Burton Albion's home League One clash with Southend United will now be played on Tuesday, December 3

The Brewers were originally scheduled to host Southend on Saturday, November 16 but due to three Albion players being called up for international duty, that fixture was postponed.

Nathan Broadhead is in the Wales' under-21s squad, Kieran O'Hara is in the Republic of Ireland squad and Liam Boyce is included in Michael O'Neill's Northern Ireland set-up for their European Championship qualifiers against the Netherlands, which they drew 0-0 on Saturday, and Germany, which is being played on Tuesday night.

The rearrangement means Burton now have two successive home games in a week, with Lincoln City visiting on Saturday, December 7.

The Brewers have already had games against Sunderland and Bolton Wanderers postponed earlier this season because of international call-ups. They will be playing Sunderland away next Tuesday, November 26
