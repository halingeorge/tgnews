Marcus Rashford talks up England's Euro 2020 attack: "It's frightening what we can do"

Raheem Sterling and Harry Kane are must-picks for Gareth Southgate, with Rashford and Jadon Sancho vying to start in the Three Lions' first-choice starting lineup

18 Nov 2019, 23:16 by Andy Dunn, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/andy-dunn/

He would be worth well over £100million on the open market and is in the goalscoring form of his short career.

Only just turned 22 and, arguably, the quickest striker in the Premier League, there is also a sensational, long-range free-kick in his locker.

Yet Marcus Rashford is still not guaranteed a starting place in his national side.

And the Manchester United forward has a word that covers that scenario. Frightening.

While Gareth Southgate continues to scratch around for his best central defensive combination and his most effective midfield formula, he can rest easy when it comes to his attacking options.

At least for now.

It is simple. Perm three from four.

Or – when Raheem is not ion the naughty step – pick either Rashford or Jadon Sancho to complete the trio with Sterling and Harry Kane.

Between them, the four have scored 55 goals for club and country this season – and December is still some way off.

Of the 37 goals England scored in their qualification campaign, this quartet weighed in with 23.

Of the other 14, two were own goals. They are startling numbers.

Rashford said: “It is frightening what we can do as a group of attackers.

“If we go into the summer anywhere near the sort of form we have been in recently then it will be a successful tournament.”

After again making a big impact when replacing Callum Hudson-Odoi against Kosovo on Sunday, Rashford seems to be ahead of Sancho in the pecking order.

Once again, the link-up with Sterling and Kane looked natural.

“I think it is obviously a good combination,” said Rashford. “But we have played games without that particular front-three and done well.

“You have to use that to your advantage and if maybe it isn’t working in one game for that three, someone has to come in and make the difference.

“I have been in two tournaments now and the squad is so important.

“It is good competition – we really do have that kind of environment when we are in the camp together. It is so positive.”

An eighth international goal came courtesy of the sort of crisp, instinctive finish that many thought was beyond Rashford.

His scoring ratio has been under the spotlight for some time amid suggestions he is not naturally lethal in the penalty area.

That is why the first-time hit against the Kosovans gave him as much pleasure as the dead-ball beauty from distance against Chelsea the other week.

He explained: “For me, when I score those type of goals – or even tap-ins – it holds bigger importance because I know those are the goals I need to get more of.

“The spectacular goals come every once in a while, so if you want to become a great goalscorer you have to score all types of goals and that is the next step I am focusing on.”

His focus on that target has been helped by automatic selection for his club, his confidence boosted by the faith of Ole Gunnar Solskjaer.

Rashford said: “It had been a tough period and now I’m sort of getting consistent games and playing week in, week out and, as a forward, that has huge importance, especially with chances.

“If you are not in the team or you are in and out, you don’t quite have that confidence in front of goal.

“But when you are having a run in the team, it is important to come up with goods and once you do start doing that, it becomes second nature.”

It is certainly second nature to Kane, as Rashford explains.

“His finishing is second to none. Playing in and around him gives us a lot of confidence because if there is only one chance, you know that if it falls to him it is more than likely going to be a goal.

"That breeds confidence throughout the team.”

And right now, few look more confident than Rashford.

Combine that with the brilliance of Sterling, the deadliness of Kane and the potential of Sancho and there is only one word for it.

Frightening.
