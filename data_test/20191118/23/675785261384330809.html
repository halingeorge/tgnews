Man City 'draw level financially with Man Utd' thanks to £650m Puma kit deal

The blue half of Manchester had to live in the shadow of their once-more successful rivals for a long time but are now almost on a par financially

18 Nov 2019, 23:27 by Alex Milne

Manchester City appear set to match Manchester United from a financial perspective for the first time in decades.

Until the takeover of the club from the Abu Dhabi United Group in 2008 City were nowhere near their bitter rivals both in terms of on-pitch and off-pitch success.

Since their massive investment, however, City have gone from strength to strength, winning four Premier League titles compared to United's three in that time as well as attracting genuinely world-class talent to the Etihad Stadium.

And according to the Daily Mail , City are set to match United's annual revenue for 2019-2020, with a total of around £560m expected for both.

This is partly due to United's revenue falling by £70m thanks to a lack of Champions League football this season, and partly because of City's eye-watering £650m 10-year deal with kit manufacturing giant Puma.

United's wages are also down by £6.8m year on year, due to them not having to pay Champions League bonuses.

To put their meteoric rise in perspective, City's revenue in the 2007-2008 season was just £104m, while United's was £257m.

United executive vice chairman Ed Woodward recently announced his club's first-quarter figures, and admitted there was much to improve on.

"Our ultimate goal is to win trophies, playing fast, fluid, attacking football with a team that fuses graduates from our academy along with world-class acquisitions," he said.

"We know this will not be achieved overnight. However we have made investments across the club that we believe have set us on the right path."

City have again been outshining United on the pitch so far this season, and are currently nine points clear of them in the Premier League.

The Premier League champions next face a tough-looking game at home to Chelsea on Saturday, while United travel to Sheffield United on Sunday.
