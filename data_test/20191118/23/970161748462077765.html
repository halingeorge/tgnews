Yaya Toure names Man Utd outcast Nemanja Matic as his toughest opponent

18 Nov 2019, 23:35

Yaya Toure has surprisingly named Manchester United midfielder Nemanja Matic as his toughest opponent.

Ivory Coast legend Toure would regularly come up against Matic during his eight-year spell in the Premier League with Manchester City.

Toure, who left English football last year, helped City win three Premier League titles and the FA Cup during his stint at the Etihad.

Serbian midfielder Matic enjoyed two spells at Chelsea while Toure was at City and then joined Manchester rivals United in 2017.

Matic emerged as a key player for former manager Jose Mourinho but has fallen out of favour under Ole Gunnar Solskjaer, although he will hope to revive his career once he returns from injury.

Asked to name the most difficult opponent he came up against during a brilliant career which also included spells at Barcelona and Monaco, Toure said: ‘Nemanja Matic was complicated.

‘He’s not quick but he’s so strong, clever.’

The 36-year-old added to The Times: ‘All the rest of the opponents, because I always looked at them carefully before games, I get their weakness.’

Toure also revealed how his move from Barcelona to Manchester City materialised and how he was determined to help turn the Premier League club into champions.

‘I was on the phone with [chief executive] Garry Cook. He promised me he was going to bring players to help me to do great things at City,’ Toure said.

‘I said, ‘Sign David Silva first, and I will come.’ And Silva was saying, ‘Sign Yaya first and I will come!’ I said to Silva, ‘Sign! I am not going to run off!’

‘He didn’t believe me because I was at Barcelona, one of the top teams in the world. But it was time for change. Kolo told me to come. I have to listen to him.

‘When I come to City, I said, ‘I come here to win and make City one of the greatest teams in the Premier League.’ People were laughing at me in the beginning.

‘I make my team-mates realise what we can become by starting with one trophy, the FA Cup.’

Toure finished his Manchester City career with 79 goals from 316 appearances.
