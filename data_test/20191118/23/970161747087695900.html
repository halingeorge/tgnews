Tottenham identify THREE candidates to replace Mauricio Pochettino

18 Nov 2019, 23:18

Tottenham have identified Eddie Howe, Julian Nagelsmann and Carlo Ancelotti as possible candidates to take over from struggling manager Mauricio Pochettino, according to reports.

Pochettino, rated as one of the best managers in Europe, is currently enduring the most difficult spell of his Spurs career.

Having led Tottenham to four successive top-four finishes in the Premier League, Pochettino now faces a fight to save his job in north London.

Spurs have won just three of their 12 league games this season and sit 14th in the table – below Newcastle, Crystal Palace and Brighton – ahead of Saturday’s trip to London rivals West Ham.

The Daily Mail say Tottenham are privately planning for Pochettino’s departure, with some insiders doubting the Argentine will turn things around.

And it is claimed three managers – Howe, Nagelsmann and Ancelotti – have emerged as figures of interest for Tottenham.

Howe, 41, has helped establish Bournemouth as a mid-table Premier League team over the past three years and is seen as one of the most promising managers in England.

German boss Nagelsmann is also well-respected in Europe, with his RB Leipzig side currently second in the Bundesliga table.

Former Real Madrid, Bayern Munich, PSG and Chelsea manager Ancelotti has won domestic titles in Italy, England, France and Germany and would be a safe pair of hands if Spurs opted to make a change mid-season.

Amid Pochettino’s struggles, former Tottenham manager Harry Redknapp says Spurs would be crazy to sack the Argentine.

He told The Sun: ‘He’s a fantastic manager. You are only as good as your players and they need to pick up their form.

‘It would not even come into the equation for me to get rid of him.’
