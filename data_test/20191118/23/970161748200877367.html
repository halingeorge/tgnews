Paedophile surgeon, 68, 'abused 250 victims over three decades'

18 Nov 2019, 23:21

A surgeon is suspected of sexually assaulting 250 people in what could be France’s biggest paedophile scandal.

Joël Le Scouarnec, 68 is accused of attacking hundreds of children over the course of three decades, sometimes while they were under anaesthetic.

The specialist in abdominal surgery was arrested in 2017 following claims from a girl, six, who alleged that she had been raped.

A search of the surgeon’s home in Jonzac in May 2017 led to the discovery of child pornography images.

Police also found secret diaries in which the surgeon described sex scenes involving scores of other children who were named.

Today it was announced that 250 potential victims had been identified and that 184 people planned to file a complaint, according to a statement from La Rochelle public prosecutor Laurent Zuchowicz.

Of the 184, 181 were minors at the time of the alleged assaults, according to French media.

Mr Zuchowicz said: ‘250 potential victims of acts of paedophilia that do not fall under the statute of limitations were identified during the (second) investigation.’

Police were able to question 209, ‘several of whom had very precise memories’ of the alleged abuse, he added.

The surgeon was convicted of possessing images of child sexual abuse in 2005 but was allowed to continue practising until his arrest in 2017, according to The Sun.

Le Scouarnec’s lawyers said the journal entries were pure ‘fantasies’, but he has admitted to sexually assaulting some child patients in hospitals.

Francesca Satta, a lawyer for some of the former surgeon’s alleged victims, told AFP: ‘They remember perfectly well what they endured but never talked about it because they were afraid.’

He will go on trial in March 2020 in Saintes for alleged rape, as well as sexual assault and indecent exposure.

The medic faces up to 20 years imprisonment if convicted on the first set of charges.
