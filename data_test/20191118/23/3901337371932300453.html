Messi Nets Late To Rescue Argentina

18 Nov 2019, 23:30 by SL International

Lionel Messi netted a late penalty as Argentina drew 2-2 against Uruguay in an international friendly on Monday evening.

Luis Suarez and Edison Cavani spearheaded La Celeste’s front line, whilst the Albiceleste had Sergio Aguero partnering Messi instead of Lautaro Martinez who dropped to the bench.

Injury forced the Uruguayans into a substitute 17 minutes after the kick off, as Giovanni Gonzalez filled in for Sebastian Coates.

Argentina showed loads of promise going forward in hope for the opener, but equally so the Uruguayans threw the gauntlet with Suarez having a shot blocked after 20 minutes.

Amidst the trying start, La Celeste took the lead in the final ten minutes of the half when Cavani connected brilliantly with Suarez to dink beyond Esteban Andrada.

Argentina clawed back within two minutes courtesy of an incisive finish from Paulo Dybala, but the attempt was chalked off after the Juventus star utilised his hand in the build-up.

After quiet start to the second half, the equaliser duly arrived when Aguero headed home from a close-range following a pin-point delivery from partner-in-crime Messi on 63 minutes.

A turnaround didn’t materialise though as Suarez hauled his side back five minutes later with an astounding free-kick from the edge of the area.

The 2019 Copa America bronze medal winners refused to die down and after a few openings around the area, threatened to level matters late on but Uruguay defended strongly. Then a penalty arrived seconds later after Martin Caceres was flagged for handling in the area, prompting Messi to fire home and bring the match to a stalemate in Michigan.
