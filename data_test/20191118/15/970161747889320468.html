Adopted girl, 7, died after nurse mom 'burnt her on stove and fed her bleach'

18 Nov 2019, 15:51

A seven year-old girl died after her adopted mother beat her, starved her, burned her and gave her bleach to drink, police say.

Samayah Ann Emmanuel from Miami in Florida was also made to stand for lengthy periods with her arms outstretched as if she was being crucified.

And her mother Gina Emmanuel, 50, would batter the youngster with a shoe when she grew exhausted and dropped her arms.

Shockingly, Gina, who is free on bail, was still a registered nurse until last week, over a year after her child’s November 2018 death.

An emergency suspension was placed on her registration after it was flagged up to officials by the Miami Herald. 

The alleged killer nurse was finally suspended from her job after the Department of Health cited her ‘systematic, willful and sadistic abuse and neglect of her children.’

She was barred from working after the Herald detailed how Samayah was so badly malnourished that her body had ‘shriveled’ up by the time of her death.

The little girl, taken into foster care after being born to a drug addict monther was covered ‘head to toe’ in bruises, burns and ‘open wounds’, according to a caller to Florida’s state-wide abuse hotline.

She was burnt on the stove by Gina when she took bread to eat, it is claimed, and allegedly given bleach to drink when she complained about being thirsty.

Samayah, her two sisters aged 13 and seven, as well as their five year-old brother, also had socks tied around their eyes by Gina, it is alleged.

She reportedly chained her children to their beds, and warned them to lie to investigators about their injuries, according to official documents.

Gina was reportedly able to get away with the abuse because adopted children are not scrutinized or supervised by social services in the same way as children in foster care.

Samayah was taken out of school, and ‘did not interact with the outside world,’ according to investigators.

Emmanuel was charged with aggravated child abuse, child neglect and physical neglect last month, shortly after she was arrested.

She is due to be arraigned on Monday.
