Mohamed Salah and Andy Robertson injury updates ahead of Crystal Palace vs Liverpool

Liverpool stars Mohamed Salah, Andy Robertson, Joe Gomez, Virgil van Dijk and Jordan Henderson were all taken home early from international duty

18 Nov 2019, 15:56 by Alex Smith

Mohamed Salah and Andy Robertson are doubts for Liverpool's clash with Crystal Palace this weekend.

Winger Salah and left-back Robertson are both undergoing rehabilitation for ankle injuries - which have been closely monitored throughout the international break.

Egyptian Salah has been suffering from his damaged ankle since the Reds' 2-1 victory over Leicester on October 5 - after Foxes midfielder Hamza Choudhury fouled him.

He missed the next fixture against Manchester United and aggravated the injury, after an tackle from Fernandinho, during the victory over Manchester City just before the international break.

Klopp revealed that Salah regularly had his foot drained and before the City game said: “I think it is more really annoying - for him, not me - the fact that the injury always has a bit of fluid in, and he has to deal with it, we get it out and it comes back, and stuff like that."

Salah gave an insight to fans the treatment on his ankle last week when he posted a video of him with a physio and was spotted wearing a protective boot in his homeland.

Scotland international Robertson had a swollen ankle after the City win which forced him to sit out of the international break.

The Liverpool Echo report that like Salah his is a doubt for the fixture against Palace at Selhurst Park on Saturday.

Robertson was left frustrated by his inability to skipper his national side for their Euro 2020 qualifiers with Cyprus and Kazakhstan.

Robertson said: “I don’t care what game it is, I want to play it and my track record in the last five years has shown that," he said. "But I don’t want to pick up more injuries and ruin my season because of it.

"I’ve had to listen to the people around me when previously I would have told them to beat it. The fact my ankle has swollen up again has made it easier to miss these games.

"For the last two and a half weeks I’ve not trained at all for Liverpool . I’ve trained the day before a game, missed a Champions League game other than the last 15 minutes against Genk and missed Arsenal in the League Cup.”

The pair are just two of five Liverpool players who were pulled out of international duty - and could miss out on the Palace game.

Joe Gomez and Jordan Henderson are also all being monitored, while Virgil Van Dijk skipped Holland duty for "personal reasons".
