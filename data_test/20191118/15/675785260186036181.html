Yousef Makki killer bids for day release from jail 'to spend time with his family'

EXCLUSIVE Public schoolboy Joshua Molnar stabbed 17-year-old Yousef Makki in Manchester and is serving a 16-month sentence for possessing a knife and perverting the course of justice

18 Nov 2019, 15:50 by Lorraine King

A teenager who killed Yousef Makki has submitted a bid to be released from jail 'to spend time with his family'.

Joshua Molnar, now 18, killed the 17-year-old in an argument over drugs in Hale Barns, Cheshire, on March 2.

Yousef, a scholarship pupil at Manchester Grammar School was knifed in the heart by pal Molnar.

Molnar, formerly of prestigious £12,000-a-year Cheadle Hume School, was acquitted of the murder and manslaughter of the straight-A student in July, on the grounds of self-defence.

He is currently serving a 16-month sentence and training order at a young offenders' institution after he admitted possessing the knife which inflicted the wound from which Yousef died.

He also pleaded guilty to perverting the course of justice by telling police someone else had caused the injury.

 

Yousef's sister Jade Akoume, 28, told Mirror Online she was visited by a family liasion officer on Friday who told her that Molnar had applied for a day release to "spend time with him family" at the end of the month.

The mum-of-three said: "We are appalled.

"It appears the application has been made so he can spend the day with his family - but it's only months after he's been sentenced.

"Yousef will never ever have the chance to spend time with his family again.

 

"It's another real kick in the teeth as we will never have the chance to have another day with Yousef and they are fully aware of that."

Yousef's family had appealed against Molnar's sentence but a judge ruled it was not unduly lenient.

The family were also left reeling after Monlar's wealthy parents Mark and Stephanie hired Manchester PR agency MC2 Communications to tell their story - a move Yousef's mum Debbie called "shameless".

Stephanie, 51, is the co-founder of a chain of Cheshire nurseries, and 56-year-old Mark, is a company director and business consultant.

Stephanie said her son fully accepts responsibility for Yousef's death.

Jade said: "This feels like another opportunity for the family to show their lack of remorse.

"Mum is not taking this news well at all, it's like every time we try to move on his name comes up again.

"It feels like he is doing this to taunt us."

If Molnar is granted the day release, which could take place by the end of the month, Jade said he is expected to be banned from entering Manchester.

"We've been told he won't be allowed back in the area so at least we don't have to worry about bumping into him.

"But the judge who sentenced him spoke out against his fascination with knives and knives being a cancer on society.

"What kind of message does it send if he can be let out on day release so soon?"

A spokeswoman from the Ministry of Justice said she was unable to comment as the application was being processed.
