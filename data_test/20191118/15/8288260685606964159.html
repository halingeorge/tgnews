Hour by hour weather forecast as Birmingham hit by 'danger to life' freezing fog warning

November 19 will see Birmingham hit by a yellow weather warning for fog as the Met Office warns it poses a "danger to life"

18 Nov 2019, 15:30 by James Rodger, https://www.facebook.com/jamesrodgerjournalist

Freezing fog is set to sweep across Birmingham tomorrow morning as the city faces a teeth-chattering start to Tuesday.

November 19 will see Birmingham hit by a yellow weather warning for fog as the Met Office warns it poses a "danger to life".

Forecasters have issued the warning for freezing fog from 4am onwards on Tuesday.

Meteorologists expect the warning to expire at around 11am - by which point it will have sparked traffic and travel chaos.

"Some dense fog and freezing fog patches on Tuesday morning likely leading to some travel disruption," the Met Office spokesman said.

The worst-affected areas include most of the Midlands, Home Counties and southeast of England.

Temperatures in these regions will struggle to exceed 5C.

 

It comes after wintry showers swept across the UK this morning.

Icy surfaces could remain a hazard tomorrow, forecasters say.
