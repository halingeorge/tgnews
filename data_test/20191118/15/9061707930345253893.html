Burglars sneak into two homes in Swadlincote after doors were left unlocked

The residents were in at the time of the raids

18 Nov 2019, 15:39 by Beth Pridding

Police are urging residents in Swadlincote to keep their homes secure after two properties were broken into on the same night.

On Saturday, November 16, two houses in New Street, Church Gresley, were broken into between midnight and 5am on Saturday morning.

A burglar snuck into the first of the two houses, through an unlocked door, at some point between midnight and 5am and stole a PlayStation console and controllers.

The second house was also entered via an unlocked door. It is not known what, if anything, was stolen, said a police spokesman.

The two burglaries occurred after another break-in in Weston Street, Swadlincote on Friday, November 8.

Police are now investigating the possibility that all three burglaries could be linked.

A spokesman for Derbyshire Police said: "We are urging residents in Swadlincote and the surrounding villages to review their home security following several burglaries.

"In each case, the homeowner was inside when the burglary took place but they did not see the thief.

"We are asking residents to make sure they lock windows and doors before going to bed."

Anyone who has information about the thefts is asked to contact PC Elliot Dicker, by calling 101, quoting reference number 19000613713.
