Man 'running across the A52' detained after police called to burglary

Police dogs were deployed

18 Nov 2019, 15:20 by Jamie Barlow

A police helicopter and force dogs were deployed on Sunday evening (November 17) after a burglary.

Detectives were called to reports of a house burglary in Ruddington Lane, Wilford, at about 7.45pm that day.

A Nottinghamshire Police spokesman said: "Officers saw a man flee over the garden fence when they arrived.

"The police helicopter later identified a man running across the A52, who was detained by officers."

A 28-year-old man has been arrested in connection with the incident.

The arrested man is also being questioned in connection with a number of other burglaries in Nottinghamshire over the past month.

Detective Inspector Kayne Rukas, of Nottinghamshire Police's burglary team, said: "Burglaries have a big impact on families and the thought of a stranger coming into their homes can be extremely distressing.

"The force recognises the seriousness of burglary, which is why we have dedicated burglary teams covering the city and county.

"This arrest follows a swift response and a coordinated action between officers on the ground, the dog section and the police helicopter."
