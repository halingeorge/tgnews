Care home 'needs deep clean and has bad smells', says damning report

There was 'unexplained bruising' on one resident and 'heavy staining' in the bathroom

18 Nov 2019, 15:34 by Conor Gogarty

Inspectors have found a family-run Brislington care home needs a “deep clean” and ordered it to address bad smells.

The Care Quality Commission (CQC) has published a report which rates Robleaze House, on Bath Road, ‘requires improvement’ following a September inspection.

The watchdog was concerned by “unexplained bruising” on one resident, as well as missing plaster in a bedroom, “heavy staining” in the bathroom and “malodours” in parts of the home.

It also revealed the inspection was prompted by a police investigation into a safeguarding incident.

“This was not substantiated but we needed to be assured people were safe,” the report reads.

The CQC found Robleaze to be in breach of six regulations, rating it ‘requires improvement’ in its safety, effectiveness, responsiveness and leadership, despite a ‘good’ level of caring.

Incidents not reported

Robleaze, which previously had an overall ‘good’ rating, cares for 10 people with a learning disability, four of whom have lived there since it opened 26 years ago.

The report says: “During the inspection we saw there had been two incidents in August 2019 where a person had kicked two other people in the home.

“Whilst these did not result in serious injury, these had not been reported to Bristol City Council's safeguarding team or to the Care Quality Commission.

“They have a legal responsibility to report these to the Care Quality Commission and the local authority's safeguarding team.

“There has also been some unexplained bruising to one person these had not been reported to safeguarding.”

'Not properly maintained'

The inspectors said parts of the home required a “deep clean” and also highlighted missing tiles in the “dated” shower room.

“Another bathroom had heavy staining on the floor, the grout and sealant needed to be replaced around the bath,” they added.

“There were cleaning schedules in place, but these had not been completed for the month of September 2019.

“One bedroom had a water mark on the roof. We were told this had occurred 18 months ago.

“This had been noted at the last inspection in April 2018. The premises were not properly maintained.”

The CQC deemed people in the home to be at risk from a resident who was “known to tip furniture”.

Despite this, there was no mention in this person's care plan about how staff should support them.

'No temperature checks'

“There were no temperature checks completed to ensure medication was stored at the correct temperature and safe for use," the report continues.

“One person had a medical condition which meant they needed supervision when bathing.

“Recommendations had been made by health and social care professionals, but these had not been incorporated into the care plan to keep them safe."

The inspectors also found there was no formal process to record staff inductions or show competence in giving medicine.

Four staff members had received no formal supervision in the previous 10 months.

The report also says: “People and their relatives spoke positively about the care and support provided by the staff and the registered manager.”

Robleaze has been approached for comment.
