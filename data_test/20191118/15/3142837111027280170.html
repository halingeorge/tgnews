CEMIG (NYSE:CIG) Coverage Initiated by Analysts at Goldman Sachs Group

18 Nov 2019, 15:20 by Teresa Graham

Goldman Sachs Group started coverage on shares of CEMIG (NYSE:CIG) in a report published on Thursday, The Fly reports. The firm issued a buy rating on the utilities provider’s stock.

Separately, ValuEngine upgraded shares of CEMIG from a sell rating to a hold rating in a report on Wednesday, November 6th. One equities research analyst has rated the stock with a sell rating, two have issued a hold rating and three have assigned a buy rating to the company’s stock. The stock currently has a consensus rating of Hold.

NYSE:CIG traded down $0.02 during midday trading on Thursday, reaching $2.95. 122,477 shares of the stock traded hands, compared to its average volume of 3,342,224. The company has a fifty day moving average of $3.28 and a 200 day moving average of $3.54. CEMIG has a 12 month low of $2.95 and a 12 month high of $4.11. The company has a current ratio of 1.13, a quick ratio of 1.13 and a debt-to-equity ratio of 0.59.

CEMIG Company Profile

Companhia Energética de Minas Gerais, through its subsidiaries, engages in the generation, transmission, distribution, and sale of electricity in Brazil. The company generates electricity through renewable energy sources, such as water, wind, sun, and biomass; or non-renewable sources, including fossil and nuclear fuels.
