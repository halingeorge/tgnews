Championship's newest manager recalls joining Nottingham Forest and then quickly regretting the move

The ex-Forest striker has taken over the reigns at Cardiff City following Neil Warnock's departure

18 Nov 2019, 15:00 by Barry Cooper

Neil Harris admits his decision to join Nottingham Forest in 2004 was a mistake.

Harris, who has just been appointed as the successor to Neil Warnock at Cardiff City, moved to Forest after cutting short a loan spell with the Bluebirds.

That move didn’t go down too well in south Wales, nor did it live long in the memory on the banks of the Trent, and almost 16 years on from that transfer to the City Ground, he says it was one he regrets.

"I made a mistake not staying here longer," Harris said, having spent three years at Forest, playing a little over 30 times.

"I mean that in total respect to Nottingham Forest, but at the time I made a family decision for various reasons.

"It's probably one of the only things I've really regretted in my footballing career so far.

"But I'm certainly proud and privileged to be sitting here. I have to win people over and I am prepared to do that."

The 42-year-old departed Millwall last month, before being confirmed as boss at the Cardiff City Stadium.

He’ll bring his new team to Nottingham to face Sabri Lamouchi’s Forest on November 30, and says he won’t look to become another version of Warnock.

"I think people are referring to the club I was at previously and the way we played," said Harris, who revealed Warnock had sent him a text congratulating him on his appointment.

"I was at Millwall who demand getting the ball forward quickly and have physical attributes to the game. Some of those principles I will stick to, but I want to adjust because of how I am as a coach.

"I don't think there is a Neil Warnock Mark II. He's his own man and I'm respectful towards that.

"I also have to be respectful of the players I've got and we can't change overnight.

"There has to be a process to that and it takes time. It is not going to be revolution overnight."
