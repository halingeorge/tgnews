Arsenal fans fume at ex-Leeds star for what he did to Nicolas Pepe during Ivory Coast match

The Arsenal winger played in the Ivory Coast's win over Niger

18 Nov 2019, 15:15 by Andy Ha

Nicolas Pepe put his Arsenal struggles to one side to help Ivory Coast beat Niger in their Africa Cup of Nations qualifier on Sunday.

The Elephants were 1-0 winners in Abidjan thanks to a second half penalty from AC Milan midfielder Franck Kessie.

The spot-kick was the second of the game for the home side, after former Leeds United and Bournemouth winger Max Gradel missed a penalty in the first half.

Pepe won the penalty and asked to take it, but Gradel won the argument with his teammate. The 31-year-old then saw his effort saved by Niger goalkeeper Kassaly Daouda.

Gradel saw his blushes spared with Kessie's eventual winner, but some Arsenal fans took to social media to voice their anger that Pepe wasn't rewarded for winning the penalty.

One wrote: 'Who made Max Gradel captain of a team and allowed him to take a pen over Pepe deadball King? Disgrace.'
