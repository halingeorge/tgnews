Stewartry police warn online daters to be wary of scammers

Officers have received reports of a dating app version of a racket known as the Spanish inheritance scam

18 Nov 2019, 15:44 by Stuart Gillespie, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/stuart-gillespie/

People looking for love online are being warned to be wary of scammers.

Stewartry police have received reports of a dating app version of a notorious racket known as the Spanish inheritance scam.

It involves messages being sent through seemingly genuine accounts on online dating websites asking for help with legal matters.

Sergeant Colin Scott, who is based in Castle Douglas, said: “The victim will receive messages over a period of a month or so then receive a message saying they need help getting access to an inheritance in court.

 

“They will never ever meet, there will always be a reason why they cannot meet.”

Sergeant Scott said this was the first incident of the scam he was aware of through dating websites but had recently dealt with a more traditional version of the fraud when a local resident handed in a letter supposedly received from a Spanish solicitor.

The message said a rich person of British nationality with the same surname as the recipient has died in Spain with no known living relatives.

The “solicitor” then invites the intended victim to act as heir to the deceased’s estate in return for a 50 per cent share of a multi-million dollar fortune.

 

Sergeant Scott said: “The fraudsters say if you pretend to be the living relative they can do all the paperwork.

“You get half the inheritance and they get the other half.

“They ask you to send a small deposit that will release the funds.

“Then the cash demands escalate from there.

“It is, of course, a complete scam.”
