'S***** little dance' - Aston Villa hero reveals explosive row with manager

Aston Villa news | Richard Dunne spent four seasons with AVFC but endured a poor relationship with the former Liverpool boss

18 Nov 2019, 15:11 by Ben Husband, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/ben-husband/

Richard Dunne has revealed he 'never liked' Gerard Houllier and it led to an expletive filled row with the Frenchman whilst the pair were at Aston Villa.

Houllier arrived at B6 in September 2010, only one year removed from the controversial play-off between France and the Republic of Ireland.

Ireland's World Cup dreams were ended against the French, when Thierry Henry's handball led to a decisive goal in Paris.

And Dunne has now claimed that there were still seriously ill-feelings when the former Liverpool boss rocked up at Bodymoor Heath.

Speaking to the Irish Independent, Dunne said: “Do you know what I remember afterwards is Gerard Houllier skipping past our bus and doing a s***** little dance.

“I remember that. And obviously he is French, but he was part of UEFA at the time and he was happy. “

Asked if that animosity was still present during their spells at Villa , the central defender made no bones about his feelings for his former employer.

“I fell out with him a few times," he explained. "I never liked him. I never liked him since then because of that and he is the one person that I never liked because of it.

“He is the one person who sort of tried to rub it in our face.

 

“I got into trouble at Aston Villa and he called us for a meeting, me and James Collins, and he called us into the meeting and before we went in James Collins said: ‘Whatever happens, don’t react to him'.

“I was like: ‘Alright, grand, grand, grand’.

“He started talking and he said: ‘I don’t know if you don’t like me because I am French or because of Thierry Henry’s handball’.

 

“And before I could even do anything, James Collins was over the table going: ‘Shut up you f****** a*******!’

“Even for him, it was still in his head.”

Dunne endured a tumultuous time during Houllier's stewardship and was at one point, along with Collins fined for an altercation on a team bonding exercise.
