'Boy, 5, ate dad's cocaine then told teacher it made him feel like Spiderman'

18 Nov 2019, 13:25

A five year-old boy ate his father’s cocaine – then told his teacher that being high on the drug made him feel like Spiderman, police say.

The unidentified youngster brought a baggie of the illegal drug to Lawrence Elementary School in Holyoke, Massachusetts, last Thursday.

It was stamped with pictures of Spiderman, with the boy telling his teacher that when he took the drug it made him feel like the superhero.

The boy’s teacher told the school principal, who called an ambulance and the police.

He was taken to hospital as a precaution, although the bag was sealed, and police do not believe he took any of its contents.

It is unclear whether the youngster may previously have taken cocaine from other bags, NBC News reported.

His dad, Benny Garcia, was found asleep at his home by police. There were 38 bags of cocaine and 70 bags of heroin in the house, it is alleged.

Garcia was being sought by police on separate drugs charges at the time of the incident with his son, and now faces further counts of the same offense.

The boy and his baby sibling both lived with Garcia and their mom, but have now been taken into social services custody.

Garcia is in jail ahead of a court hearing Wednesday to decide whether he should be freed on bail.
