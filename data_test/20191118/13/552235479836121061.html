Body pulled from river in search for missing Scot Calum Mackenzie

The 41-year-old went missing from his home in Salvesen Crescent in Alness sparking a search by emergency services across the area.

18 Nov 2019, 13:59 by Douglas Barrie, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/douglas-barrie/

A body has been pulled from a river in the Highlands during a search for a missing man.

Calum Mackenzie went missing from his home in Salvesen Crescent in Alness , Ross and Cromarty , on Thursday November 7.

The 41-year-old was last seen visiting Morrisons supermarket in Dalmore Road shortly before 1.30pm.

He bought food before leaving the shop on foot.

Local Coastguard, RNLI and mountain rescue teams were helping the Police Scotland search effort and a body was pulled from River Averon in the town on Friday afternoon.

Mr Mackenzie's family has been informed but the body has yet to be formally identified.

A police spokeswoman said: "Around 4.30pm on Friday November 15 2019, the body of a man was recovered from the River Averon in Alness close to Braeface Park .

"Although no formal identification has taken place, the family of Calum Mackenzie, who has been missing from Lanes for over a week, has been made aware.

"Inquiries are continuing."
