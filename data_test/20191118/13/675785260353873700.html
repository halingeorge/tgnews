Bayern Munich chiefs travel to England amid Pep Guardiola and Leroy Sane speculation

Two of Bayern Munich's hierarchy were seen in England as doubts over the futures of Pep Guardiola and Leroy Sane at Man City persist

18 Nov 2019, 13:59 by Sam Meade

Two of Bayern Munich's top dogs travelled to England last week as speculation over the futures of Pep Guardiola and Leroy Sane mounts.

The German champions are without a manager and are in the process of reinvigorating their squad.

Hasan Salihamidžić, their Head of Sports, and chief scout Marco Neppe both headed to England on Wednesday - three days after Manchester City 's loss at Liverpool - but Bayern refused to comment on the motive of their visit when questioned by German journalist Christian Falk.

Reports linking Guardiola with a return to Bayern have gathered momentum in recent weeks despite the Catalan having another 18 months on his City contract.

He has won successive Premier League titles but faces a tough task to make that a hat-trick with Liverpool having a commanding lead at the top.

Guardiola has also cut a frustrated figure since the start of the season with gripes over constant VAR injustices getting the better of him.

But he retains a property in Bavaria having led Bayern for three years and remains highly thought of by those at the Allianz Arena.

Another reason for the Bundesliga champions to visit the British Isles is their very public pursuit of Leroy Sane.

The winger, who is yet to play a game this year after getting injured in the Community Shield, has refused to be drawn on speculation linking him with a return to his homeland.

Sane was not guaranteed regular football last season such is City's depth of talent and he has been backed to accept the mantle left by Arjen Robben and Franck Ribery.

The English side though are hoping the 23-year-old, who joined from Schalke three years ago, will extend his stay at the Etihad.

But were he to leave City they have been linked with Kingsley Coman who joined Bayern while Guardiola was in charge.

Several variables exist in the deal were it to materialise, but Guardiola's presence at City may be one of them.

Bayern and City return to action on Saturday with both sides looking to overhaul points deficits to their respective league leaders.
