These Seattle neighborhoods have the steepest rent increases

18 Nov 2019, 13:48 by KOMO News Staff

SEATTLE - Rent prices are shooting up all over the Seattle area, but a new survey has found that the increases are steeper in some neighborhoods - reaching 25 percent or more over the same time last year.

"With industry giants like Amazon and Microsoft headquartered in this city, it’s no stretch to understand why Seattle is a trendy place to live — and why there’s been an uptick in rent prices," says the new survey by Rent.com.

According to the study's authors, five neighborhoods stood out for their dizzyingly high rent increases:

1. Greenwood, with a year-over-year rent price increase of 25.57 percent, had the steepest rise in rent costs. The average rent for a one-bedroom in Greenwood is now $2,021.60. The growing neighborhood has a little bit of everything, including the annual historic Greenwood Seafair Parade, making it a growing region of Seattle.

2. Northgate experienced the second-steepest increase of 24.66 percent, with an average price of $1,818.65 fot a one-bedroom. This district is named after the famous Northgate Mall, which was built in 1950 as one of the nation’s first post-World War II retail complexes, and is now undergoing a complete transformation.

3. The Central District, with a year-over-year increase of 19.19 percent, had the third-steepest increase. The average rent for a one-bedroom there is now $2,401.09. The Central District is said to be overflowing with culture and history, and a number of iconic musicians and actors who revolutionized pop culture were originally from the Central District, including Quincy Jones, Sir Mix-a-Lot, Bruce Lee and Jimi Hendrix.

4. Delridge posted a year-over-year average rent increase of 17.95 percent, for a ranking of fourth-highest. Average rent for a one-bedroom here is $1,916.77. The Delridge area is mostly residential with loosely defined zones and a large open space greenbelt. But it used to be known as “Little Pittsburgh” because of its industrial origins.

5. South Lake Union, which some have dubbed "Amazonia" because of Amazon's headquarters here, posted an average rent increase of 17.15 percent, with the average rent for one-bedroom pf $2,547.47. The district is a hub for active young professionals and bustles with hipster coffee shops, busy bars and delicious food trucks.
