Strangers arrived at woman's home after boyfriend posted nudes and her address

18 Nov 2019, 13:37

A mother was left terrified after a dozen strangers turned up to her house in one evening expecting sex with her.

Without her knowing, her boyfriend had posted nude photos online along with her Birmingham address on sex websites.

Builder Darren Rowe, 49, secretly took the images without her consent and uploaded them using a customer’s wifi.

He has now been prosecuted and received a suspended sentence.

Mother-of-three Sharon, who did not give her full name, said: ‘You don’t expect the man that you love, who says he loves you, to try and destroy you.

‘You’ve got to get your head around the fact that potentially thousands of people have seen those pictures of you.

‘One night 12 men came to the door. I was with my three-month-old baby at the time. Another time someone tried to get in the house.’

Rowe, from Rowley Regis, was given a 12-month suspended jail sentence after admitting two counts of disclosing private sexual photographs and films with intent to cause distress at Wolverhampton Crown Court.

His 39-year-old victim told the BBC she was ‘disgusted’ by what she believes is a lenient punishment.

Over a four-month period in summer 2017, around 35 men came to her house, she said.

At first, her partner claimed the photos had been posted by one of her friends, but police uncovered the truth.

Customer services assistant Sharon said: ‘The police think he wanted to play the hero, whereas I think he had certain fetishes, which I’m not interested in.’

She added: ‘I believe he put me in danger for his own enjoyment and I would encourage any other woman going through something like this to report it.

‘I loved this man unconditionally, and for the man you love to try and destroy you mentally is something that you don’t ever get over.

‘You learn to live with it daily unfortunately, but time is a good healer. If anyone is going through this, I beg you to speak out, you are not alone.’

PC Lee Newell, of West Bromwich CID, said: ‘This has been a long and complex case.

‘But our investigations revealed that the only person linked to an email address used to transmit the material was in fact Rowe.
