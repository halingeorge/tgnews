Ronaldo Slams 'Potato Field' After Missing Out On Milestone

18 Nov 2019, 13:28 by SL International

After narrowly missing out on becoming only the second player to reach the milestone of 100 internationals goals, Portugal superstar Cristiano Ronaldo slammed the pitch on which he had to play on Sunday.

Read: Ronaldo Facing Two-Year Ban?

Ronaldo took his international tally to 99 goals during Portugal's 2-0 win over Luxembourg on the weekend, and helped his nation qualify for the 2020 UEFA European Championship.

Despite the result, though, the Juventus star was unhappy with the quality of Luxembourg's pitch, which he compared to a potato field.

Click through the gallery below to see some images of Ronaldo's performance against Luxembourg

"We had two games (during this international break) we had to win," Ronaldo said, as reported by Channel News Asia.  

“I am very happy that we won and qualified for my fifth European Championships. It's difficult to play on these types of fields, a potato field. I don't know how it's possible for teams at this level to play on fields of this quality.

"The performance wasn't beautiful, but we did our job. It was the pitch we had to play, and we won 2-0, which was our priority."

Ronaldo's goal was the 14th he had scored for Portugal in 2019, his best return in a single calendar year since his senior international debut in 2003.
