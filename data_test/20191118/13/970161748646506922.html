Star Wars Mark Hamill won't be in Guardians of the Galaxy after all

18 Nov 2019, 13:24

US filmmaker James Gunn has shut down rumours that Star Wars legend Mark Hamill will appear in Guardians of the Galaxy Vol.3.

After a snap appeared of Gunn and Hamill on social media last May, fans started going wild for the possibility that the Luke Skywalker actor could join the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU).

Alongside the original photo on Instagram, Gunn wrote: ‘Finally stopped by my neighbor @hamillhimself‘s for that cup of coffee we talked about on twitter months ago. And some bbq. And cornbread. And cobbler.

‘And OKAY IT’S TRUE I ATE THE HAMILL’S OUT OF HOUSE AND HOME BACK OFF. Honestly, though he’s an amazing guy and I am really grateful to him and the whole Hamill clan for a great Sunday.’

Hamill also posted the picture, gushing that he spoke about EVERYTHING with Gunn.

And everyone assumed that meant Marvel must have come up in conversation.

‘Such a great day meeting my neighbor @JamesGunn!’ he wrote.

‘We have so much in common & talked about EVERYTHING: the weather, gardening tips, favorite recipes, growing up Catholic, our mutual love of dogs, bleu cheese, risqué limericks & the weather again. #FunWithGunn.’

But Gunn finally poured cold water over these rumours at the weekend.

He held an Instagram Stories Q&A with fans on Sunday 17 November, and admitted he and Hamill never discussed him joining the blockbuster comic book franchise.

‘Mark is my friend,’ he explained. ‘We never had these talks you speak of.’

It all kicked off basically because of one Twitter conversation.

Back in February 2018, a Twitter user wrote: ‘@JamesGunn, could you do me a huge favour and find a role for @HamillHimself in Guardians 3? The awesomeness of it might end me, but I’m willing to risk it. Thanks.’

Then Gunn replied saying the star can ‘come over’ to his house in Malibu to talk about a role.

He replied: ‘I think @HamillHimself lives around the corner from me in Malibu so he can just come over and we can talk about it over a cup of coffee.’

And Hamill, whose character died in the eighth instalment of the franchise, joked he was happy to meet with the filmmaker as he is now an ‘unemployed actor’.

‘I would be happy to do so, both as a good neighbor & an unemployed actor,’ he wrote. ‘All the best, mh … DM me for my contact info if you’re serious. (& a good neighbor) xoxo, mh.’

So they did kind of get everyone’s hopes up.

Meanwhile, Gunn has also claimed that the MCU character Adam Warlock is unlikely to feature in the movie, despite Ayesha, played by Elizabeth Debicki, name-dropping him in the post-credits of 2017’s Guardians of the Galaxy Vol.2.

He confirmed: ‘I never said Adam Warlock was in the movie.’

Now we just want to hurry up and find out who is going to be in the new GOTG film.
