Who is Lyndon Dykes? Lowdown on Australian-born striker linked with Derby County

Latest Derby County transfer news | We take a look at the latest player to be linked with a move to Pride Park during the January transfer window

18 Nov 2019, 13:11 by Mark Wakefield

Derby County have been linked with a move for Livingston striker Lyndon Dykes during the January transfer window.

Reports over the weekend claimed that the Rams are one of six clubs to have made an enquiry for the 24-year-old forward.

Middlesbrough, Cardiff, Swansea, Bristol City and Blackburn have also been credited with an interest in the player.

So who is the latest player to be linked with a move to Pride Park?

Dykes was born on October 7, 1995, in the Gold Coast in Australia, stands at 6ft2” tall and plays predominantly as a striker.

After growing up in Australia, Dykes toured Scotland, where his parents originate, and was signed by Queen of the South in the summer of 2016.

His first goal in professional football came in his sixth game as he scored in QoS’s 3-1 win over Hibernian in the Scottish League Cup tie in August 2016.

In his first season as a professional, Dykes scored just four goals in 40 appearances, but after eventually netting 22 times in 136 outings he was signed by Livingston in the summer.
