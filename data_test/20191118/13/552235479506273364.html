Kilmarnock mum issues desperate plea for teenage daughter to come home after vanishing

Shekira Horner was last seen on Saturday night.

18 Nov 2019, 13:23 by Ross Dunn, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/ross-dunn/

A Kilmarnock mum has issued a desperate plea for her teenage daughter to come home.

Shekira Horner was last seen more than 24 hours ago and her family and friends are now urging her to get in touch with them.

The 17-year-old was last seen heading towards Kilmarnock train station on Saturday evening, her family confirmed.

 

Friends say the hairdressing student's phone has been switched off.

Kelly Anne Horner, Shekira's mum, posted on Facebook this morning: "Still no news on Shekira.

"Her phone is still off and she's not been on social media at all.

"Nobody knows where she is or who she is with. Shekira just come home."

Kelly told the Kilmarnock Standard: "She was last seen half 5 on Saturday heading towards Kilmarnock train station.

"She said to a friend she was going to Glasgow to her uncle's which wasn't true as she wasn't going to her uncle's at all."

 

One friend Rachel Cruickshank: "Has anyone seen Shekira Horner?

"She’s been missing for over 24 hours now, with no contact except for a text last night at 6.30pm.

"Her phone is switched off and when it’s switched back on it’s only for a minute and back off again.

"If anybody hears or sees anything, please contact myself or a member of kira’s family.

"She’s not been on social media and hasn’t been seen since 5.30pm on Saturday. Someone must know something!

"Kira if you see this, nobody is angry at you and you’re not in any trouble.

 

"Just come home, even send a text or phone someone so we know you’re safe.

"We’re all here for you beautiful, let us be there and come home. We love you so much. Get in contact please!
