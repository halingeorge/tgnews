WeWork could lay off thousands as turmoil grows

18 Nov 2019, 16:10 by Noah Manskar

WeWork could soon lay off at least 4,000 employees as the beleaguered office giant grapples with massive financial losses, a new report said.

The bloodletting could see up to 2,500 people cut from WeWork’s core office subleasing business, the New York Times reported Sunday. Another 1,000 people reportedly would be axed as WeWork winds down its Manhattan private school and other offshoots, and about 1,000 maintenance workers would move to an outside contractor.

The planned cuts, which could affect as many as 6,000 workers, are part of a plan to revamp the struggling company over five years that may be presented to employees as soon as Tuesday, according to the Times.

The specter of mass layoffs looms less than a week after reports that WeWork lost $1.25 billion in the quarter that ended in September as the company opened nearly 100 new locations across the world.

The cutbacks are being planned as SoftBank, the Japanese investment firm that bailed out WeWork last month, tries to stabilize the floundering business, the Times reported.

The report that about a third of WeWork’s staff could soon be out of a job marks a stark contrast to the $1.7 billion package its co-founder and ex-CEO Adam Neumann received when he agreed to walk away from the firm last month.

WeWork was reportedly courting T-Mobile CEO John Legere, another unconventional long-haired executive, to replace Neumann. Legere announced plans to leave his current company, which is merging with Sprint, CNBC reported Friday.
