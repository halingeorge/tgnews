Suspect in deadly North Kansas City shooting apprehended in Ottumwa

18 Nov 2019, 16:47 by Beth Waldon

OTTUMWA, Iowa â€” A suspect wanted in connection with a North Kansas City shooting that killed a man and injured another has been captured in Ottumwa.

Wapello County deputies arrested 27-year-old Zachary B. Gifford on Saturday.

Gifford is charged with second-degree murder and armed criminal action in connection with the Nov. 13 shooting death of 28-year-old Jacob Roberson. Gifford is accused of shooting Roberson multiple times in the chest during an altercation at American Inn Hotels in North Kansas City.

Investigators say a second victim, Michael Rudolph, was staying in a neighboring hotel room when a stray bullet grazed his left arm.

Gifford is also accused of fleeing the scene, eventually making his way north to Iowa.

Authorities received a tip on his whereabouts in Ottumwa three days later.

Gifford remains in custody at the Wapello County Jail awaiting transfer to Clay County, Missouri.

The Ottumwa Police Department and Iowa State Patrol assisted the Wapello County Sheriff's Office with Saturday's arrest.
