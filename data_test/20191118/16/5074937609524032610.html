Black Stars pip Sao Tome to go top

18 Nov 2019, 16:08 by Simon Asare, GNA

     

Accra, Nov. 18, GNA - The Black Stars of Ghana recorded their second win in the 2021 Africa Cup of Nations (AFCON) qualifying campaign after dispatching Sao Tome and Principe 1-0 at the Estadio Nacional Stadium.

Jordan Ayew solitary strike from the penalty spot earned the Black Stars all three points as they moved top of Group C with six points while South Africa and Sudan are on three points each after two games. Sao Tome and Principe are at the bottom of the group with no point.

The Black Stars begun the game on the front foot on a very difficult turf, mounting relentless pressure on the Sao Tome who stood resolute in the opening minutes of the game.

Ghana Striker, Emmanuel Boateng was handed the early opportunity past the 20th minute mark but couldn't capitalize on the exquisite pass from Jordan Ayew.

Ghana controlled the game in the first half but couldn't penetrate the defence of the home side as they were often left to shoot outside the box.

The first half ended scoreless with Ghana have about 77 percent of ball possession with the home virtually creating nothing in the opposition's half.

Ghana begun the second half on the bright note, as they searched for the lead and were deservedly awarded a penalty after Sao Tome goalkeeper Gilmar Evsebio brought down Emmanuel Boateng after he poorly handled a feeble shot.

Jordan Ayew stepped up and struck Ghana home from the penalty spot on the 49th minute mark. The home side responded very well after going a goal down as they look to create something in the attacking third.

Sao Tome's attacking prowess obviously lacked the quality to break the Ghana defence who managed to hold onto their one goal lead.
