Tottenham hero Clive Allen backs Harry Kane to make it in the NFL

18 Nov 2019, 16:29

Former Tottenham star Clive Allen has backed Harry Kane to fulfill his ambition of starring in the NFL after his football career comes to an end.

The England captain said earlier in 2019 that becoming a placekicker in the NFL is something he ‘definitely wants to try in 10 or 12 years.’

Kane is a big fan of American football and suggested that crossing codes would give him the chance to become the ‘greatest sportsman ever.

The Spurs striker told ESPN: ‘It goes back to that drive to be the best. Even if I download a game on my phone, can I be the best in the world?

‘If you play in the Premier League and the World Cup and you then play in the NFL, would you then be considered one of the greatest sportsmen ever?’

Allen knows better than most about what it takes to play both sports, having also played for Spurs and England, and for the London Monarchs in NFL Europe.

The 58-year-old recognises that Kane would have a lot to learn but has backed him to manage the switch.

‘Former strikers are the ones that can do it well,’ Allen told the Daily Star. ‘Kane has a great interest in it and I wouldn’t be surprised if in years to come he’ll be kicking an NFL football.

‘Knowing Kane – and having worked with him – I know all about his mentality and attitude.

I’m sure he could adapt his style to do the job and I wouldn’t put it past him being very successful at it.

‘It’s also not got the physical demands of soccer – kicking at least – so players can go on a little longer.’

‘It’s a different technique, certainly from kicking a soccer ball.’The NFL ball is remarkably light and deviates a lot.’The kicking technique is straight leg, locked knee and pointed toe. And where you strike the ball is really important.’It took quite a lot of practice and work for me to hone the skill and make it work.’

Morten Anderson, who is the NFL record appearance-holder and former kicker, says that Kane could not just jump straight into the NFL, but he could certainly learn.

‘It is ludicrous to think you can just step in and do it at that level,’ Andersen told Sky Sports. ‘I want the fans to understand that I don’t feel disrespected, but there is more to it than meets the eye.’

Anderson did offer his services to help Kane with his ambition, though, telling talkSPORT: ‘I don’t think he would have a big learning curve. I would love to work with him. I think that would be fascinating.’
