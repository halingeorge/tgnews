Five candidates for the 2019 General Election in Torbay

Previous Tory MP Kevin Foster is defending the seat he first won from the Liberal Democrats in 2015

18 Nov 2019, 16:03 by Edward Oldfield

Five candidates are competing for votes at the 2019 General Election in Torbay.

Previous Tory MP Kevin Foster is defending the seat he first won from the Liberal Democrats in 2015.

Two years later at the General Election following the Brexit Referendum, Mr Foster increased his majority from 3,000 to 14,000.

The stalled Brexit process has added a new and unpredictable element to the vote on Thursday, December 12.

Torbay voted around six to four in favour of leaving the European Union. 

Mr Foster, a government minister, has been a firm backer of prime minister Boris Johnson and his attempts to get his new Brexit deal approved by Parliament.

The Tory campaign in Torbay was due to benefit from the decision by Brexit Party leader Nigel Farage not to challenge sitting Conservative MPs.

But former Brexit Party candidate James Channer has defied the instruction and is standing as an Independent.

Conservatives are concerned that a pro-Brexit candidate could split the Leave vote, benefiting the Remain-supporting Liberal Democrats.

This time the Liberal Democrat challenger is Lee Howgate, a teacher and former diplomat who is a member of Torbay Council for the Goodrington with Roselands ward.

The Labour Party candidate is Michele Middleditch, and the Green Party challenger is Sam Moss, who stood at the June 2017 vote.

The Torbay constituency covers the towns of Torquay and Paignton, with neighbouring Brixham covered by the Totnes MP.

Analysis carried out for the In Your Area website says the Liberal Democrats would need a 14 per cent swing to win in Torbay, with Labour needing a 17.4 per cent switch of support.

The constituency has a population of 100,000 with 76,000 eligible to vote.

Torbay 2019 General Election candidates

  • James Channer, Independent
  • Kevin Foster, Conservative
  • Lee Howgate, Liberal Democrats
  • Michelle Middleditch, Labour
  • Sam Moss, Green
