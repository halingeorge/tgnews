'Loving daughter' Ellie Bryan, 18, died in Aberystwyth crash

A woman has been arrested on suspicion of causing death by dangerous driving

18 Nov 2019, 16:11 by Laura Clements

An 18-year-old woman who was killed in a night time collision between two cars has been named.

Police have confirmed Ellie Bryan, from Aberystwyth , died in the crash, which happened at around 10pm on Saturday, November 16.

In a statement released on Monday afternoon her family said: "We are distraught by our loss of Ellie. She will be missed by us all.

"Ellie was a loving daughter, sister and granddaughter.

"We would like to thank everyone for their support at this devastating time.

"We would appreciate having time to grieve in privacy."

Ellie was travelling in a black Vauxhall Astra on the A487 when it collided with a silver Audi A6 at Commins Coch, just outside of Aberystwyth.

Dyfed Powys Police also confirmed on Monday they had arrested a driver in connection with the fatal collision.

A spokesman for Dyfed Powys said: "An 18-year-old woman was arrested on suspicion of causing death by dangerous driving, and has been released on bail pending further enquiries.

"The collision occurred on the A487 at around 10pm, and involved two vehicles.

"Enquiries have revealed the involvement of a third car and yesterday the driver of this vehicle was arrested."

Police continue to appeal for witnesses or anyone with information about the collision to contact the Serious Collision Investigation Unit.

They are also keen to speak with anyone travelling along the stretch of road at the time of the collision who might have dash cam footage.

Anyone with information should report it online at http://bit.ly/dppReportOnline, by email at contactcentre@dyfed-powys.pnn.police.uk or by calling 101, quoting reference: DP-20191116-353.
