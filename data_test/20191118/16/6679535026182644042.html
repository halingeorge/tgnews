Primacy of cooperatives in economic development stressed

18 Nov 2019, 16:31 by Special Correspondent

A special lecture, debate and elocution competition were conducted by the Department of Commerce and Financial Studies, Bharathidasan University, as part of 66th National Cooperative Week celebrations.

Delivering a lecture on ‘Innovation and Best Practices in Cooperatives’, C Pitchai, Professor of Cooperation, Gandhigram Rural Institute, Gandhigram, centered his talk on ‘whether cooperative is an alternative or substitute for communism or capitalism’.

Cooperatives have been successful in the fields of insurance, wholesale and retail, agriculture and food, banking and financial services throughout the world, including USA, European, African, Australia, and Asian, Prof. Pitchai said.

The uniqueness of cooperatives was in the ethics in business, and in maintaining a balance between private and public sector.

Two groups of three students each took part in a debate, one group on ‘Cooperatives Promotes Weaker Sections’ and the other on ‘Cooperatives Does Not Promote Weaker Sections’.

The group that spoke in favour of cooperatives listed the advantages to economy in the forms of democratic principles, especially women entrepreneurship, lending to agriculturists, schemes for disabled persons, providing educational loan, and promoting fishery development.

The other group in the debate described the problems that were faced by agriculturalists, women, disabled persons and fishermen.

The elocution competition on ‘Technology Adoption and Digitalisation of Cooperatives’ attracted participation of students firom various Post Graduate and Five Year Integrated Programmes in the University Departments.

Students representing Bharathidasan School of Management, Department of Sociology, and Department of Tamil Studies won the first, second and third prizes respectively.

Delivering the valedictory address, L. Ganesan, Finance Officer In-Charge, Professor of Economics, Bharathidasan University, explained how cooperatives improve standard of living of rural poor, provide job security, promote social harmony, create casteless society, and pave strong foundation for women development.

Mohan Gnana Olivu, Professor and Assistant Editor of SMART Journal of Business Management Studies, also addressed the students during the valedictory session.

M. Selvam, Professor and Head, Department of Commerce and Financial Studies, and Dean, Faculty of Management, said the programme envisaged inculcating ethical business practice among students.
