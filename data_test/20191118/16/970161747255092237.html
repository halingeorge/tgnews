Most Americans now want Donald Trump booted out of White House, poll claims

18 Nov 2019, 16:30

Most Americans now think Donald Trump should be removed as president and booted out of the White House, according to a new poll.

A survey undertaken by ABC News found 51% of people want Trump to be impeached and convicted, which would spell the end of his presidency.

The same survey found that 70% of Americans thought Trump was wrong to pressure Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky to probe his opponent Joe Biden.

Just over one in 10 (13%) agreed Trump’s behavior with Zelensky was wrong, but said it is not an impeachable offense.

And exactly a quarter of people polled think Trump has done nothing wrong.

He faces ongoing impeachment hearings over the July 25 phone call to Zelensky, which Democrats claim constitutes an abuse of office for personal gain.

Trump, who denies any wrongdoing, is accused of offering the Ukraine $400million in military aid, then suggesting it probe Joe Biden’s son Hunter.

Hunter Biden has faced questions over his links to a Ukrainian natural gas company called Burisma, although denies any impropriety. His father is front-runner to be nominated as a presidential candidate by the Democrat party next year, meaning he would battle Trump in the November 2020 presidential election.

Ukraine has faced repeated aggression from Russia, with the US cash intended to help bolster its defenses and deter Russian President Vladimir Putin from a potential invasion.

Public impeachment hearings continue on Tuesday, and are expected to wrap up by the end of the week.

Last week former US Ambassador to the Ukraine Marie Yovanovitch told the hearing on Capitol Hill that she felt ‘threatened’ by Trump’s behavior to her.

The highly successful diplomat was fired from her posting in Kiev in May 2019 after a smear campaign by Trump lawyer Rudy Giuliani, reportedly at the president’s behest.

She believes she was fired because of her focus on tackling corruption.

The lower house of the US Congress – the House of Representatives or ‘House’ are likely to prepare articles of impeachment – equivalent to charges.

If enough representatives vote to impeach Trump on these charges, he will face a trial in the upper house of Congress – the Senate.

That will see represenatives from the House present their case just as prosecutors would at a criminal trial.

The Senate then holds a vote on whether or not to convict the president, with a two thirds majority needed to remove him from office.

It is currently comprised of a majority of Republican senators, with those politicians currently considered unlikely to vote their own president out of office.
