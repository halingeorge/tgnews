Samsung is already leaking the Galaxy Fold 2

18 Nov 2019, 16:57 by Jared Peters

Samsung’s Galaxy Fold has only been available for a couple of months at this point, but that’s not going to hold up the company’s other plans to release a follow-up device. In fact, that successor might come sooner rather than later, since Samsung’s own website has already listed some references to a Galaxy Fold 2 in support pages.

The references come from Samsung Africa’s website and mention the SM-F700F, which is reportedly codenamed Bloom internally. The original model number for the Galaxy Fold was SM-F900F, so we know this is going to fall in that same category of folding devices, even if it isn’t necessarily called the Galaxy Fold 2.

This device is also rumored to launch not long after the Galaxy S11, assuming we don’t have as many delays as the original Galaxy Fold. That timeline makes sense, since it would’ve released about a year after the original Galaxy Fold if its launch date hadn’t been pushed back a few months. It should make for an interesting start to the year for Samsung.

Aside from this model number reference, though, we don’t know anything else about the phone. It could be a continuation of what the Galaxy Fold started, or it could be something more in line with the brand new Motorola RAZR with a clamshell folding design instead. Samsung likes to throw a lot of things at the wall to see what sticks, so that wouldn’t surprise me.
