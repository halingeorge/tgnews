'Small margins' - Derby County goalkeeper identifies where Rams need to improve

Latest Derby County news | The Rams have picked up 21 points from their opening 16 Sky Bet Championship matches and are in 16th place in the league table

18 Nov 2019, 16:00 by Mark Wakefield

Derby County goalkeeper Kelle Roos is confident that the Rams can climb the Sky Bet Championship table in the coming weeks.

The Rams are currently in 16th place as we approach the end of the November international break, and resume their league campaign against Preston North End at Pride Park Stadium on Saturday.

Phillip Cocu’s side have amassed 21 points from their opening 16 league fixtures, but the contrast between their home and away form is starling.

At Pride Park, Derby have won four of their eight matches and picked up 15 points from a possible 24.

However, on the road Derby have struggled. The opening day win away at Huddersfield Town remains Derby’s only victory on their travels in the league – they have accumulated just six points and have lost four matches.

Roos says that improving on Derby’s away form is key to their ambitions of climbing up the Championship table as the season progresses.

“It all starts on the training ground,” Roos told the club’s website .

“You must work hard every day and then that becomes the mindset.
