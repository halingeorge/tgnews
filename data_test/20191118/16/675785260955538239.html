Odd behind-the-scenes details from Prince Andrew's interview in Queen's quarters at Palace

Prince Andrew was grilled by Emily Maitlis about his relationship with Jeffrey Epstein, but the moments leading up to the televised chat were slightly odd

18 Nov 2019, 16:23 by Zoe Forsey, https://www.facebook.com/zoeforseymirror/

Many people sitting down to watch Prince Andrew's interview about his relationship with paedophile Jeffrey Epstein were left stunned and baffled by a number of his remarks.

He told Newsnight's Emily Maitlis that he didn't sweat - something that has also left medical experts scratching their heads, and couldn't have had sex with accuser Victoria Roberts as he was at Pizza Express in Woking.

But before he had even started talking about the convicted sex offender's "unbecoming" behaviour, there were a number of bizarre moments between the Queen's son and Maitlis.

Writing for the Times, Maitlis reveals what happened on the day she went to Buckingham Palace to ask Andrew a series of very difficult questions.

When the crew arrived they were taken in through the Queen's entrance and started to set up in the south drawing room.

 

She writes: "I am trying to understand the significance of the Queen giving us her own formal quarters in which to film, but it feels like a code I do not properly understand. Is she endorsing her son? The need for this interview? Or am I reading way too much into every step, merely because there are so many of them."

She admits then when she walked into the room she was so taken by her surroundings that didn't immediately spot that Andrew was already there.

She was carrying so much stuff that she didn't have a free hand to shake his, and feared that if she tried to curtsy she "may not make it back up again".

When she nipped to the bathroom she realised she had chocolate on her teeth, which she wiped off with what she realised a moment too late was a Buckingham Palace handtowl.

She writes: "This is the most disastrous start to any interview I can imagine. And then I suddenly recognise it for what it is: pure stomach-gripping nerves. And the recognition of something so obvious relaxed me."

Back in the room, Andrew was offering advice to the engineering team setting up the make-shift set.

 

She writes: "He starts directing the mike placement until the cameraman and the sound engineer are following his orders. He finally sits back, satisfied that he has solved a technical conundrum for the team."

She says that Andrew didn't seem particularly nervous in the lead up to their chat, which was scheduled to last 40 minutes.

Prince Andrew has been heavily criticised for a number of his comments in the interview, including the shocking moment when he described Epstein's record as a convicted sex offender as "unbecoming".

Behind the scenes palace sources have described the duke's performance as “utterly jaw dropping” with one insider suggesting aides were left “dumbfounded” after watching the Newsnight interview on Saturday evening.

But the duke apparently saw it differently, telling friends he thought the interview went “pretty well” after being quizzed on allegations he had sex with Epstein accuser Virginia Giuffre (nee Roberts), who claims the paedophile kept her as a "slave".
