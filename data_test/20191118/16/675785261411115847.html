Jewish man takes on yobs hurling anti-semitic abuse in kebab shop

Marlon Solomon, 40, performs a one-man show on anti-semitism and was a match for the two thugs who denied the holocaust and then claimed Jews should be 'wiped out'

18 Nov 2019, 16:57 by Alexandra Rucki, Lila Randall

Two men harassed a Jewish man in a kebab shop while he waited for his takeaway - but they picked the wrong person to insult.

Marlon Solomon, 40, was targeted by two yobs with ‘unfriendly banter’ that quickly turned heated, with the men shouting “are you a f*****g Jew?”

Mr Solomon - who performs a one-man show about anti-semitism - responded to this with, ‘I am a f*****g Jew’, which sparked a torrent of anti-semitic abuse from the two men.

He said the men began saying Jewish people should be "wiped out" and denied the Holocaust took place, reports Manchester Evening News .

Marlon began to film at Al-Faisal, in the Northern Quarter what was being said on his mobile phone and captured the aftermath of the incident.

In the clip one man can be heard saying: "You know what it's a load of b*******? It is a lie, the Holocaust."

"The Jews would be wiped off the face of the earth," a man is heard saying.

Marlon is heard responding: "There is more decent people in this country that know you are full of s***.

"This is the best country to live in as a Jew. You are going to try and change that, but you won't because this country is better than that."

Marlon claims the pair gave a fascist salute as they left the takeaway.

Greater Manchester Police confirmed they are investigating the incident, which took place at around 10.30pm on Saturday, November 16.

Marlon, who performs a one-man show about anti-Semitism, said: "They both went on saying 'Jews are the worst, Jews are the worst of the lot you rule the world'.

"They were saying they wished 'Jews had been all wiped out'.

"The interesting bit was they were talking all about Palestine. One of them was ex-Army.

"They were hypocritical and so racist going on about his grandfather serving in Palestine.

"My mum pointed out her dad also served in Palestine for the British Army alongside those guys."

Marlon added: "We have been in Manchester for 120 years, our family.

"I haven't seen anything like this before. This is the current political climate at the moment, but part of me thinks these people have always been there.

"My family had to fight in the former Jewish quarter where Strangeways is.

"It is a really dark time at the moment with people like this."

A GMP spokeswoman said: "Police were called to Thomas Street, Manchester. It was reported to us as a hate incident.

"Enquiries are ongoing. There have been no arrests."
