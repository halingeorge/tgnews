Cab driver accused of stabbing a ‘fare-beater’ will turn himself in to NYPD

18 Nov 2019, 16:25 by Daniel Cassady

The cab driver accused of stabbing an alleged fare-beater in The Bronx will turn himself into the NYPD on Monday in the hopes of clearing his name.

Sandy de Jesus Rodriguez will surrender to detectives in the 46th Precinct in University Heights to speak about the alleged Oct. 13th stabbing of a man inside an apartment building on Webster Avenue near 184th Street.

De Jesus Rodriguez claims he was defending himself against the man and his girlfriend, who allegedly robbed him of $190 after catching a ride. He said the pair then attacked him when he followed them into the apartment building to get the money back.

“The only mistake I made was trying to get the $190.00 the female passenger stole from me. I should have taken the loss and drove away but it was hard not bringing food to my table. I hope that they are held responsible for what they did,” said de Jesus Rodriguez in a press release by the New York State Federation of Taxi Drivers.

The cabbie claims it wasn’t the first time he was targeted by the lovebirds. He said the same couple skipped out on a fare four months before the alleged stabbing.

De Jesus Rodriguez is calling on police to check medical records of the man he is accused of stabbing, which he claims will prove his innocence.

“I did not hit him or stab him unless he intentionally cut himself to make it like but I did it but I did not cut him in any way,” the cab driver previously told The Post.
