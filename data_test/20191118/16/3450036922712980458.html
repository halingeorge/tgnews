Southeast Iowa woman seriously injured in morning crash

18 Nov 2019, 16:50 by KTVO News Desk

APPANOOSE COUNTY, Iowa â€” A southeast Iowa woman was seriously injured Monday morning in a one-vehicle crash.

According to the Appanoose County Sheriff's Office, the Appanoose County Law Enforcement Center received a 911 call just before 8 a.m. of a vehicle crash on Highway 2, about 2 miles east of Centerville.

When emergency crews arrived, they found a car overturned on the north side of the highway in a ditch.

Cindy Montegna, of rural Moulton, Iowa, was found lying on the ground near the car.

Montegna was transported to Centerville by ambulance and then airlifted to a Des Moines hospital with life-threatening injuries.

A preliminary investigation of the crash shows that Montegna was traveling west bound on Highway 2 when she lost control of her vehicle on a curve. The car left the roadway and began to roll.

Montegna was ejected from the car.

The vehicle came to rest on its top, receiving major damage after rolling, striking a utility pole and an embankment.

There is no word on Montegna's condition at this time.

The crash is under investigation by the Appanoose County Sheriff's Office. The sheriff's office was assisted by the Centerville Police Department, Centerville Fire and Rescue and Alliant Energy.
