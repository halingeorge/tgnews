'We have been looking' - latest on new investment plans for Derby County

Owner and chairman Mel Morris bought the Rams in 2015 and has invested tens of millions of pounds in the club

18 Nov 2019, 16:32 by Steve Nicholson

Mel Morris says the challenge to succeed at Derby County remains a "constant quest".

Derby's owner and chairman, who bought the Rams in 2015, has invested tens of millions of pounds in the club and he has been searching for new investment.

That investment could be in place by the end of the year, DerbyshireLive understands.

Morris said recently that the club is "close" to landing new investment.

Those discussions are ongoing, and progressing.

Morris recently received a Lifetime Achievement Award at the 2019 Derby Telegraph Sports Awards' ceremony at which he said: "We have been looking for investment and we are close to trying to get something sorted out on that front, which is important," he added.

"I have always believed that it's not the best structure to have a single person at the club because it's expensive, but also because you need a balance. So we have been looking for the right person."

As DerbyshireLive has reported previously, it is likely to be part-investment and not a takeover.

Mel Morris' words at the Sports Awards' evening pointed to the new investment being a 'partnership', with him staying on which is something he has said he is very keen to do.

Morris was asked on the night if owning Derby County has been what he expected.

"It's not been anything like I thought it would be like, it has been a lot tougher than I thought it would be," he said.

"But it's one of the challenges. What keeps us going is the fans, the supporters of the club.

"We want to achieve. We want to continue to strive for more. That is a constant quest for us.

"So, of course, that needs money but it also needs the people - and we have got some great people."

Derby have gone close to winning promotion in four of the past six seasons.

They were beaten finalists in 2014 and again in May of this year when they lost to Aston Villa at Wembley.
