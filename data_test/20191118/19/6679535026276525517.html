Project to clear waterlogging to be completed before March 31

Corpn., high-level committee told to submit reports to HC

18 Nov 2019, 19:58 by Special Correspondent

Projects for clearing waterlogging in the city will be completed before March 31, the State has informed the Kerala High Court.

A master plan for preventing waterlogging in the city will be prepared before December 16.

A decision to this effect was taken at a meeting convened by the Chief Secretary, the Advocate General informed the High Court.

The high-level committee formed for addressing the issue and the Kochi Corporation should take all possible steps for averting waterlogging in the city.

The civic body and the committee should submit periodic reports to the court, directed the High Court.

It was on a petition filed by a few persons seeking directions to the authorities to clean the canals and clear waterlogging that the court issued the directives.

The District Disaster Management Authority will lead the drive for averting waterlogging. A technical committee will also be formed for the purpose.

Under the canal cleaning project, Mullassery, Thevara-Perandoor, Market, Chilavannoor and Edappally canals will be cleaned. The District Collector has been asked to monitor the cleaning of the canals and submit periodic reports to the court.

The authorities have held discussions with the agency that has been asked to look into the issue of flooding and waterlogging the city.
