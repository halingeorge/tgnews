Claudio Yacob and Zach Clough back in action for Nottingham Forest but how did they perform?

Two of Forest's fringe players were involved in a behind-close-doors friendly with local rivals

18 Nov 2019, 19:00 by Barry Cooper

Claudio Yacob and Zach Clough were part of a Nottingham Forest team that won 3-2 against Notts County in a behind closed doors friendly on Monday.

The game, played at the Nigel Doughty Academy was a side largely made up of young players, overseen by Jimmy Gilligan and Chris Cohen, with Clough and Yacob along with Toby Edser all given run-outs against the Magpies.

Notts assistant manager Neil Cox took charge of Forest’s cross-city rivals in a game which gave the National League outfit an opportunity to get minutes into senior players coming back from injury, and praised the Reds for helping get the game on.

“Nottingham Forest have looked after us this morning and put on a good pitch with the weather being bad,” Cox said, having seen Max Ram, Ethan Stewart and Will Swan score for the hosts.

Yacob and Clough have yet to feature under new head coach Sabri Lamouchi, with the Argentinian’s last outing coming at QPR in April, while former Bolton man Clough hasn’t played for the Reds since an FA Cup game at Hull last January and has been out on loan at Rochdale and Wanderers.

Forest’s Under-23 haven’t tasted action since losing at Southampton earlier this month, and saw last Friday’s game at Hull City called off due to the weather.

They're next in action this Friday when they host Bolton at the City Ground.
