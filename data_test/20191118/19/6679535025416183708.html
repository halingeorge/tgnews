Municipal school students to learn composting, waste segregation

There will be at least one class per month, says GVMC Commissioner

18 Nov 2019, 19:55 by Harish Gilai

The Greater Visakhapatnam Municipal Corporation (GVMC) has decided to launch orientation classes on home composting and segregation of waste at source in municipal schools.

“There will be at least one class per month on segregation of dry, wet waste and hazardous waste at source and home composting. The orientation classes aims at providing students a hands-on approach towards the issue. Awareness on waste management in the tender will help children do their bit towards protection of environment and reduction of waste,” says Municipal Commissioner G. Srijana.

According to J.V. Ratnam of Green Climate, an NGO, children need to be sensitised to home composting, source segregation and other eco-friendly techniques, given the growing pollution levels.

“Many people still dump or burn garbage, contributing substantially to environment pollution. The Swachh Bharat guidelines also stress reduction of waste. Introducing such techniques in school is a good move,” he opines.

Mr. Ratnam also says that they have started home composting classes in a few schools in the city earlier and a school in Bhimili has done well in this regard.

Around 30,000 students are studying in 147 schools including 116 primary schools, 27 high schools under the purview of the GVMC.

The GVMC is giving a top priority to waste management techniques and is planning to ensure that waste is being reduced right at the source.

SHG women sensitised

The Urban Community Development (UCD) wing of the civic body is planning to create awareness among homemakers on waste management such as segregation of waste, home composting and house farming. A drive involving a large number of women working in SHGs associated with the GVMC is also being undertaken. The women are being sensitised to home composting.

“Home composting is a growing trend in the urban area these days. You grow vegetables in your home. This is for our health and we have been creating awareness over this,” Ms. Srijana adds.
