De Blasio formally guarantees pay hikes for pre-K educators

18 Nov 2019, 19:24 by Selim Algar

Mayor Bill de Blasio and City Council Speaker Corey Johnson formalized the deal they struck with early education teacher unions guaranteeing pay hikes over the next three years, officials announced Monday.

The accord with District Councils 37 and 1701 will boost salaries for certified pre-K educators by $17,000 to $20,000, depending on their level of education — putting them on par with Department of Education staffers.

“This City Council believes strongly in tackling pay inequities among city workers, and as part of that commitment we promised to address the gap between community-based early childhood educators and their Education Department counterparts,” Johnson said in a statement.

By October of 2021, early education teachers with a bachelor’s degree will pull in $61,070 while those holding a master’s will make $68,652.

The unions had argued that paltry pay contributed to a 30 percent turnover rate for early education workers, far higher than the 10 percent rate for DOE teachers.

“Early childhood education opens up a world of possibilities for our youngest New Yorkers and their families,” de Blasio said in a statement.
