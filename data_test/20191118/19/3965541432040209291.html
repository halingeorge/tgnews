Oregon mother arrested for leaving child in locked car, officer cuts arm breaking window

18 Nov 2019, 19:33 by News Staff

GRANTS PASS, Ore. (KTVL) – A mother was arrested, accused of leaving her infant locked inside a car Saturday afternoon at the Josephine County Fairgrounds.

An officer ended up cutting his forearm after breaking out one of the vehicle’s windows to rescue the child at about 1 p.m., the Grants Pass Department of Public Safety told our affiliates at News 10.

The mother, identified as Stephanie Larkin-Reinert, 34, of Grants Pass, was later found and arrested on second-degree Child Neglect and Reckless Endangerment charges.

Police noted that the car was partially parked in the sun. Officer reports state Larkin-Reinert was observed with two other kids at a flea market, getting popcorn for them.

The mother has since posted bail. Charges from the Josephine County District Attorney's Office reflect only the single count of Child Neglect.

Police have not released the child’s current condition.
