Soldier caught twice by paedophile hunters while trying to meet girls for sex

18 Nov 2019, 19:18

A former soldier who fell for paedophile hunter sting operations twice in a row after walking free from prison has been jailed.

Anthony Nendick, 36, sent sexual messages to a fictitious 13-year-old girl played by an adult from the ‘Children’s Warriors’ group.

He had left prison just two weeks earlier, having been released nine months into a 16-month sentence for attempting to incite a child to take part in a sexual activity.

Nendick represented himself at Teesside Crown Court, where jurors heard of his string of online child sex offences dating back five years.

He was given a three-year community order in 2014 after pleading guilty to inciting a real underage girl to engage in sexual activity.

Prosecutor Jo Kidd said Nendick sent pictures of himself to the girl online, encouraging her to do the same, but was arrested after she told her mother.

Several years later he began sending sordid messages to a decoy 13-year-old girl on social media, the court heard.

Jurors were told how he encouraged the stooge to watch pornography and touch herself, and sent an image of him doing the same.

The trial resulted in the 16-month sentence handed to him in January this year.

Two weeks after his release on September 13, he messaged another decoy 13-year-old girl, telling her to touch herself and that he wanted to have sex with her, jurors heard.

He gave his address to the decoy on request, only to be confronted by Children’s Warriors and the police.

Nendick, of Middlesbrough, admitted to again attempting to incite a child to engage in sexual activity between September 27 and October 2.

He also admitted breaching a sex offender order by not telling authorities of the phone he used for the offences.

He refused to explain himself after Judge Deborah Sherwin questioned him on telling a probation officer he had no sexual interest in children.

Judge Sherwin told him: ‘You put your needs before those of any child.

‘Is there anything you would like to say to me by way of mitigation?’ she added.

Nendick replied: ‘No, your Honour. No.’

Sentencing, Judge Sherwin said: ‘I’m unable to do anything other than draw a conclusion that you are attracted to juvenile female children.

‘And that you do pose a high risk of harm to children when you communicate with any genuine children in future.

‘In those circumstances there really is no reasonable alternative to custody.’

She took into account his background and his post-traumatic stress disorder suffered from distressing experiences in the Army.

He was jailed for three years, while his 10-year sexual harm prevention order (SHPO) and his signature on the sex offenders’ register were extended to become indefinite.
