Prince Andrew loses support for his charitable work over Epstein case

18 Nov 2019, 19:03

Accountancy firm KPMG has announced that it will not be renewing its sponsorship of Prince Andrew’s Pitch@Palace scheme.

It comes after the the Duke of York gave an interview to the BBC, in which he denied he had sex with Virginia Roberts when she was 17.

In the interview on Saturday, the Prince was also questioned about his links to convicted paedophile Jeffrey Epstein, who died in prison earlier this year.

Royal commentators described the royal’s Newsnight appearance as a ‘car crash.’

The Financial Times reported that in light of the Duke’s tone in the interview, companies have begun reconsidering their links to him.

The Pitch@Palace scheme is an initiative that seeks to ‘amplify and accelerate the work of entrepeneurs’.

KPMG’s sponsorship of the scheme came to an end in October and the accountancy firm has decided not to renew it, according to Sky News.

Insurance firm Aon is also believed to be distancing itself from Prince Andrew in a bid to protect its reputation.
