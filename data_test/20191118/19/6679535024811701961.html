Protest against transmission towers on agriculture lands

Farmers court arrest; agitation staged in 13 districts

18 Nov 2019, 19:50 by Staff Reporter

Farmers associations and political parties on Monday staged protests in parts of Coimbatore and Tiruppur against setting up of transmission towers on agriculture lands.

In Coimbatore, the protests were held at Karumathampatti and Sulthanpet. V.M.C. Manoharan, Coimbatore rural district president of Congress, participated in the protest at Karumathampatti. In Sulthanpet, DMKâ€™s Pongalur N. Palanisamy took part along with members of Federation of Farmers Protection Association. The protesters courted arrest. Police removed eleven persons from Karumathampatti and 47 from Sulthanpet, sources said.

Tiruppur

As many as 314 protesters were removed by police in various parts of Tiruppur on Monday.

A total of 103 protesters including nine women in Palladam, 76 including 14 women in Kangeyam, 88 including three women in Dharapuram and 47 protesters in Kaniyur were removed, police sources said.

Muthu Viswanathan from Tamil Nadu Farmers Protection Association took part in the protest in Kangeyam and S. Muthukannan, district CPI(M) secretary, in Dharapuram.

Police sources said the protesters were kept in wedding halls and police station till further action was decided, as this was a State-wide agitation where farmers associations staged protests in 13 districts across Tamil Nadu.

Protests against transmission towers have been continuing for the past few months in Tiruppur.

On September 13, Shanmugasundaram, Muthu Viswanathan, Parthasarathy, Thangamuthu and Easan from Tamil Nadu Farmers Protection Association were arrested and remanded for a month in Coimbatore Central Prison till October 12 after organising protests against installation of transmission towers on agriculture lands.
