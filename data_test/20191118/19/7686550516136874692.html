England head coach Eddie Jones admits regret over calling Wales a 'little s**t place'

The outspoken England coach had made his barbed comment at an event in Japan

18 Nov 2019, 19:11 by Andy Howell

Eddie Jones has opened up the about the two major off-field blunders he's made since being England coach.

And they concern Wales and Warren Gatland with Jones admitting his own goals proved costly.

The first came when a video was leaked of the outspoken Australia bad-mouthing Wales and Ireland at a corporate event in Japan.

It came on the eve of England's failed attempt to deny Ireland a Grand Slam in the 2018 Six Nations.

"In my attempt to muse the Japanese audience I referred to the 'scummy Irish' and Wales as a 'little s**t place' ," wrote Jones in his new autobiography My Life And Rugby.

"I was embarrassed. Not only because the slurs weren't funny but because they weren't my real views.

"I was going for a cheap laugh. While it was clear in the video I was smiling and joking, it looked bad. It was bad."

Jones' second mistake was a howler which he feels may have cost any chance of beating Gatland to the job of Lions head honcho for the cream of British and Irish rugby's 2021 three-Test series with new world champions South Africa.

"I have for a long time wanted to coach the Lions. I'd kept it a secret that I had set my sights on the 2021 tour of South Africa," wrote Jones, who steered England to this month's Rugby World Cup final.

But he made a public gaffe when interviewed by a journalist from Brisbane, Australia.

"I tried to have a laugh with him and said coaching the Lions was an ambassador job. 'The last thing I want to do is spend eight weeks in a blazer'," recalled Jones.

"'I'm a coach. I'd rather coach the Queensland Sheffield Shield [cricket] team'.

"Again, it was a terrible lapse of judgement. To this day, I don't know why I said it."

Jones lost out to Gatland when the appointment was made but still hopes one day to lead the Lions, saying it would be an "honour and a privilege."
