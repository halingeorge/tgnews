Liverpool fans explain what Jurgen Klopp should do with Xherdan Shaqiri

Xherdan Shaqiri has struggled for game-time under Jurgen Klopp at Liverpool

18 Nov 2019, 19:25 by Paul Clarke

Whilst Liverpool are enjoying a fantastic season so far, it has been another frustrating spell for Xherdan Shaqiri.

The 28-year-old Switzerland star made the switch from Stoke City ahead of the 2018/19 season and provided an instant impact last term.

He bagged six Premier League goals but his chances became fewer and far between during the back end of the campaign, something which has followed on this term.

Shaqiri has been widely labelled the 'forgotten man' at Liverpool during what could be a potentially historic season for Jurgen Klopp's side.

But Shaqiri, whilst feeling dissatisfied by his lack of opportunities, insists he is still fighting for his chance despite starting just four games in 2019 and none at all this season due to injury issues and competition for places.

"Of course I’m dissatisfied that I do not play more often," Shaqiri said last month. “If that is not the reaction of a pro, he’s in the wrong job. He (Klopp) cannot personally discuss the role of each player, that’s normal. We have a big, talented squad, and many have to wait for their chance."

The issue of Shaqiri's lack of game-time rose its head again on Monday morning thanks to a piece from ECHO writer Paul Gorst, who suggested that Klopp could still rely on the Swiss star at some point.

This viewpoint naturally got Liverpool fans talking and led to reaction aplenty.

The love from Liverpool fans for the player is clear no matter the verdict, though the majority do concur with Gorst's stance.
