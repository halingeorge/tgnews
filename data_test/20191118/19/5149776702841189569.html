Two pedestrians, 60 and 69, left in hospital after being hit by car in Hucknall

A 25-year-old man was arrested on suspicion of causing injury by dangerous driving and resisting arrest

18 Nov 2019, 19:49 by Ben Reid

Two pedestrians were left in hospital after being hit by a car in Hucknall.

Nottinghamshire Police was called to Portland Road, Hucknall, at around 7pm on Sunday (November 17) after reports of the crash.

A 69-year-old man and a 60-year-old woman were taken to hospital for treatment for injuries not believed to be life-threatening.

A 25-year-old man was arrested on suspicion of causing injury by dangerous driving and resisting arrest following the incident.

Inspector Dean Gallacher, of Nottinghamshire Police, said: “Our officers acted quickly to arrest the suspect after two people were injured.

"Thankfully, they are not believed to be life-threatening injuries but it could have been more serious.

“If you witnessed the incident or have information or dashcam footage that could aid officers in their investigation, please contact 101 and quote incident number 667 of November 17 2019.”
