Strictly's Michelle Visage chokes up in tears defending tribute to LGBT+ community

The Drag Race star was emotional while admitting she wanted to make a powerful statement that 'love is greater than hate' with her final ever Strictly Come Dancing routine

18 Nov 2019, 19:13 by Kyle O'Sullivan, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/kyle-osullivan/

Michelle Visage started 'choking up' in tears on tonight's It Takes Two while defending her tribute to the LGBT+ community.

The Drag Race star, 51, was the shock eight celebrity to leave Strictly Come Dancing this weekend after her street commercial routine to Madonna's iconic hit Vogue.

Michelle said she "went out in a blaze of gay glory" and her dance with pro partner Giovanni Pernice was a "love letter to my lovely LGBT+ community".

Presenter Rylan Clark Neal asked Michelle how she felt about claims it was not a dance online before showing her some support from a fan.

"That chokes me up," confessed emotional Michelle while Rylan passed her some tissues.

She admitted: "This was not about a dance. It was about a statement. Love is greater than hate. So much more than just a dance. It was beautiful.

"It meant so much to do this on a platform. Shine light on people who never get the attention they deserve and do it with class, sass and a fierceness."

"Some things are more important than the winning. You can’t move mountains by sitting back. Voices are being heard."

Turning to Giovanni, she added: "I want to say my partner has been by me all the way through this there by my side.

Giovanni admitted that they both knew the street commercial dance was a risk and could see them go home.

He said: "Both us said it's a risk. its not a style you see often in Strictly Come Dancing. We knew for a fact if we are in the dance-off with any other people we’d go home as it's different.

"It is a style, a different style of dance. We wanted to send a message and were sure this message arrived everywhere. We were happy to go out sending a message to everybody and we were proud of it."

Michelle and Giovanni were booted out of the competition after all the judges chose to save YouTuber Saffron Barker and her partner AJ Pritchard.

She branded her experience "amazing" and admitted that she couldn't have done the show without her professional partner Giovanni.

Michelle said: "At 51 years old and never danced in my life, I never thought I’d be able to do what I have done and to have such an incredible partner.

"To be here for my husband and my kids and the entire community, that’s why I did what I did. I am so honoured to be welcomed by my new friends here.”

Speaking about Giovanni, she added: "Obviously I couldn’t have done it without him. He has been so amazing, so transformative. Just my rock and my best friend throughout this entire competition.

"He could take it as much as he could dish it, and I needed somebody like that. I’m going to miss seeing him every day. I would never have wanted to do this with anyone else."

Praising his partner, Giovanni said: "You are wonderful. We laugh, we cry, we fight and we argue and we’v e done everything but I’ve loved every single second.

"I think you are inspirational and we did this dance and wanted to send a message.

"You are a phenomenal lady and I am so proud of everything we’ve done. I’m here to say thank you, thank you, thank you."
