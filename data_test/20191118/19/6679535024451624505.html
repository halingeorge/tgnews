Insurance firm told to pay ₹60.97 lakh as compensation

Payout for 2012 accident in which 2 died

18 Nov 2019, 19:51 by Special Correspondent

The Motor Accident Claims Tribunal has awarded a total compensation of ₹60.97 lakh to 22 persons who sustained injuries, and kin of two persons who sustained fatal injuries in a road accident in 2012.

According to the petitions filed, 24 persons were standing near Mettu village, Chithamuru Mandal, Nellore, A.P. when a tanker capsized. In the subsequent fire, all the petitioners sustained injuries and two persons — Damodhar Reddy and T.Krishnaiah — sustained fatal injuries.

K.Sivakumar, owner of the tanker and The Oriental Insurance Company Limited, the insurer of the vehicle, were named respondents.

Based on the submitted evidence and documents, the tribunal concluded that the accident occurred due to rash and negligent driving by the tanker driver. All the petitioners were entitled to compensation, it said.

It awarded a compensation of ₹30,000 each to 11 persons and ₹40,000 to one person who sustained injury. It awarded compensation of ₹10,64,700 to kin of Damodhar Reddy. Out of it, the tribunal said his wife is entitled for ₹7,45,290 and his son ₹3,19,410.

To the kin of T.Krishnaiah, the tribunal awarded a compensation of ₹10,32,200. Out of the compensation, his parents are entitled to a sum of ₹5,16,100 each.

Gundala Sathish and T.Mathaiah, who sustained burn injuries, were awarded compensation of ₹1,27,200 and ₹1,08,000, respectively. Vataru Muneiah and Eduru Jayapal were awarded ₹1,78,500 and ₹2,75,600, respectively, for disability sustained in the accident.

Six more persons, who sustained burns and injuries, were awarded compensation as follows — Nandhaiah ₹1,58,000, Vakati Chandramuli Reddy ₹6,49,300, Sanam Rajasekar Reddy ₹16,69,200, Kasturaiah ₹1,75,300, Suman ₹1,61,500 and Prathap ₹1,28,000. The tribunal directed the insurance firm to pay the compensation.
