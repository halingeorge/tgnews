Aaditya Thackeray holds review meeting with Sena leaders, BMC officials in Worli

In the last week of September, Aaditya had said that people from all over the world would come to see the development pattern in Worli in the next five years.

18 Nov 2019, 22:24 by Express News Service

Even as government formation is yet to take place, Aaditya Thackeray, Shiv Sena heir apparent and newly elected MLA from Worli, has started holding meetings with the local Sena corporators and BMC officials in his constituency to make Worli a model Assembly constituency.

Aaditya along with Sena leaders held a review meeting with BMC officials in Worli on various development works. “Ongoing and proposed works were reviewed. Directions were given to BMC officials to fast track the work. He is interacting with various stakeholders and wants to make Worli A+ constituency,” said a Sena leader.

In the last week of September, while addressing a gathering of Shiv Sena activists in Worli, Aaditya had said that people from all over the world would come to see the development pattern in Worli in the next five years. During campaigning, Aaditya, in pamphlets distributed to people, had promised to create open and accessible sports infrastructure, upgrade pedestrian pathways, bring tourism to Koliwada with others.

The Sena leader said that development of about eight major roads, to be made in concrete and asphalt mastic, along with the improvement of nine traffic junctions and proposed road widening was also discussed.

Another Sena leader said the work for a water pipeline to Madraswadi in Worli was started.
