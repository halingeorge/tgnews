Player ratings as Ireland fall just short against Denmark

18 Nov 2019, 22:11 by James Cox

Ireland put in a great performance against Denmark but a 1-1 draw means they fall short of Euro 2020 qualification with the play-offs to come.

Here's how we rated Mick McCarthy's side.

Darren Randolph - 6

Randolph could do nothing about the goal and he put the ball on David McGoldrick's head on plenty of occasions.

Matt Doherty - 7

It would have been an eight but for the defensive lapse that allowed Martin Braithwaite in for the Danish goal.

It's a shame for Doherty because other than that he was superb.

It was his best showing in a green shirt as he showed what he can do going forward, including the equalising goal.

Shane Duffy - 8

Commanding in defence and he provided our biggest goal threat when McCarthy threw him up top as a Hail Mary.

John Egan - 6

Egan did little wrong before coming off injured at half-time.

Enda Stevens - 7

Stevens was impressive going forward like Doherty, James McClean tidied up after he made his only notable mistake.

Glenn Whelan - 7

Whelan has plenty of critics but he was solid again, distributed the ball well and even skinned Christian Eriksen with one silky touch.

Jeff Hendrick - 5

We were hoping for Euro 2016 Jeff Hendrick but he was anonymous again.

Alan Browne - 5

He was played with the hope of adding some attacking spark but besides a well hit volley in the first-half, the Preston man was too quiet.

Ended up playing as an auxiliary centre-back as Duffy went up front late on.

Conor Hourihane - 6

Hourihane's passing was spot on and he was often at the centre of Ireland's best play. It seemed odd that he came off for Callum Robinson, rather than the quieter Browne.

Sadly for the Aston Villa man, his performance will be remembered for spurning a glorious chance to put Ireland in front.

James McClean - 5

Full of heart and running as always. McClean did alright and made some important tackles but his play in the final third was lacking.

David McGoldrick - 8

McGoldrick won nearly every ball, even the ones hit towards him more in hope.

His link-up play was great despite the fact he was often painfully isolated.

If only that long-range strike was a few inches lower!
