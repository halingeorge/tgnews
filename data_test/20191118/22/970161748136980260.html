Mateo Kovacic reveals how 'perfect' Frank Lampard behaves around Chelsea stars

18 Nov 2019, 22:25

Mateo Kovacic insists Frank Lampard is the ‘perfect teacher’ for Chelsea’s young guns and has lifted the lid on how the manager behaves around the squad.

Chelsea’s all-time record goalscorer returned to Stamford Bridge in the summer following Maurizio Sarri’s departure and has already made a big impact in west London.

Under Lampard, Chelsea have climbed to third in the Premier League table, receiving plaudits for their exciting and attacking style of football.

Kovacic says Lampard is a ‘great guy’ who enjoys laughing and joking with the Chelsea squad and insists his team-mates are enjoying playing under the legendary midfielder.

‘It’s been nice, nice of course,’ Kovacic told Chelsea’s official website. ‘Now I see why he was such a great player.

‘His mentality is really strong. That’s what he wants to bring to us: to have a strong mentality, a winning mentality. He always won a lot of trophies.

‘He has a great connection with every player. He is a great guy, he likes to joke, he likes to laugh. He’s the perfect teacher for us.

‘We need to learn from him, and for now we are enjoying it, he is as well enjoying it, so it’s a nice connection.

‘Now the moment is good, when the tough moments come it will be a test for us and for him, but we need to keep training as we are doing.

‘We are working really hard. Four months he has been here, and every training was hard, and that’s important being at such a great club like Chelsea.’

Former Real Madrid man Kovacic, who turned his loan spell at Chelsea into a permanent transfer last summer, helped the Blues win the Europa League in May.

Kovavic produced a superb performance in the final against Arsenal but said: ‘I don’t think about what I have achieved or what I have to achieve; I think about what the team has to achieve.

‘That’s obviously trophies. Chelsea is a club that has always won trophies, needs to win trophies, and this year we need to go for everything. Then what happens, happens.’
