TITLE BATTLE

Kenny Miller believes outcome of Rangers vs Celtic Betfred Cup final won’t have bearing on who wins the league

18 Nov 2019, 22:30 by Colin Duncan

KENNY MILLER doesn’t believe the outcome of next month’s Betfred Cup final will have any bearing on who wins the league title.

Rangers and Celtic battle it out at Hampden on December 8, with the first silverware of the season up for grabs.

Celts are going for a tenth straight domestic triumph — while the Ibrox men are desperately trying to end an eight-year trophy drought.

The stakes couldn’t be higher, but Miller, who starred for both Old Firm clubs, reckons both teams are mentally strong enough to bounce back if the result goes against them.

Miller, still banging the goals in for Partick Thistle at 39, said: “The final could go either way, but regardless of the outcome for Rangers it won’t detract from where they are.

“Rangers are in a good place, as are Celtic with Neil Lennon doing a fantastic job. The Cup Final is a massive game for both sides, with Celtic trying to continue their dominance.

“But Steven Gerrard and Rangers will be desperate to end Celtic’s run and also get their first silverware in eight years. There’s a lot on the line for both teams and someone will lose, but it shouldn’t detract from how good both teams have been this season.

“I don’t see the outcome of the final having a bearing on the league title.

“Once it’s gone then you need to refocus, and there’s even Europa League games after it, so it’s a big week and it will immediately shift to the next match.

“I know from being involved in that environment if you lose then you want to bounce back straight away.

“Whatever manager loses will be asking for that — and what better way than on a European night."

This is the first Old Firm meeting at Hampden since April 2018, when the Hoops crushed their bitter rivals 4-0 in the Scottish Cup semi-final.

It was the game which sparked the infamous dressing room rammy, which prematurely brought an end to Miller’s third stint at Ibrox.

But the former Scotland star feels the current Gers side are unrecognisable to the shambles they were 18 months ago. Miller added: “I’m not surprised at the progress Rangers have made, because
Gerrard is a winner, and you see the level of staff he’s brought in.

“Any good manager’s team will be a reflection of them, and that’s translating into their performances. Right from the first league game, there seems to be a real willingness to find a way to win.

“Gerrard was a winner as a player, and his team are showing that mentality too.

“Rangers have improved massively. You maybe saw some signs of that in his first season in charge, but the real improvement has come in this campaign.

“You only need to look at the first league game, when they were away to Kilmarnock, and Killie equalised with about 15 minutes to go.

“It looked for all the world as though it was going to be another poor start to the season for Rangers.

“But they somehow found a way to win, and that’s what successful teams do.

“They didn’t play particularly well that day, and Killie were excellent in the second half and worthy of a draw.

“But title-winning teams dig deep in these situations and get the job done.

“Previously, that wasn’t the case, and I’m not just speaking about last season, but the years before also. Rangers weren’t consistently getting results until now.

"I’m getting a really different vibe from the whole team just now. They seem to have the bit between their teeth domestically, and they are also having a brilliant run in the Europa League against good teams.

“They have looked the best team in their group, and they can take the title race right down to the wire this time.”

Part of Gers’ resurgence this term has been down to the form of lethal hitman Alfredo Morelos.

The Colombian has already banged in 22 goals already, and has improved beyond all recognition since he shared a dressing room at Ibrox with Miller two years ago.

Miller said: “Morelos was very raw when he first arrived, and everyone could see that along with the qualities he had.

“The one thing that’s never been in question is his level of goals, and that’s improving with each year.

“When you look at the standard of player arriving at Ibrox in the last 18 months then it helps because you will always get opportunities.

“Morelos has improved all-round, and his game seems to be a bit cleaner.

“His link-up play has definitely improved, and he’s kept his edge without getting embroiled too many situations which is really important.”

KENNY MILLER was speaking at a William Hill Scottish Cup event. The third round of the competition has been been dedicated to Stonewalls Rainbow Laces Campaign.
