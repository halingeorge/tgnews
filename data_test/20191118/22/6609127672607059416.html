UP FOR FIGHT

Scotland boss Steve Clarke vows he’s tough enough to fight clubs of stay-away stars

18 Nov 2019, 22:54 by Robert Grieve

STEVE CLARKE vowed he’s tough enough to go to war with the clubs of Scotland’s stay-away stars.

But he fears invoking Fifa’s five-day rule would be a mistake.

English Premier League men Andy Robertson, Kieran Tierney and Ryan Fraser are missing for the Kazakhstan clash.

Clarke is satisfied they have genuine injuries — but would fight his corner if he felt clubs were leaning on their players to pull out.

Clarke said: “As you probably noticed from my short time at Kilmarnock, I don’t mind a fight if I have to fight, as long as it’s for the right reason.

“If I do have to have a fight, I’ll face it head on. I don’t shirk away from it.

“I don’t think I’m a soft character. If I need to be strong, I’ll be strong. But for the moment I don’t see any value in it.”

Clarke could use the Fifa rule book to stop players from playing for their clubs within five days of internationals.

But he added: “It becomes a case of not wanting to cut off your nose to spite your face.

“You have to be in a position where you can get your best players. You don’t want to cause upset between players, clubs and international bosses.

“I also have to take it at face value that every call-off is an injury call-off.

“The only one which was for personal reasons this time was Matt Phillips. Everyone else was flagged up as an injury.

“Of course you don’t want to harm the relationship between players and their clubs.

“It’s easy to say ‘invoke the five-day rule’ — but I’m not sure there are any winners when you do that. It puts the player in a difficult position."

Clarke would also love to see the SPFL help Scotland qualify for the Euros by imposing a fixtures shutdown the weekend before the first March play-off clash.

Chief Executive Rod Petrie appealed for that to happen at the weekend.

But as SunSport revealed league chiefs are struggling to find a way to do that without causing a fixture pile-up.

Clarke admits he’s now planning to go into the vital clashes without being able to get his squad together for a week.

He added: “I’m going to have three training sessions to get them back on the bike and ready to go.

“It’s not a lot but, on the back of what we’ve done previously, then I hope it will be enough.

“I can’t remember if I actually said I wanted a cancellation of fixtures — I don’t think I did.

“All we are asking for is for a little bit of common sense to come in on that weekend.

“The flip side of that is that I don’t want Rangers or Celtic to get knocked out of the Europa League before then.

“But if they are playing on the Thursday night the previous week, then there’s nothing we can do about that.

“So you have to respect all sides. It’s probably something to speak more about after Kazakhstan.

“It’s better to concentrate on the game on Tuesday night.”
