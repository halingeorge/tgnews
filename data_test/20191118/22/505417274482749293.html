'I Don't Think The Team Did Enough To Warrant A Place In The Finals'

18 Nov 2019, 22:53 by Michael Corry

Ireland are down but not out after a 1-1 draw with Denmark at the Aviva Stadium.

Mick McCarthy’s side went into this evening’s qualifier clash with the Danes, a team they’ve played six times in two years now, knowing that a win would seal a place at Euro 2020 next summer.

The Boys in Green put in a spirited performance but yet again lacked the cutting edge needed in front of goals. Denmark were poor but a lapse in concentration at the back allowed Martin Braithwaite to poke home before Ireland responded through Matt Doherty to set up a tense and thrilling finale.

However, Ireland couldn’t get that elusive second goal they needed on a night were most supporters will have left the stadium feeling down but proud of the performance.

Following the game, Liam Brady and Richie Sadlier both praised the Irish effort as having been one of the best performances in years.
