Ed Woodward optimistic of completing £185m double deal for Man Utd

18 Nov 2019, 22:25

Manchester United believe they can sign England sensation Jadon Sancho and prolific striker Erling Braut Haaland during the January transfer window, according to reports.

The Red Devils have endured a dismal start to the 2019-20 campaign and are ready to spend big in January to boost the club’s hopes of qualifying for next season’s Champions League.

Despite winning two of their last three games, United will resume the Premier League campaign after the international break sitting just seventh in the table, nine points adrift of the top four.

Ed Woodward brought three players – Harry Maguire, Aaron Wan-Bissaka and Daniel James – to Old Trafford last summer and is ready to back under-fire manager Ole Gunnar Solskjaer with another spending spree.

The Red Devils have expressed interest in signing Sancho and Haaland and Goal say there is optimism at the Theatre of Dreams that the highly-rated duo will be joining in January.

It is claimed Woodward has renewed hope of landing England international Sancho after the 19-year-old clashed with Borussia Dortmund manager Lucien Favre.

Sancho was fined and dropped from the first-team after returning late from international duty earlier in the season, and then subbed off in the first-half of Dortmund’s defeat to Bayern Munich two weeks ago.

The potential unease has given Manchester United fresh optimism of striking a deal with Dortmund, but any transfer would cost around £100million.

Woodward is also keen to bring Haaland to the Premier League, with the Norway international scoring a staggering 22 goals in 18 matches so far this season.

According to Goal, a deal worth £85m is being lined up and Manchester United would be willing to send Haaland back to Red Bull Salzburg for the remainder of the 2019-20 campaign after capturing his signature.

United’s pursuits of Sancho and Haaland may ensure the Manchester club shelve their interest in West Ham midfielder Declan Rice and Juventus forward Mario Mandzukic.

Solskjaer’s United, who beat Brighton before the international break, travel to Premier League new-boys Sheffield United on Sunday afternoon.
