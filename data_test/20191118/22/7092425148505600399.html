'Mixed emotions' for Mick McCarthy after Ireland draw

18 Nov 2019, 22:25 by James Cox

It was a case of mixed emotions for Mick McCarthy after Ireland's 1-1 draw with Denmark.

He revealed he was busy giving out to Matt Doherty about his mistake in the buildup to Denmark's goal and forgot to congratulate him.

"I was giving out to him about the goal and forgot to congratulate him about the goal scored!"

Speaking at his post-match press conference, McCarthy outlined how proud he was of his team.

As for the play-offs, he insisted Ireland can win if they replicate tonight's performance.

"Mixed emotions, I'm very proud of the lads though.

"I've got three months to prepare for that [play-offs], play as well as that and we can beat them.

"We played well, we competed well. If they leave everything on the pitch for me, the lads, I'll take that. That's the only thing I'm disappointed with [the goal conceded].

"We'd played well up until conceding."

McCarthy insisted he wasn't disappointed with any result in the campaign in particular.

"No, I'm here to talk about this performance and this game."

On Ireland's possession game, McCarthy said they "played exactly how I asked them to play. There'd be no team of mine who go out and just do what they want".
