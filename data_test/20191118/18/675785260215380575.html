Royal Variety Performance: Kate told kids 'not on school night' as they asked to attend

The cast of Mary Poppins, Lewis Capaldi, Mabel, and Robbie Williams are among the performers at the event tonight attended by Kate Middleton and Prince William

18 Nov 2019, 18:33 by Danya Bazaraa, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/danya-bazaraa/

Kate Middleton arrived at the Royal Variety Performance 2019 wearing a stunning Alexander McQueen gown along with Prince William.

The Duke and Duchess of Cambridge were greeted by two children, one handing over a posy of flowers, as they entered the London Palladium Theatre, where the show is held.

They went on to meet the cast of Mary Poppins who will be one of the performers. Kate, wearing an exquisite black floor-length gown with a scooped back, appeared to share a joke with the cast members as they shook hands.

She told the cast she'd had to tell George, Charlotte and Louis "not on a school night" when they'd asked to attend the show.

As the royal pair entered the theatre, William was seen placing an affectionate hand on Kate's back.

The annual event is in aid of the Royal Variety Charity, which the Queen is patron of, and this year is hosted by comedians Rob Beckett and Romesh Ranganathan who joked William and Kate were on a 'date night'.

 

Performers for the night include big names such Lewis Capaldi, Mabel, Robbie Williams, the cast of Come From Away, and a special collaboration by Emeli Sandé and Manchester’s Bee Vocal choir.

Petula Clark CBE, Zizi Strallen and Charlie Stemp, who play the Bird Lady, Mary Poppins and Bert respectively, chatted with the Duke and Duchess of Cambridge.

Prince William told them "I love Mary Poppins" and Kate added that their kids would love the show.

  

As she chatted with Petula Clark the Duchess said her children had been really excited about where she and William were going - particularly the dancing and singing - and asked if they could come but she had to tell them "not on a school night".

Kate was presented with a flowers picked from the garden of the Royal Variety Charity’s residential home in west London, Brinsworth House, when they first entered.

This year it is called 'Frosty Winter Garden' and was handed to Kate by Lydia Jones, 10.

Meanwhile a programme was presented to William by Oliver Hinton, eight.

The couple then greeted executive from the Royal Variety charity and ITV who will broadcast the show in early December.

Headline act Sir Rod Stewart was unfortunately forced to pull out at the last minute on ‘strict doctor’s orders’ due to a throat infection.

He tweeted that he was "absolutely devastated" to be unable to perform and described it as an "amazing show which I love being part of".

"I’m very disappointed that I can’t be there to give my support," he said.

The Royal Variety Performance takes place every year, either in London or in a theatre around the United Kingdom.

Money raised from the show helps hundreds of entertainers in the UK, who need assistance as a result of old age, ill-health or hard times.

Giles Cooper, Chairman, said: "It's with great pleasure that I announce that this year’s Royal Variety Performance will be held on Monday 18th November, 2019, at the London Palladium Theatre.

"We’re delighted to be returning to this iconic venue for what we hope will be both a fantastic fundraiser for the Royal Variety Charity and also a very enjoyable and memorable night of entertainment; in the presence of senior members of the Royal Family. The show will then be broadcast throughout the world over the Christmas period."

Last year, Prince Harry and Meghan Markle attended the show, which was hosted by Greg Davies. The Duchess of Sussex was pregnant at the time.

Kate has previously been to two Royal Variety Performance nights, in 2017 and in 2014.

The famous TV show Britain's Got Talent donates a proportion of the money raised from viewers' telephone votes to the Royal Variety Charity.

In return, the winner of the popular series appears on the show.

Lost Voice Guy was crowned the winner of Britain's Got Talent 2018.

The hilarious comedian, whose real name is Lee Ridley, took home the trophy after seeing off tough competition during the live final.

He picked up £250,000 and a spot at the Royal Variety Performance.
