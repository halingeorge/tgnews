Man dies after being hit by car while crossing the road

Road closures are currently in place around the scene of the crash

18 Nov 2019, 18:16 by Jenny Kirkham

A man has died after being hit by a car in a horrific lunchtime crash.

Emergency services were called to Dentons Green Lane in St Helens shortly after 12pm to reports of a crash between a pedestrian and a Renault Clio.

The man, who is believed to have been crossing the road at the time of the crash, was rushed to hospital with serious head injuries but has since died.

Road closures were put in place by police who have now confirmed that the victim was a 72-year-old man from St Helens.

A spokesperson for Merseyside Police said shortly after the crash: "We can confirm that emergency services are in St Helens following a serious road traffic collision this afternoon, Monday 18 November.

"At 12.15pm, we were called to Dentons Green Lane to reports of a collision between a Renault Clio and a pedestrian.

"The Renault Clio driver is helping with enquiries.

"A road closure is in place on Dentons Green Lane to the junction of Bishop Road.

"We would ask that road users avoid the area and find an alternative route."

At 6pm, officers confirmed that the man had died and his next of kin had been informed.

In a statement, the force said: "Following a road traffic collision in St Helens earlier today, the pedestrian has sadly been pronounced deceased. He is a 72-year-old man from St Helens.

"His next of kin have been made aware and are being supported by specialist officers.

"The road will be reopened shortly and we thank road users for their patience while the investigation is carried out.

"We are continuing to appeal to anyone who witnessed the incident or has any CCTV or dashcam footage which may assist to contact @MerPolCC or 101 quoting log 377."
