Reports: Ronaldo Wants Man Utd Star At Juve

18 Nov 2019, 18:30 by David Kappel

With Juventus still building a squad to win the UEFA Champions League, Cristiano Ronaldo has reportedly urged his club to buy a Manchester United superstar.

The 34-year-old took his international tally to 99 goals during Portugal's 2-0 win at Luxembourg on Sunday – is now just 10 strikes short of Ali Daei's world record – and will now return to his club.

He also downplayed talks of a rift with Juve coach Sarri, saying his recent early substitutions were based on him not being 100% fit.

His recent outburst, though, once again showed how influential he is at Juventus, and the latest reports suggest that he has advised the club to go for a particular transfer target.

According to Tuttosport, Ronaldo wants record Manchester United signing Paul Pogba to return to Turin.

The Frenchman, who was on Juve's books between 2012 and 2016 before returning to his boyhood club, has been linked with a move back to the Bianconeri over past few months.

Real Madrid are also said to be keen on the 26-year-old, but Juventus could perhaps offer United some of their unwanted stars as part of a potential deal.

Players such as Danilo, Emre Can, Sami Khedira, Mario Mandzukic and even Paulo Dybala could reportedly form part of a swap deal for Pogba.

The France midfielder has made 148 appearances for United so far, scoring 31 goals and assisting another 31 strikes, but has been sidelined with an ankle injury since September.
