DEBATE SNUB

SNP and Liberal Democrats lose legal battle to be included in tomorrow’s ITV General Election leaders’ debate

18 Nov 2019, 18:40 by Justin Bowie

THE SNP and Liberal Democrats have lost their legal battle to be included in ITV’s leaders’ General Election debate tomorrow.

Party leaders Nicola Sturgeon and Jo Swinson will not take part – meaning the debate will be a head-to-head between Boris Johnson and Jeremy Corbyn.

The SNP’s Westminster leader Ian Blackford said it was “simply not on” that his party had been excluded.

He said: “This is a democratic outrage.

“The SNP have won the last three Scottish elections, the last two Westminster elections – we are the party of government in Scotland.

“The last poll in Scotland showed us with a 20-point lead against our nearest challengers.”

First Minister Ms Sturgeon previously said her party's exclusion would be a “fundamental breach of broadcasters’ obligations” to reflect views from across the UK.

She had said: “It is also entirely possible that we will hold the balance of power in the House of Commons after this election - making it all the more important that our perspective is heard and indeed scrutinised.”

Lib Dem Education spokesperson Layla Moran tweeted: “The court ruled against us. But the fight must continue! It is outrageous that the #RemainVoice is missing from the ITV debate.

“It’s simply wrong of broadcasters to present a binary choice and pre-empt the decision of the people in a general election.”

Tomorrow’s head-to-head contest will be the first in a series of debates scheduled to take place in the run-up to the December vote.

ITV is set to host a multi-party debate on December 12.

And the BBC will also host a live head-to-head debate between Mr Johnson and Mr Corbyn on December 6.
