Ireland Name Team To Play Denmark In Decisive Euro 2020 Qualifier

18 Nov 2019, 18:24 by Robert Redmond

Mick McCarthy has announced the Republic of Ireland team to play Denmark.

Ireland take on the Danes for the sixth time in two years in a game where the objective could not be any clearer for McCarthy’s side – if they win, they will qualify automatically for Euro 2020. A draw or defeat will see Denmark go through to the finals, and Ireland will take their place in the Nations League playoff next March.

McCarthy has opted for Darren Randolph in goal. The Middlesbrough goalkeeper has returned to fitness after a spell on the sidelines recently.

Shane Duffy captains the side and starts in the centre of defence, alongside in-form John Egan. Enda Stevens is at left-back and Matt Doherty starts at right-back, with Seamus Coleman missing the game through suspension.

In midfield, Glenn Whelan, Conor Hourihane and Jeff Hendrick start. Alan Browne, who is a central midfielder by trade, is on the right of a front-three, James McClean on the left and David McGoldrick starts at centre-forward.
