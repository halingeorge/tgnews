Shane Williams urges Wales to cap rising number 10 star before England do

The Cardiff-born 24-year-old has been impressing all season

18 Nov 2019, 18:20 by Anthony Woolford

Shane Williams is urging new Wales coach Wayne Pivac to bring Callum Sheedy into his Six Nations set-up to keep the exciting Bristol Bears No.10 out of the clutches of rivals England and Ireland.

The Cardiff-born 24-year-old has been a pivotal figure in big-spending Bears' rise to the top of the Gallagher Premiership table this term.

His form from the kicking tee and overall game management skills means Irish international outside-half Ian Madigan has been playing second fiddle at Ashton Gate.

The former Millfield School captain has played 56 games in all for Bristol and scored 294 points in the process since his debut in the 2015-15 British and Irish Cup.

Despite playing for England in the summer against the Barbarians in a non-capped international, Sheedy remains eligible for Wales through birth and Ireland having an Irish father.

And Wales' record-breaking try-scorer believes Pivac should make a big play for putting Sheedy into his 2020 Six Nations squad - with Gareth Anscombe out for the season after knee surgery plus question marks surrounding Rhys Patchell following a shoulder operation.

Shane wrote in his Rugby Paper column: "I think he has to be looked at for the Six Nations. He has had an impressive start to the season.

"You would imagine barring injury Dan Biggar will be the first-choice fly-half for Wales in 2020 and that would be quite right as I thought he had an excellent World Cup.

"But with Gareth Anscombe a long-term absentee and Rhys Patchell a Six Nations doubt, I'd like to see Sheedy get a first international call up next year. He deserves one in my opinion.

"For starters, he is being picked at Bristol Bears - who top the Gallagher Premiership after four rounds - ahead of Ian Madigan which tells you everything about how Pat Lam rates him.

"I've watched him a few times this season and what I like about hias game is his control. I watched Bristol's comeback win at Exeter Chiefs.

"Sheedy might not have got the headlines, but he was excellent in that game, playing the full 80 minutes and helping his team turn the tables.

"If he can do that now, I'm excited about what he can achieve in the future at the next level up."

Sheedy, who came through the Cardiff Blues academy, has previously kept his international options having withdrawn from a Welsh Under-20s squad for a match with France that would have tied him to the red jersey.

"I think Wales would do well to get him into their international set-up as soon as possible because if he continues his upward curve, the other countries he could represent will surely come sniffing," added Shane.

"The Six Nations would be the perfect opportunity to give him a first taste of an international environment.

"No-one is expecting Sheedy to come in and start the tournament ahead of someone like Dan Biggar.

"As a small nation Wales have to maximise what they can from their playing pool and at 24, Sheedy has all the ability to become a Test player. All he needs first is an opportunity."
