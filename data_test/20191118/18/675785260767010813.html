'Secret double life' of man 'who suffocated ex and dumped her body in the desert'

John Matthew Chapman from Pennsylvania, who allegedly confessed to kidnapping Jaime Feden and leaving her to die, was reportedly leading a secret life away from his wife

18 Nov 2019, 18:27 by Milo Boyd, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/milo-boyd/

A man who kidnapped his girlfriend led a double-life in which he posed as the victim online and had a wife, it has been claimed.

John Matthew Chapman has been in custody after he allegedly kidnapped his former girlfriend Jaime Rae Feden, 33, in the US.

The Pennsylvania man is accused of bounding and suffocating her then dumping her body in the Nevada desert.

Feden, who was just over 4-ft tall, had been missing two months before a woman's remains were found in the desert.

It is alleged that on Friday he admitted to officers at Bethel Park Police Department that he killed Feden in Las Vegas by suffocating her.

Chapman is believed to have also confessed of his crime to Maureen Chapman - a woman he has been married to for just over a year.

Maureen had no idea that her husband had another partner or that he killed her, until Chapman phoned from the police department at around 6am, it is claimed.

She told WXPI: "He said he had murdered a woman. I was in disbelief."

The apparent confession has left Maureen questioning her relationship with the 39-year-old, who she now believes was leading a double life.

She said that her husband told her that he killed Feden "because I had to".

Chapman allegedly told his wife that he had to travel in Feden's truck for work after a trip to see his aunt and uncle.

"Oh, my God, it's sick. It's really sick," Maureen said.

"I just want the truth as to what happened. I feel I'm owed that."

The suspect's family told Channel 11 that he met Feden at a school for students with special needs in around 2009.

Police said Chapman would often stay at Feden's Bethel Park home.

The situation is confused by Chapman's Facebook page which lists him as a single man from Pennslyvania.

His relationship with Maureen is obscured by the fact she is listed as her sister on the website.

Beneath one photo he commented: "Lookin good sis."

According to police documents Chapman convinced Feden to drive into the desert for a photo shoot.

He allegedly bound her feet and hands with zip ties before tying her to a lamppost.

Chapman then bound her mouth and nose with duct tape, causing her to suffocate,  it is alleged.

After the death he used feden's phone to send messages to her family while pretending to be her, it has been claimed.

Nikki Lawrence, a close friend of Feden's, said the 33-year-old probably had no idea that Chapman was married.

She claimed Chapman mistreated her friend, used her for money and once arrived at a bar with her sporting two black eyes.

"The owner of the bar chased him down the street. After that he didn’t really come around any of her friends and family," she told Buzzfeed News.

"It makes me physically sick," Lawrence said, adding that she believes Chapman was posting photos and check-ins to Feden's Facebook account weeks after she was last seen by family and friends.

Feden's family, who had not seen her since September 15, reported her missing on Thursday.

During a welfare check of Feden's home that day, crime scene investigators recovered her phone and a backpack with plastic zip ties and duct tape inside, according to CBS 2.

Nevada officials are now working to identify a body they found on Oct. 5 that matches Feden's description.

She was born with a syndrome a series of birth defects that affect several parts of the body and caused her short height.

According to an anonymous listing on the National Missing and Unidentified Persons System a body found on October 5 matched Feden's description and bore two tattoos -  one read "Keith, Robin, Jason" and the other was a four-leaf clover on the right lower leg.

Lawrence told BuzzFeed News that Feden had the same tattoo in honour of her uncle, brother and mother, who died when she was young.

Chapman has been charged with kidnapping, obstructing administration of law, and criminal use of a communication facility.
