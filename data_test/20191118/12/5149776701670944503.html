These are the events taking place to mark 400 years since Pilgrims from Notts set sail on the Mayflower

The events mark 400 years since Pilgrims from North Nottinghamshire voyaged to America

18 Nov 2019, 12:51 by Gurjeet Nanrah

A series of Pilgrim-inspired events will take place across Nottinghamshire to help mark 400 years since people from the county sailed to America and helped form the nation.

Events which are planned include a Thanksgiving meal at schools, a Pilgrim-themed survival challenge, festivals celebrating local heritage, and community plays based on the Pilgrim story.

The Pilgrims' journey to the famous Mayflower ship can be traced from Yorkshire and then through north Nottinghamshire, taking in villages including Scrooby and Babworth, before continuing through the Midlands to their end destination.

They made their voyage in 1620 to flee religious persecution from King James and eventually set up a colony in Plymouth, Massachusetts.

Nottinghamshire County Council has announced the events to mark the 400th anniversary.

Councillor Kay Cutts, leader of Nottinghamshire County Council said: “We have a history and heritage to rival the very best the UK has to offer.

“The Pilgrim story, which started right here in Nottinghamshire, is one which needs to be showcased, so I’m delighted to announce our planned series of local educational, community and creative events to help commemorate this historic milestone.

“These 400th anniversary plans present a fantastic opportunity to show the rest of the country and the world what we have to offer to boost the county’s tourism credentials.

“These events will hopefully inspire more people to learn more about this extraordinary journey where people travelled across the world set up a new colony and establish principles for the foundation of freedom and modern democracy, led by William Brewster from Scrooby.”

The plans to celebrate the anniversary of the Pilgrims’ voyage were given the green light by county councillors at Nottinghamshire County Council’s policy committee on November 13.

Final details and venues of the events taking place next year are still to be confirmed.

Some of the events set to take place include:

  • The installation at a Pilgrim-themed garden at the National Civil War Centre, Newark
  • A thanksgiving school meal across the county in November 2020
  • Pilgrim survival challenge event for young people
  • A community play award to be offered to local theatre groups to produce a Pilgrim play
  • A festival-style event to help celebrate local food and drink and heritage
  • A community planting scheme involving 400 major oaks and also a special Pilgrim Apple Tree
  • Special pilgrim exhibitions, including one at the University of Nottingham, followed by a proposed county-wide tour.
  • Plans to install heritage lighting columns across Scrooby
  • A Pilgrim music festival and opera programme

Councillor Cutts added: “What happened here in Nottinghamshire hundreds of years ago had a far-reaching impact in Britain and beyond.

“To help younger generations learn more about how a boy from Nottinghamshire grew up to help to change the world, I’m delighted that a new book, created by council staff which uses digital technology, is also due to be made available to every school and library in Nottinghamshire throughout the county in the coming months."

The Pilgrims signed the Mayflower Compact in 1620 that became America’s first self-governing document and is widely believed to have laid the foundation for the US Constitution.

Other events taking place in the county ahead of the anniversary next year include the unveiling of a £750,000 gallery in Bassetlaw, which features authentic furniture and an augmented reality mirror where visitors can listen to William Brewster - a leader of the Pilgrims from Scrooby - recount his life in Nottinghamshire.
