Alfredo Morelos won't get Rangers more than £7million and he'll never score against Celtic - Hotline

Gary Ralston answered your calls and there was plenty of chat about Scotland, Steve Clarke, Alfredo Morelos and the Rangers Colts team.

18 Nov 2019, 12:34 by Sports Hotline, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/sports-hotline/

If you thought the Euro 2020 play-offs were confusing then it has nothing on the mindset of the Tartan Army.

Scotland recorded a rare victory on the road against Cyprus at the weekend ahead of the Hampden clash against Kazakhstan.

But the fans still weren’t entirely satisfied, claiming too much has been made over a victory that once was commonplace when Scotland qualified for major tournaments.

Kenny Rae, Wishaw said: “We’re going totally over the top about a performance that was nothing to write home about. I’ve been watching Scotland for 50 years and Steve Clarke isn’t the man for the job.

"We’re in the build-up to the play-offs and we’ve yet to bring a young player through. I’m a Motherwell fan but even I can see Declan Gallagher isn’t a Scotland class central defender.”

Steven Craig, Kirkcaldy said: “It has reached the stage we’ll cling to any positives, but the Scotland defence was unconvincing and it doesn’t bode well for the play-offs, whoever we end up facing.”

Andy Stewart, Kilwinning said: “I can’t agree with Michael Gannon’s praise of Declan Gallagher. He should be put in a wrestling ring, rather than a football pitch as his arms were all over the Cypriots. Clarke should also have played one of the other keepers to give them a chance. We know what David Marshall can do.”

George Walkingshaw, Glenrothes added: “I don’t know why there is so much fuss over a few players withdrawing from the national squad. However, it highlights a lack of depth and quality. Even if we don’t qualify for Euro 2020 we should not sack the manager again. We need some stability.”

Tony Brown, East Kilbride said: “Sure, let’s take the positives from the win against Cyprus, but it only underlined how ruinous so many of our performances have been in this campaign.

“Has it come down to talking about revenge against Kazakhstan? A side that were thumped by Russia and Belgium and lost to Cyprus, but still managed to put three goals past us. I guess the only way is up.”

However, Scotland fans are being urged to follow the path set by our Celtic cousins across the water.

Frank McFerran, Carrickfergus said: “I’ve read some shocking things by Scotland supporters aimed at their team and manager. Give the players a chance to gel. The Northern Ireland supporters always give their team a boost - and we only have a handful of Premier League players. We are always the 12th man.”

The Premiership may be on international break, but the off-the-field exploits of Rangers continue to dominate.

A broadsheet newspaper claimed a “blunder” by HMRC effectively put Rangers out of the game, a suggestion rebutted forcefully by the taxman.

Jim McLean, Rothesay said: “Whether it was £50million or £20m the bottom line is Rangers Football Club cheated and that’s why they were punished, end of story. We are fed up to the back teeth hearing about it.”

James Brown, Glasgow said: “Hotline caller John Bruce is typical of the Rangers support. He claims his club ‘could have paid the £20m comfortably’, so why didn’t they? He should dry his eyes.”

Gordon Ashey, Ayr said: “We're hearing the usual bluster from Rangers fans like Andy Adams and John Bruce, saying Rangers were robbed and Alfredo Morelos is a world beater. Wake up and smell the coffee. HMRC made no miscalculation and the £7m rated Morelos hasn’t scored against Celtic in 10 games and never will.”

James Robertson, Dennistoun said: “I notice the ex Rangers players who were recipients of EBTs never uttered a syllable on the back of the recent story about HMRC. Why not? For once, they have been rather conspicuous by their silence.”

Still, there was one call in support of the Ibrox club after their kids, under Graeme Murty, reached the semi-final of the Tunnock’s Caramel Wafer Challenge Cup.

Walter Eadie, Clydebank said: “It’s great to see Rangers emerge from the period of turmoil under Steven Gerrard and there is clearly good work going on behind the scenes in the youth department as well.

“Rangers’ Colts have gone further in this competition than any other youth side. The board can’t keep forking out money to buy new players. Hopefully we’ll see kids like Nathan Young-Coombes emerge in the next couple of years for a first team spot.”
