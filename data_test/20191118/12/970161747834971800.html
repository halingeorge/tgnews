Riverdale actress Lili Reinhart disgusted over 'make me slim' app

18 Nov 2019, 12:48

Riverdale actress Lili Reinhart has spoken out against photoshopping apps which encourage or enable people to change what their bodies look like.

She has called apps like BodyTune ‘hazardous to our health’.

The 23-year-old Hustlers star took to Instagram to voice her concerns on Sunday, 17 November, after stumbling across BodyTune in search for a photo re-sizing app.

The app markets itself as ‘Make me slim, skinny booth’.

Lili actually screenshotted the process you go through on the app to make your arms and waist skinnier, saying ‘this is not okay’.

She shared a lengthy discussion on her Instagram stories, saying ‘social media has become hazardous to our health’ and ‘people have unrealistic expectations of their bodies’ because of BodyTune and similar apps.

Lili continued: ‘Looking skinnier on Instagram is not worth the detrimental psychological effects that these photoshopping apps have given our generation.

‘My heart goes out to those people who feel like their bodies aren’t good enough.’

The star, who has struggled with body dysmorphia herself, urged fans to ‘not use these kinds of apps’.

She added: ‘If you photoshop your body, you are adding to this problem.’

It’s clear this topic is very close to Lili’s heart after she opened up recently about her experiences with her eating disorder to Glamour UK.

‘Even today, I see myself in the mirror and think, this doesn’t look the way the world tells me it should,’ she explained.

‘I don’t have a cinched, minuscule waist. I do have curves, I have cellulite, my arms aren’t stick thin.

‘This is my body and we’re told that it should fit certain proportions. There’s such a disgusting problem right now with people photoshopping their bodies.

‘Obviously, there’s a reason why people do it, they’re insecure, they feel like they’re not good enough, and that’s incredibly sad.’
