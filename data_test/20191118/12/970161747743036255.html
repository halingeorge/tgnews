Love Island’s Wes Nelson trolls Molly-Mae Hague’s unseasoned chicken

18 Nov 2019, 12:09

Love Island star Wes Nelson has become the latest to mock Molly-Mae Hague’s unseasoned chicken dinner and it’s literally too hot to handle.

Molly, 20, became the butt of jokes earlier this month when she cooked boyfriend Tommy Fury a roast dinner with chicken that fans described as ‘bland’ due to the lack of seasoning.

Well, Wes found the perfect way to troll the couple’s unsavoury taste buds while showing off the spice rack in his new kitchen.

In an Instagram story on Sunday, the former Dancing On Ice star joked: ‘Here is the kitchen set up at the moment, some nice little bits that we got today.

‘This in particular I imagine is Tommy and Molly’s worst nightmare,’ while swivelling the spice rack carousel around.

We’re guessing the lovebirds won’t be accepting an invitation for Sunday dinner at Wes’ place.

After the Molly’s dinner became meme of the week, Tommy defended his leading lady and explained: ‘Can I just say that the meal @mollymaehague made for me was a fighters meal clean cut food I don’t like seasoning or any of that btw.

‘It was the most caring thing anyone has ever done for me so thanks for the meal babe I love you come on guys where’s the love [sic].’

Wes, 21, gave fans a brief tour of the new apartment he has just moved into with girlfriend Arabella Chi, who coincidentally appeared in the same Love Island series with Tommy and Molly this summer.

Celebrating the new chapter in their relationship, Arabella wrote on Instagram: ‘Packing completed. 8 years in this flat.

‘Some good memories, some bad but beyond excited for this new chapter in our new home @wes.nelson.’

The model later added: ‘We are in baby.’

It makes sense that Wes and Arabella move into the same apartment as he admitted he had pretty much moved into her flat soon after they began dating in August.

‘I haven’t left Arabella’s at since the first time I stayed over right at the start of our romance. There was never a set moment when we decided I’d move in, it just happened,’ he told OK! Magazine.

‘We’ve just got our own place together now but it doesn’t feel like a big jump as it’s what we’re already used to.’
