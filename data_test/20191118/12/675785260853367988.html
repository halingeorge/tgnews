ASBO boy, 14, banned from Burger King is told he'll spend Christmas behind bars

Kenton Knight, of Swindon, Wiltshire, can now be named and shamed after flouting an order to stay away from Burger King and two shopping centres in the town

18 Nov 2019, 12:37 by Bradley Jolly, Tom Seaward, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/bradley-jolly/

A tearaway teenager will spend Christmas in jail after a reign of terror which saw him banned from a Burger King, two shopping centres and a community centre.

Kenton Knight, 14, can today be named and shamed after a furious judge said his anti-social behaviour had "affected the public detrimentally".

The baby-faced teen was previously given a court order banning him from the fast food outlet and shopping areas in Swindon, Wiltshire, but just weeks later Knight was spotted loitering on a street corner in the area when he should have been in school.

And on Friday, the youth was locked up for two months.

His tearful mum asked custody officers if she could give the teen a cuddle before he was taken down.

She said: "So he's going to be away for Christmas. He's only a boy."

But Michael New, chairman of the bench at Swindon Magistrates' Court, said: "Kenton has waived his right to anonymity by repeatedly committing anti-social behaviour in public and behaviours which affect the public detrimentally.

"Local people who have been affected need to be aware of him and what has happened today.

"You have many court orders against you - and you have shown complete disregard for all those court orders."

Knight is well known to the youth court, with his name appearing several times in the lists this year. These offences were linked to anti-social behaviour and covering his face with clothing.

But the injunction last month stated he should not be at the Burger King, shopping centres or community centre in Wiltshire unless he had an adult with him.

He was also told to be in school between the hours of 9am and 3pm and to stay away from a 17-year-old who cannot be named.

Describing the two-month sentence as a welcome result, PCSO Philip Day of Wiltshire Police said: "Knight's prolific and relentless behaviour in our communities for a significant amount of time has caused alarm and distress to many victims, young and old.

"The injunction served on Knight in October provided clear boundaries that he has clearly chosen to ignore, and as such he has now received today's sentence.

"We hope that Knight's time in detention will go some way in changing his offending behaviour when he returns back into the community."
