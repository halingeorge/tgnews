Ed Woodward outlines Man Utd's "clear vision" ahead of January transfer window

Manchester United chief Ed Woodward gave an update on their vision and direction ahead of the January transfer window as he addressed the New York Stock Exchange

18 Nov 2019, 12:22 by Rich Jones, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/rich-jones/

Manchester United executive vice-chairman Ed Woodward insists the club have a "clear vision" ahead of the January transfer window.

Woodward has been accused of lacking the football background to succeed in his role by many fans and club legends such as Gary Neville and Paul Scholes.

There have been reports the club are keen to bring in a director of football to support Ole Gunnar Solskjaer as he continues his rebuild.

But Woodward has now claimed they hae a plan for "football philosophy and recruitment" as he addressed the New York Stock Exchange to inform shareholders of results from quarter 1 of the financial year.

He praised the impact of investment in their academy amongst other areas with a number of young players making an impact in Solskjaer's side.

Woodward said: “We have a clear vision in terms of football philosophy and recruitment.

"The significant investments that we have made in recent years in areas such as transfers, recruitment infrastructure, analytics and academy are already beginning to bear fruit.

"We are very proud to be approaching our 4,000th game featuring an academy player and we are particularly optimistic regarding considerable young talent coming through.

"The ultimate goal is win trophies playing exciting football with a team that fuses academy graduates and world-class acquisitions"

Woodward also revealed that the club have cut their wage bill by 8.8%, down to £70.2milllion per year, stating the cut is mainly as a result of failing to qualify for the Champions League.

Their cash balance is down £107.2million as a result of summer signings, bringing in Daniel James, Harry Maguire and Aaron Wan-Bissaka in the summer.

They posted a first quarter overall revenue of £135.4m, up marginally by £0.4m year on year.
