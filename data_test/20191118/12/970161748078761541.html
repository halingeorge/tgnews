Vinnie Jones confirmed to stay on X Factor: Celebrity after trying to quit:

18 Nov 2019, 12:26

Vinnie Jones will be staying on X Factor: Celebrity despite trying to quit the spin-off series in scenes that aired at the weekend.

During Saturday’s show, the actor asked music mogul Simon Cowell if he could forfeit his place in the competition so that Glee star Kevin McHale could go through to the semi-finals, but the judge denied his plea.

‘Simon, I feel like I’ve had a fantastic time, I’ve really enjoyed it,’ Vinnie said at the time. ‘It’s been everything I’ve need since my wife’s passing.’

Pointing at Kevin, Vinnie added: ‘This guy is a real singer, I think he can win it. For his career, I’d like to step down so that he can go through.’

‘The last person I want to fall out with is Vinnie Jones,’ Simon replied. ‘But I think we’re about to because I’m going to ask you to stay in the competition.’

A show insider has since confirmed that Vinnie will return to the stage when the show returns later this week.

A TV source told Metro.co.uk: ‘As Vinnie said at the end of the show, Simon is the boss and he was happy to accept Simon’s decision.’

Kevin was the latest contestant to leave X Factor: Celebrity, meaning just six acts will be going head-to-head in the semi-final.

Simon, who announced on last weekend’s show that the final would feature a wild card act from among those who have been eliminated, still has three acts left in the competition.

Meanwhile, Nicole Scherzinger has two and Louis Walsh has only Megan McKenna.

The controversial ‘safe-seat’ was scrapped by Simon in the show’s most recent episode.

With one per judge, an act chosen for a safe seat would go automatically into the next week’s live show, thereby skipping the all-important public vote.

Addressing the change, Simon said: ‘I actually do read social media, and they said they hated [the safe seats] so they’ve gone.’

X Factor: Celebrity continues on Saturday at 8pm on ITV.
