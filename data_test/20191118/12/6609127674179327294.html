EARACHE

Fife teen, 19, has ear sewn back together with 11 stitches after savage car attack

18 Nov 2019, 12:06 by Alice Walker

A FIFE teenager needed 11 stitches to sew his ear back together after a savage car attack.

Cody Crossan, 19, was "jumped" by four yobs after heading to pick up a pal on Overton Road last night.

The bartender, from Kirkcaldy, said he was punched on the head and face 30 times in the unprovoked assault after the gang yanked open his car door.

But the top of his ear was completely ripped off when one of his attackers grabbed hold of his bar-style earring and "just pulled".

He rushed to the town's Victoria Hospital after the horror 10.30pm ordeal where medics reattached the top of his ear.

Cody said: “This lad grabbed hold of it and just pulled. He ripped the top of my ear right off.”

“There was blood pouring everywhere."

The 19-year-old managed to drive off after the attack, and called in cops.

Speaking to the Evening Telegraph, he added: “I drove straight to Victoria Hospital where doctors gave me 11 stitches to put my ear back together.”

A police spokesman said: “Enquiries are underway following the report of a serious assault in Kirkcaldy last night.

“Around 10.20pm a 19-year-old man was assaulted while in his car.

“He attended Victoria Hospital in Kirkcaldy where he was treated for a head injury."
