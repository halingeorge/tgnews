Talented Wallsend teenager speaks of pride as she'll represent North East in Japan dance competition

The talented young dancer is preparing for her biggest performance in a prestigious worldwide competition

18 Nov 2019, 12:00 by Nicole Goodwin

A Wallsend teenager was left speechless after finding out that she will represent the North East in a worldwide dance competition.

Urban cheer dancer Katelyn Patterson will travel to Japan on Tuesday to compete in the International Federation of Cheerleading World Championships.

This year will see the annual competition host the first urban cheer category in its 10 year history.

After beating hundreds of hopefuls, Katelyn, 16, said: “I can’t wait to compete in this competition. I’m so excited, I just wish it was happening now.”

Katelyn will be one of the youngest to compete in her category.

However, the graphic design student at Newcastle College explains that despite being fairly new to urban cheer, she feels confident and enjoys working under pressure.

The opportunity has required great dedication from Katelyn, who has had to balance her education with travelling to Manchester every month for training.

Katelyn’s mum, Julie Patterson, 48, said: “I’m bursting with pride. It’s hard to believe she is going to compete in such a big competition. I’m so unbelievably proud of her.”

Katelyn has been dancing at Smiths Academy Performing Arts School of Dance, at Byker Community Centre, for three years.

She joined the school when it had recently opened and explains that she couldn’t have asked to attend a better dance school.

Dance teacher Dominique Bias said: “This is the biggest thing Smiths Academy has been a part of and the most amazing opportunity for our students.

“I am extremely proud of Katelyn, Team UK couldn’t have picked a more kind and caring student. She is a born leader and a fantastic role model.

“I am honoured to have Katelyn in my dance school and she deserves all the recognition she can get as she has worked extremely hard.”
