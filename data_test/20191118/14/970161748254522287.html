Parents of sick baby targeted by 'Stop asking for money. Let the baby die' signs

18 Nov 2019, 14:30

The family of a sick infant are distraught after posters were erected in their hometown saying: ‘Stop asking for money. Let the baby die.’

Those billboards, targeting the family of four month-old RJ Ahlers in Toledo, Ohio, continue: ‘Its (sic) called Darwinism. Happy Holidays.’

RJ’s father KC says his family was targeted after he put up six signs at the local Franklin Park Mall last month detailing RJ’s condition and asking for help.

He was stunned to see the troll boards placed directly under his own appeal – with an arrow pointing up – and said: ‘I came out. I saw it. Immediately took the one down.

‘There were other ones that were up, but somebody, some other Good Samaritans kicked them down.’

The baby suffers from a debilitating brain condition called Agenesis of the Corpos Callosum.

The birth defects leaves sufferers without the corpus callosum – the band of white matter which connects the two hemispheres of the brain.

RJ also suffers a rare genetic condition called Mosaic trisomy 9. That sees sufferers’ entire ninth chromosome appear three times – rather than the usual two – in some cells of the body.

It leaves some sufferers with facial disfigurements, and intellectual disabilities. RJ also has a hole in his heart.

Doctors have warned RJ’s family that just half of babies with Mosaic trisomy 9 live to the age of two.

KC says the original posters he erected, as well as a superhero-themed fundraiser, netted around $4,000.

The cash was spent on an expensive genetic test, with RJ’s family vowing to continue fundraising to give the baby the best chance possible.

KC told WTVG: ‘We want to raise our son to not reciprocate hatred.

‘We want to battle hatred with love.

‘So, we obviously know somebody that did this is disturbed and we hope they get help.

‘But, we’re going to keep taking your signs down as we see them because this town doesn’t need hatred.

‘This town needs love.’

RJ’s family are now planning another fundraiser for next month, and have set up a GoFundMe page to raise further cash.
