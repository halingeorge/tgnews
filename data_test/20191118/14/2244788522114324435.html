Yahaya Bello reclaims Kogi as PDP refuses to sign result

18 Nov 2019, 14:37 by Lukman ABOLADE

THE Independent National Electoral Commission INEC has pronounced the candidate of the All Progressive Party, Yahaya Bello, winner of the November 16 Kogi gubernatorial election.

The Returning Officer for the state election and Vice-Chancellor of Ahmadu Bello University (ABU), Zaria, Ibrahim Umar, declared him as the winner at the INEC office in Lokoja, the Kogi State capital.
The collation was postponed to Monday because results of two local governments arrived Lokoja late.
Bello and Wada’s vote were close until the arrival of the Ibaji local government and Lokoja local government results arrived.
According to Umar, a total of 1,646,350 people registered for the election while 636,202 were accredited to vote.
Umar said the APC’s candidate; Bello defeated his closest opponent, Musa Wada of the People’s Democratic Party with victory from 12 local governments out of the 21 local governments of Kogi State.
Yahaya Bello polled a total vote of 406,222 votes to defeat Wada, who polled 189,704 votes at the election.
However, the Returning Officer of the PDP refused to sign the results of the election.
Natasha Akpoti, the candidate of the Social Democratic Party (SDP) was able to rack in a total vote of 9,482.
However, the PDP returning officer refused to sign the results of the election after Yahaya Bello was declared the winner.
Foreign observers and civil organisations said the election was marred by vote-buying, violence and voter’s intimidation.
