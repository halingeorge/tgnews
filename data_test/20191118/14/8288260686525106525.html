Boss ran family restaurant as 'secret nightclub'

Augustine Nsinga allowed DJs to blast out late-night music at Afro Coast Cuisine in King Street, Dudley

18 Nov 2019, 14:56 by Charlotte Regen, https://www.birminghammail.co.uk/authors/charlotte-regen/

A restaurant boss who turned the venue into a 'nightclub' with DJs playing after-hours music has been hauled before the courts.

Augustine Nsinga ran Afro Coast Cuisine in King Street, Dudley, where police who checked the premises  in January discovered unlicensed partying.

She admitted one offence of playing unlicensed music after permitted hours and two of supplying false information to obtain temporary licenses at Wolverhampton Magistrates' Court on November 14.

The court heard that Nsinga, who ran the family restaurant, was effectively operating a nightclub.

She applied for temporary licenses - apparently for family celebrations - but investigation of social media uncovered what was really going on.

This information was passed to the local authority licensing team and further investigation discovered the false application offences.

The court sentenced Nsinga to a fine of £300 on each offence, a victim surcharge of £30 and a contribution towards costs in the sum of £500.

Alan Lunt, deputy chief executive at Dudley Council, said: "This is a great example of the council, police and community members working together to ensure justice is served.

"It is a reminder to licence holders that they need to ensure they are fully compliant or they too could face the strong arm of the law. As a result of the prosecution the premises is now operating with a new licence with strict conditions attached."
