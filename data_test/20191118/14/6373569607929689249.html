Woman has wedding ring stolen during stay in Derriford Hospital A&E

Police are appealing for anyone who has information on the theft to come forward

18 Nov 2019, 14:52 by Molly Dowrick

A Plymouth woman has been left devastated after her wedding ring was stolen during her stay in A&E at Derriford Hospital.

Angela Riell had her wedding ring and engagement rings stolen while she was on one of the wards for an operation last month.

Angela's husband, Paul Riell, told Plymouth Live that Angela is "devastated" and the pair are shocked the rings were stolen.

Now, Devon and Cornwall Police has confirmed it is investigating the theft, and is asking anyone with information to contact them on 101.

Police believe the theft happened between 5.30pm Saturday, October 5 and 10am on Sunday, October 6.

Paul Riell told Plymouth Live that Angela's engagement ring is Egyptian and looks 'unusual,' with 'two small hearts - one with diamonds and the other is gold'.

The stolen wedding ring is a 'two-tone white gold and gold Celtic ring' with a Celtic pattern.

Devon and Cornwall Police told Plymouth Live: "Police are investigating a theft at Derriford Hospital in Plymouth.

"A wedding and engagement ring was stolen from a female patient while she was staying on one of the wards.

"The theft happened sometime between 5.30pm on Saturday, October 5 and 10am the next day.

"One ring is made of white gold, inset with diamonds and a gold heart, and the other is described as a two tone white gold and gold Celtic ring with a Celtic pattern within."

Anyone who has any information about the theft is asked to contact police via 101@dc.police.uk or by phoning 101, quoting crime reference number CR/103232/1.
