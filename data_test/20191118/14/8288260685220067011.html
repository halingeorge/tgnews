Gritters out from 7pm as temperatures set to plummet

Birmingham City Council’s fleet of 25 state-of-the-art lorries will start work tonight to ensure main routes are accessible for tomorrow’s commute.

18 Nov 2019, 14:11 by birminghammail.co.uk

Gritting lorries will be out in force across Birmingham as temperatures are set to plummet.

Forecasts suggest temperatures tonight, Monday, November 18,  will fall to below zero, creating the risk of frost and ice on roads and pavements on Tuesday.

The city council’s fleet of 25 lorries will start work at around 7pm to ensure the main routes and side routes are accessible for Tuesday’s commute.

The council tweeted: “With temperatures set to drop again tonight, our gritters will be out on the roads from 7pm, with footways being treated from 4am. Please take care if you’re out and about tonight.”

There is also the risk of fog and rain with temperatures set to peak at a cold six degrees, with spells of sunshine, before falling again in the evening.

The city has been hit by icy cold temperatures, winds and heavy rain with many areas hit by floods last Thursday.

Some of the worst affected parts included the Ford in Green Road, Hall Green, Queslett Road in Great Barr, Dog Kennel Lane in Halesowen and several routes in Solihull.

There was also widespread train chaos after lines became blocked with services cancelled. Drivers were also stuck in traffic gridlock in Birmingham City Centre on Thursday evening.

There were around six flood warnings issued for the region by the Environment Agency on Thursday.
