Live Like a Monarch in a Townhouse Featured in The Crown

18 Nov 2019, 14:47

Avid watchers of The Crown, take note: you can now experience the ultimate Royal stay, complete with corgis, jewels and a personal butler, at a London townhouse featured in the second series of the cult Netflix show.

To celebrate the third chapter of The Crown reaching our screens, Plum Guide (the premium short-term home rental company) is offering a truly decadent chance to get one step closer to living like the Royal family.

Large enough to accommodate up to 16 guests, the Earl of Fitzroy is a Grade I-listed Georgian townhouse in London’s Fitzrovia. It features a fully stocked library, Robert Adam fireplaces, an indoor pool, sauna and gym, as well as a spacious double garage.

Excitingly, you can stay in the wonderful townhouse throughout December, which would set the tone for a very merry Christmas indeed. Plus, you can curate your visit by booking an array of activities that are fit for a Royal…

These include:

  • A personal butler
  • A Royal etiquette workshop with the world-renowned coach William Hanson
  • Horse-and-carriage transportation
  • Afternoon tea in the company of corgis (the Queen’s favorite breed)
  • A Royal banquet dinner
  • A selection of regal jewels, fit for the Duchess of Sussex
  • A hamper filled with the Queen’s favorite brands

Afterwards, you will be able to enjoy an exclusive, private tour of the Stately Rooms at Buckingham Palace.

This unique opportunity is special even for the Plum Guide’s standards, which are so high that the brand only accepts the top one per cent of luxury homes. Bookings for this stay are available on a limited, first-come, first-served basis for a minimum of one week.

Starting at £50,000 (about $64,800), the price reflects the highly luxurious experience, but for Royal enthusiasts wanting to get as close as they can to the Queen’s lifestyle, it’s certainly a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity.

For more information and to book, visit www.plumguide.com.
