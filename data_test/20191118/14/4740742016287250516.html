'Liam Walsh or Lionel Messi' - Everyone's raving about Bristol City loanee's Coventry City wondergoal

The midfielder struck a sensational winner for the Sky Blues

18 Nov 2019, 14:47 by James Piercy

As the majority of Bristol City's squad took in the international break with some rest and recovery, the club's loan army were flying the flag for the club.

As Joe Morrell excelled on his competitive debut for Wales against Azerbaijan in Euro 2020 qualification, fellow midfielder Liam Walsh stepped up to add some stardust around St. Andrew's.

In the sky blue of Coventry City, the 22-year-old collected the ball on half-way in the 72nd minute before dancing and jigging his way through the Rochdale midfield and defence and finishing into the corner.

It was one of the great solo goals in recent memory and although social media may be a haven for hyperbole, echoed elements of Lionel Messi in its skill and execution.

Speaking after securing the 2-1 win to move Coventry into third in League One, Walsh was typically modest: "I couldn’t tell you what was going through my mind. I just kept my head down, kept running, I got towards the edge of the box and got my shot off and thankfully I scored.

"Everyone around me was shouting for the ball, but I thought I’d just have a go myself and luckily it went in."

But the Scouser didn't need to be over the top in describing what surely is the best goal of his career, as Bristol City teammates and fans did the rest, while Coventry manager Mark Robins labelled it as a work of "genius".
