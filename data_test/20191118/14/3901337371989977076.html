Mbhele To Stay Despite Reported Chiefs Interest?

18 Nov 2019, 14:30 by Soccer Laduma

Sifiso Mbhele could decide to stay with Highlands Park despite the reported interest from Kaizer Chiefs in his services. 

As reported in edition 1144 of Soccer Laduma, the defender is being monitored by Chiefs among other clubs, according to sources.

In fear of losing the player in January, as he will be eligible to sign a pre-contract with a club of his choice, the Lions of the North have initiated talks with him regarding a new contract.  

Baroka FC is another club believed to have been monitoring Mbhele for his progress in coach Owen da Gama’s team.

Contacted for comment, the player’s representative Ratshibvumo Mulovhedzi of Rush Hour Sports Management confirmed interest in the 31-year-old, as well as talks with the Lions of the North.  

“I am happy that the boy is attracting interest from clubs as that is a sign that he is a good player and doing well. Clubs are keen to sign him on a pre-contract but Highlands Park – the player’s current employers – want to keep him. The boy is valued at the club. He is well taken care of at the club and is playing regularly,” confirmed Mulovhedzi.
