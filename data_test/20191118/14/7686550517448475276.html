Mum opens a Paw Patrol chocolate and finds this maggot crawling around inside

It was in a pack of Paw Patrol chocolate coins she bought from Morrisons for her son

18 Nov 2019, 14:36 by Laura Clements

A mum was horrified to find a live squirming maggot in a chocolate treat she bought for her two-year-old son.

Evie Chisholm bought a net of Paw Patrol chocolate coins for Jacob from Morrisons in Port Talbot on November 16, but got a shock as she unwrapped the foil.

There, poking out of a small hole in the milk chocolate coin, was the glistening head of a maggot. The toddler, who loves Paw Patrol, had already polished off most of the coins before his mum made the gruesome discovery.

Miss Chisholm said she felt sick at the thought the toddler may have already swallowed a maggot. She said: "I bought the chocolates from Morrisons in Baglan on Saturday when I saw the maggot.

"I felt sick as my two-year-old son had already eaten half the pack and I was angry in case it made him ill as I didn't know if he had already eaten one."

Little Jacob was unimpressed when his mum told him he couldn't have any more of his favourite treat, which is made by Italian company Walcor.

Miss Chisholm, from Neath , checked the packaging for the best before date on the chocolates, which said September 2021.

Keeping the last single coin, she decided to call the Morrisons store straight away to warn them about other products which potentially had the same problems.

"I just wanted to make others aware of this," she added, warning others to keep their eyes peeled.

Morrisons were contacted but said as it was not their product they would not comment.

It is understood Walcor are to investigate the matter. The company has been approached for comment.
