Jack Ross targets Hibs stay as he aims to become longest serving boss since Alex McLeish

The 43-year-old is the club's 13th manager since Big Eck quit to join Rangers and is determined to make his mark at Easter Road.

18 Nov 2019, 14:57 by Mark McDougall, https://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/authors/mark-mcdougall/

It's been 18 years since Alex McLeish left Hibs to join Rangers and Jack Ross has become the 13th permanent manager since.

And now the former Sunderland boss wants to buck the trend and become the first Hibs boss to last three years since Big Eck.

There's been some successful managers in that time with Tony Mowbray, John Collins, Alan Stubbs and Neil Lennon the best of them but the majority have been major flops.

The likes of club legend Franck Sauzee, Colin Calderwood, Pat Fenlon, Terry Butcher and the recently axed Paul Heckingbottom are among them.

Ross had a similar situation at Sunderland when he became their sixth manager in three years as they tumbled down the leagues.

The 43-year-old stopped that run and was the longest serving boss since Steve Bruce left in 2011 and intends on doing similar at Hibs after signing a three-and-a-half-year deal at Easter Road.

He told Sky Sports: “That’s part of the attraction for me as well in the early discussions we had, is the want for me to be here for a number of years.

"I’m not here to use it as a stepping stone or a platform, I want to be a successful Hibs manager and I want to be here for the long term.

"I suppose my other answer for that is I went to a club where there was a high frequency in changes of managers and I think I was the longest serving since Steve Bruce so I did okay in that respect, I could have been there for longer but certainly the ambition here is to be here for a long period of time."

Heckingbottom's time in charge had a mixture of poor results and even worse football and Hibs have had a long history of playing some of the best in the country.

Ross was also criticised for his style at Sunderland and admits that while he hopes to get his side playing good football, results are more important.

He said: "That's the challenge, you have to win football matches as a manager and as a club. Supporters want that but equally there are demands at certain clubs as well for how you do it.

"It's not like I'm not familiar with this club I have been here often enough as an opposition player, coach and manager. I've seen some really good sides so it's up to me to make sure I can provide a side that can satisfy the demands of the supporters as well.

"From the initial conversations with Leeann (Dempster), Graeme (Mathie) and Ron (Gordon), I got a good feel from them and I think that's important. We then progressed to the more formal discussions in terms of what I could offer the club.

"There's a lot of good things in place at this club whether it be the training ground or stadium. There's a lot of good things behind the scenes that people don't see but are very good and very positive but I feel it is a good fit. Ultimately the proof will be in the pudding in terms of what I can produce on the pitch but it felt very natural from very early on."
