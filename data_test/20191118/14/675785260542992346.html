Richard Keogh shows off car crash knee scars as he trains after Derby sacking

Richard Keogh suffered a horrific knee injury after he was involved in a drink-driving car crash with former Derby County team-mates Tom Lawrence and Mason Bennett

18 Nov 2019, 14:19 by Alex Smith

The extent of the injury Richard Keogh suffered during the Derby County drink-drive smash has been shown for the first time.

Defender Keogh, 33, could be out of action for more than a year after he seriously damaged his anterior cruciate ligament during a night out with team-mates Tom Lawrence and Mason Bennett in September.

The Irishman, who was the Rams' club captain, was a unbelted passenger in a Range Rover which was being driven by Lawrence as it crashed into a lamp post.

Derby sacked Keogh last month following a disciplinary hearing into the episode which found him guilty of gross misconduct.

The PFA have vowed to stand by him, saying: "We will robustly defend our member because we feel he has been unfairly treated."

Keogh, who has been seen at Derby matches with crutches, spent the international break with his Ireland team-mates.

The centre-back travelled over to his homeland to continue his rehabilitation at the FAI headquarters.

Keogh was pictured on an exercise bike as he attempted to get strength back into his leg knee, while joking around with Shane Duffy and David McGoldrick.

Ireland face Denmark in a crunch Euro 2020 qualifier on Monday night - with Martin O'Neill's men needing to either win by two goals to confirm their place in next summer's tournament.

Lawrence and Bennett avoided jail terms and have not been dismissed by Derby for their part in the incident.

Meanwhile, Keogh hasn't publicly commented on the crash or his injury, his wife posted the message: “Wife to a professional scapegoat,” after he was let go.

Agent Cos Toffis, who represents Keogh, added to the Sun : "I think it could be quite a big can of worms.

"If this goes in Derby's favour there will be a lot of players in difficult positions at their football clubs.

"Older players with less market value who are on a lot of money, these are scenarios you have at every football club in the world.

"Should they start wrapping themselves up in cotton wool to avoid any situations like this?"
