Bilborough school bids farewell to dinner lady who has retired after 36 years

Janet Flowers began working at Jubilee Academy in 1983

18 Nov 2019, 14:50 by Phoebe Ram

An enthusiastic, dedicated and long-standing member of staff is due to retire after serving Jubilee Academy for the past 36 years.

Janet Flowers began working at the school, then known as Highwood Player Infant and Nursery School, in 1983 as an assistant cook and has seen the school evolve over her many years of service.

Speaking about her time with the school she said: “I have really enjoyed my time working here at Jubilee. I have seen lots of changes over the 36 years.

"The quality of the school dinners being one the biggest changes. I will miss all the staff and children.”

After dedicating herself to the school for 36 years, Janet, of Broxtowe, has become a well-known and familiar face, and the school says she will be greatly missed by both staff and pupils.

In an assembly, she was thanked by all staff and pupils and presented with a bunch of flowers.

Speaking about Janet’s time at the school, headteacher Nicola McIntyre, said, “We would just like to thank Janet for all her hard work and commitment to Jubilee L.E.A.D. Academy and the community over the past 36 years.

"We wish Janet a very happy retirement. She will be sorely missed.”
