Michael Schumacher film with never-before-seen footage of F1 legend's life 'postponed'

Very little information about Michael Schumacher has been released since his skiing accident in December 2013 - with the new film set to show unreleased footage

18 Nov 2019, 14:36 by Alex Smith

A feature film which was due to celebrate the life of F1 legend Michael Schumacher has been postponed.

The movie was announced in May and will tell the story of Schumacher, from his time as a talented go-karter in Germany all the way through to his devastating ski-accident.

His wife Corrina has given her full backing to the film, along with the couple's children Gina and Mick.

But B14 Film, who are creating the movie, have put back the release date - which will include never before seen footage.

A spokesman for production company B14 Film said: “Due to the very extensive material the directors and the production want to spend even more time to complete the film.”

Schumacher's manager Sabine Kehm had previously announced the film and explained what would be included.

She said: “The highlights of his Formula One career, his athletic highs and lows, will be as much a part of the story as previously unseen archive footage.

“It sheds light on the uncompromising driver, ambitious young man, pragmatic mechanic and confident team player as well as the family man.”

Schumacher has been guarded from the public gaze since the ski-accident in December 2013 which left him in a coma and with a serious brain injury - with very little information released.

Earlier this month, Willi Weber, his long-term manager, said he believed Corinna feared the truth about Schumacher’s condition entering the public domain.

But now Corinna has hit back at Weber in a rare interview, saying 50-year-old Schumacher was "in the very best of hands".

"He is in the best of hands right now and we are doing everything we can to help him," she said to She's Mercedes magazine.

"Try to understand that we follow Michael's desire to keep his health a secret.

"You can be sure that he is in the very best of hands and that we are doing everything humanly possible to help him.

"Please understand if we are following Michael's wishes and keeping such a sensitive subject as health, as it has always been, in privacy."
