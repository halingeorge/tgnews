Jermaine McGillvary apologises for Lions defeats as players back Wayne Bennett to stay

Great Britain slumped to four straight defeats to raise questions over veteran coach’s future

18 Nov 2019, 14:58 by Julie Stott

Jermaine McGillvary has apologised to fans after the Great Britain Lions completed a disastrous tour on a sour note.

Defeat against Papua New Guinea on Saturday condemned them to a four-match whitewash, following early defeats to Tonga and twice to New Zealand.

Winger McGillvary said: “All I can say is sorry and it is not good enough. We caused our own downfall.”

The three previous defeats have seen the Lions give themselves an uphill battle before trying to rally.

 

In Papua New Guinea they collapsed after dominating the first half when they should probably have led by more than 10-0, courtesy of tries from Blake Austin and Josh Hodgson.

They leaked a sloppy try on the stroke of half-time and then caved in alarmingly in the second half.

McGillvary said: “The tour as a whole has not been great and it is kind of embarrassing talking about it.

“It is not good knowing we are going home to face a backlash but deservedly so because we haven’t been good enough.”

Britain were beaten 14-6 by Tonga before losing twice to New Zealand, 12-8 and then 23-8, ahead of this setback on Saturday.

The four defeats, and the dull manner in which the Lions have played at times, have raised anger over Wayne Bennett’s leadership.

The 69-year-old Australian’s contract as England and GB boss is now at an end but he wants to be reappointed as England coach for the next two years.

It is an appointment that both McGillvary and Joe Philbin support, despite the protests from some quarters.

McGillvary said: “If Wayne is getting criticism we all get it. We are in it together, whether it’s Wayne, myself or the rest of the team and the staff.”

Philbin, one of the few to earn plaudits on the tour, said: “I disagree with the criticism to be honest.

“We are a new group and we have lost some monumental players in Sean O’Loughlin and Sam Burgess.

“The tour looks like it’s all doom and gloom but it is kind of a rebirth and we will definitely ask ourselves some honest questions.

“We will go back to our clubs feeling sore and sorry for ourselves but we know what we need to do when England play Australia next year.

“I completely disagree with people who say Wayne shouldn’t stay on. He has been fantastic for me and the rest of the group.

“Fans back home do not see what goes on behind closed doors and the reasons behind the decisions that are made.

“People make their own assumptions but we knew we’ve got a special group here and we’ve got a great leader in Wayne.”
