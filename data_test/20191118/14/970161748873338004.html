GMB Christmas ad to rival John Lewis features baby Piers and Susanna

18 Nov 2019, 14:48

Good Morning Britain has launched its annual project to beat the John Lewis Christmas ad on a budget – and racked up just £80 on a new clip to tug on your heartstrings.

Well, it worked – and live on air, Charlotte Hawkins was mocked as she burst into tears.

As the sad piano of Ellie Gouding’s I Know You Care played in the background, the clip featured a young Piers Morgan looking sad as no one would play with him.

Thankfully, young Susanna becomes his friend, and while they mess about with each other, they help each other out.

Piers then announces that he’s heading to America without her, leaving Susanna sad and wanting her best friend back as she heads to Salford.

Putting him on her wish list for Santa, a Christmas miracle is performed and he reappears beside her, right in front of television centre.

They then walk off hand-in-hand, because Christmas is a time to be emotional as hell.

While the actual Piers and Susanna were unmoved by the cute scenes, newsreader Charlotte was left in tears – which obviously was made fun of immediately.

‘You watched that utter load of cheese and you’re crying?!’ Piers declared, astounded. ‘This is why John Lewis do so well, because people like you fall for this!’

‘Are you suddenly maternal?!’ he asked as the gang laughed.

‘Just coz you’ve got a heart like a swinging brick!,’ Richard Arnold added, defending his pal.

Seems you can’t win everyone!

‘We wondered whether you could create an advert that had as much sentimentality, meaning and emotion, as much as that, but for a much smaller budget,’ Susanna said, revealing only £80 was forked out.

Turns out all you need it a cute story and a sad song, who knew?!

The ad was made as a direct retaliation for the annual tearjerker that is the John Lewis Christmas ad, which cost around £7million to make, according to reports.

This year features Excitable Edgar, a young dragon who bursts out flames when he’s excited, and his best friend who helps him when he keeps having accidents that ruin Christmas.

Good Morning Britain airs weekdays from 6am on ITV.
