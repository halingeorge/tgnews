Plymouth brothers charged after expensive cars stolen in string of burglaries

Jordan, aged 20, and 22-year-old Jacob Lemm allegedly plotted to take Porsche and Aston Martin

18 Nov 2019, 14:51 by Stuart Abel

Two brothers have faced a judge after a string of burglaries at ten homes across the South West where high-value cars were stolen.

Jordan, aged 20, and 22-year-old Jacob Lemm, appeared in custody at Plymouth Crown Court charged with conspiracy to burgle.

They allegedly plotted to raid four properties in Plymouth, one in Chudleigh, one in South Brent, one in Cornwood, one in Landulph and two in Weymouth.

The pair allegedly took keys which they used to take a number of expensive vehicles, including a Porsche 911 and an Aston Martin Vantage.

The Lemms, both of Constantine Street, Greenbank, were not asked to enter pleas as they appeared from Exeter Prison over the videolink.

Jason Beal, for the Crown Prosecution Service, said police and prosecutors needed more time to prepare the complex case.

Judge Paul Darlow adjourned the hearing until January 16, when the pair are expected to enter pleas.

He granted both men conditional bail.
