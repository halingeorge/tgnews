Iamgold (TSE:IMG) Given New C$7.25 Price Target at National Bank Financial

18 Nov 2019, 14:28 by Scott Moore

Iamgold (TSE:IMG) (NYSE:IAG) had its target price reduced by equities research analysts at National Bank Financial from C$8.00 to C$7.25 in a research report issued to clients and investors on Monday, BayStreet.CA reports. The firm presently has an “outperform” rating on the mining company’s stock. National Bank Financial’s price target suggests a potential upside of 51.99% from the stock’s current price.

Separately, Cormark cut shares of Iamgold from a “buy” rating to a “market perform” rating and cut their price target for the stock from C$7.00 to C$5.50 in a report on Friday, August 9th. Three research analysts have rated the stock with a hold rating and five have assigned a buy rating to the stock. Iamgold has an average rating of “Buy” and an average price target of C$6.24.

Iamgold stock traded up C$0.08 during midday trading on Monday, hitting C$4.77. 615,816 shares of the stock were exchanged, compared to its average volume of 1,860,474. Iamgold has a 52-week low of C$3.08 and a 52-week high of C$5.55. The stock has a market capitalization of $2.19 billion and a price-to-earnings ratio of -23.85. The business’s 50-day moving average price is C$4.71 and its 200-day moving average price is C$4.40. The company has a quick ratio of 2.94, a current ratio of 4.07 and a debt-to-equity ratio of 14.76.

About Iamgold

IAMGOLD Corporation explores for, develops, and operates gold mining properties in North and South America, and West Africa. The company owns interests in the Rosebel mine located in Suriname, South America; the Essakane mine in Burkina Faso, West Africa; the Westwood mine in Quebec, Canada; and the Sadiola mine located in Mali, West Africa.
