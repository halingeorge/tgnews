Now de-cluttering queen Marie Kondo wants to sell you stuff

18 Nov 2019, 14:40 by Rob Bailey-Millado

First, she came for your clutter — now she wants to sell you housewares.

Marie Kondo — who became a pop culture sensation with a series of books, YouTube tutorials and her hit “Tidying Up” Netflix series — launched an online shop Monday where you can stock up on candles, linen robes, kitchen and bath wares, home décor and, yes, books.

That’s a far cry from what some critics suggest was a long con — aka “Kon Mari” — which featured the 35-year-old Japanese decluttering expert tweely prodding people into piling up their belongings and tossing out anything that doesn’t “spark joy.”

Despite claims that she co-opted a concept created by celebrity organizer Linda Koopersmith, Kondo has sold more than 11 million copies of her book “The Life-Changing Magic of Tidying Up: The Japanese Art of Decluttering and Organizing.”

Now, her new website’s homepage hypes, “Our goal is to help more people live a life that sparks joy, and we are committed to offering the simplest, most effective tools and services to help you get there.”

Items range from the low-end — a minimalist $8 ceramic chopstick rest — to full-on luxury goods: a $275 brass kitchen utensil caddy by Japanese designer Oji Masanori.

Of course, as Kondo told the Wall Street Journal, she would never suggest “over-purchasing anything.

“What’s most important to me is that you surround yourself with items that spark joy,” she said. “If the bowl that you’re using currently sparks joy for you, I don’t encourage replacing it at all.”
