Accused McChord Mart killer ordered held on $2,050,000 bail

18 Nov 2019, 14:16 by KOMO News Staff

TACOMA, Wash. - The man suspected of stabbing a Lakewood convenience store owner to death during a robbery has been ordered held at the Pierce County Jail on $2,050,000 bail.

The suspect, Marcus L. Williams, 24, pleaded not guilty to the murder of In Cha Choe, owner of the McChord Mart, during a court hearing Monday.

Williams was arrested in Tacoma last week, after a month on the run, when someone tipped off police to his whereabouts and officers took him into custody.

Williams' bail was initially set at $2 million, but an extra $50,000 was added to that amount after he admitted bringing marijuana into his jail cell. Corrections officers searched the cell more thoroughly and located multiple rolled joints of marijuana, a match, a striker and more loose lead marijuana, officials said.
