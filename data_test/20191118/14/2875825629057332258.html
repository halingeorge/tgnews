England boss Gareth Southgate told he has 'missed the boat' with Jack Grealish omission

Aston Villa midfielder Grealish continues to be overlooked by England boss Southgate - and Tim Sherwood believes that is a mistake

18 Nov 2019, 14:33 by Irishmirror.ie

England have “missed the boat” with Jack Grealish according to Tim Sherwood who has compared the Aston Villa ace to Paul Gascoigne.

Former Villa boss Sherwood gave Grealish his full Premier League debut in April 2015 as the starlet flourished under him and helped them to the FA Cup final.

The attacking midfielder, 24, has since blossomed into Villa's captain and has impressed in the Premier League this term with two goals and three assists after inspiring them to promotion.

Grealish is the most fouled player in the top-flight ahead of Wilfried Zaha after also being singled out in the Championship.

But the uncapped former England U21 ace continues to be overlooked by Three Lions boss Gareth Southgate.

Sherwood reckons it is a mistake ahead of next summer's European Championships after comparing his ability to England legend Gazza.

Sherwood, in charge at Villa Park from February 2015-October 2015, told TalkSport: “You have two friendlies in March but I think it may be a bit late for Jack.

“I think we've missed a trick because this kid is special - 100% they missed the boat on him. This kid, the ball's his friend, he's something different.

“Jack takes the ball to the opposition, he goes right at the heart of people, he dribbles like Paul Gascoigne.

“He's got a long way to go before he's anywhere near Paul Gascoigne but he can see the play.

“He's the most fouled player in any league he ever plays in because he creates a melee but he sees the pass and releases it at the right time.

“Yes, he's got to add more goals to his game, he won't ever score as many as Mason Mount but what he does is release players with freedom because he creates such a crowd around him.

"He's quicker with the ball than he is without it and that's a mindset. If we're out of possession, yes he needs to get himself back around.

“But if he's [Gareth Southgate's] going to play a six with two eights I would definitely be playing him. Id' be playing Mount and Grealish either side of Harry Winks.”
