'Four or five times' - The cash Nottingham Forest offered midfielder now being linked with Manchester United

It is two years since the Reds attempted to bring McGinn to the City Ground from Hibs

18 Nov 2019, 14:02 by James Pallatt

Nottingham Forest were offering John McGinn "four or five times his money" when they tried to sign him from Hibs, according to the Scottish Premiership club.

The Scotland international is now playing in the Premier League for Aston Villa, following a move to Villa Park in August 2018.

And his impressive performances in the top flight has led to him being linked with Manchester United.

It is two years since the Reds attempted to bring him to the City Ground from Hibs, but they ultimately failed to agree a fee for the attacking midfielder.

And Hibs' sporting director Graeme Mathie has recalled McGinn's reaction to a move to Forest breaking down.

"I'll never forget the text he sent back," Mathie told the Daily Record.

"He came back and said: 'Look, I get it, this is football, it's no problem at all. I'm loving being at Hibs and I want to have the best season of my career and hopefully get a move to a good club'.

"Let's be honest, while John was committed to Hibs, somebody was potentially offering to give him four or five times his money to take him to the English Championship and us, as a club, had said no to that.

"I thought that was an unbelievable thing for a young man to say."
