France bridge collapse: Girl, 15, killed and several missing near Toulouse

The bridge connects the towns of Mirepoix-sur-Tarn and Bessieres, north of Toulouse, and a desperate search has begun to find the missing

18 Nov 2019, 10:01 by Tom Davidson, Peter Allen, https://www.mirror.co.uk/authors/tom-davidson/

A teenage girl has been killed and four other people are missing after a suspension bridge collapsed into freezing water in France.

The 15-year-old had been in a car with her mother when the 150-metre-long bridge, over the River Tarn, gave way and plunged into the water.

The mum was rescued from the sinking car and screamed that her daughter was still inside, said witness Eric Bonnin, who owns a restaurant overlooking the bridge, around 20 miles north of Toulouse.

The structure connects the towns of Mirepoix-sur-Tarn and Bessieres.

Witnesses said it sounded like an "explosion" or a "thunderclap" when at least one truck and two cars plunged into the river at about 8am local time.

The teen's mother was pulled alive from the car by rescuers, some of whom were badly injured as they saved four people.

Her daughter's body was recovered from the river, which is 65ft deep at the bridge, and two people were said to be in a life-threatening condition.

A lorry had just driven onto the bridge, which is in a popular tourist area, when it collapsed.

Mr Bonnin said: "I’m in deep shock, it's horrible to think about it.

"I’d just opened the kitchen door, when I heard a huge noise, like an explosion.

"I thought a plane had broken the sound barrier. I looked out the window and shouted 'Oh no, the bridge has broken'."

A witness, who lives near the bridge, told BFMTV: "We heard like a huge thunderclap."

The resident added: "I went outside and saw the bridge collapse with a car half out of the water." 

By Monday afternoon, a car bonnet could be seen sticking out of the water as helicopters circled overhead and emergency workers in inflatable boats searched for survivors.

A number of witnesses were rushed to hospital after diving into the river to try to save the victims and getting into difficulties.

More than 60 emergency workers are at the scene this morning and an investigation has been launched, according to the head of the Haute-Garonne department Georges Meric.

Mr Oget said articulated lorries were not allowed onto the bridge but that some had nonetheless been seen crossing over in the past.

He said he had no information on whether the fallen truck exceeded the allowed weight.

In September last year France’s Ministry of Transport released a report that concluded that out of France’s 164 largest bridges, 23 were in need of urgent repair.

But Mr Oget  said the suspension bridge at Mirepoix-sur-Tarn was not on the list of bridges in need of urgent repair and had not been not under close watch.
