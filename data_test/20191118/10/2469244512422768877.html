Tiverton Film Club screen award winning Lebanese movie Capernaum

The show follows on from the Autumn programme

18 Nov 2019, 10:45 by Lewis Clarke

An award-winning Lebanese movie is the next to feature at the Tiverton Film Club.

The show follows on from the Autumn programme’s Danish thriller, The Guilty in September and French drama The House by the Sea in October.

The next meeting is on Wednesday, November 20, where they will be showing Capernaum. The film won the Cannes Grand Jury Prize in 2018.

It is the story of a clever and gutsy 12-year-old boy surviving on his wits in the slums of Beirut.

Ian Harkness from the club said: “The passion and compassion of the story illustrate what poverty and deprivation - and love - means in this troubled part of the world.

“In summary, it is a tough and funny tale of a little boy lost in an unjust world. It is a very relevant film about a very crowded part of the Middle East, and the young star gives a wonderful performance.

 “As ever, the film starts at 7:00 pm in the Petroc Theatre. Doors open 6:30 pm and guests are welcome at £5 a head. Our next film will be ‘Faces Places’ a French road movie on December 11.”
