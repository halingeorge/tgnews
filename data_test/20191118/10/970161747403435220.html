Ben Affleck has finally joined the #ReleaseTheSnyderCut movement

18 Nov 2019, 10:42

Hollywood superstar Ben Affleck and the Justice League have finally united in the Snyder Cut movement.

Affleck tweeted a simple #ReleaseTheSnyderCut yesterday to let fans know he wants Zack Snyder’s cut of the Justice League film to be out for the public to see. The 47-year-old posted just hours after Wonder Woman star Gal Gadot also tweeted in support of the Snyder Cut.

This is all a bit confusing, but basically the Snyder Cut is referring to another version of Justice League that was shot but not released.

The film came out on 17 November 2017 and Affleck starred alongside Gal Gadot, Jason Mamoa as Aquaman and Henry Cavill, playing superman, among others.

It was sort of supposed to be DC’s version of the Avengers, bringing popular superheroes together into one film.

Yet Justice League did nowhere near as well as Avengers at the box office. It only brought in $657.9 million (£507.7 million) worldwide against a budget of $300 million (£231.5 million) and reviews released at the time were pretty negative.

The movie had a last minute change, replacing director Zack Snyder with Avengers writer and director Joss Whedon in post-production.
