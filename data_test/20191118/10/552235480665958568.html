Saints boss Jim Goodwin eyeing up squad reinforcements in January

The St Mirren gaffer hopes the club can strengthen their side when the transfer window swings open again.

18 Nov 2019, 10:25 by Ben Ramage

Saints manager Jim Goodwin says the club are already looking at reinforcements in the January transfer window.

While he is pleased with the squad he has assembled, he believes it’s only right that the club are always looking to improve the side.

“We’re always looking for reinforcements, absolutely,” he told Express Sport.

“We’d like to strengthen again in January if we can. I think we need to.

“I’m not going to say in what areas at this time but we’re similar to every other team out there.

“We’ll try and get people in that can improve the squad, which is the same in every window.

"If there’s players available that we think are good enough, who can make us better then we’ll certainly look to try and do something.”

One player who won’t be signing up at the Simple Digital Arena is Ross Wallace.

While some media outlets reported he was undertaking a trial with St Mirren , Goodwin revealed he was simply using the training facilities to keep fit.

He added: “Ross is a friend and asked if he could come in and do a bit of training.

“We were short of bodies at the time so that was more to facilitate Ross.

"It wasn’t a trial and he’s not back in with us now.”
