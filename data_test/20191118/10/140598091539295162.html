Entire weekend schedule in Spain's top women's football league postponed after players strike

18 Nov 2019, 10:51 by Molly McElwee

All eight of Spain's top division women's football matches were postponed at the weekend as players went ahead with planned strike action following a wage dispute with the league's clubs. 

Two games on Saturday and six on Sunday, including Barcelona's tie at Real Sociedad and Atletico Madrid's away fixture at Valencia, were affected as all league players barring one - Maddi Torre of Real Sociedad - refused to take to the field. It was as a result of failed negotiations over collective bargaining agreement for players, who are looking to increase their wages and improve their workers' rights.  

A video supporting the strike, including Athletic Bilbao's Ainhoa Tirapu and fellow Spain international Silvia Meseguer of Atletico Madrid, was promoted on social media by the Association of Spanish Footballers at the weekend, and was viewed over a million times.

"We are the same as you, we are workers," Tirapu said, speaking directly to camera. "It's the time, the time to make history."

"This fight is for those that came before us, those currently playing and those who will one day be in our place," Jade Boho of EDF Logrono continued. 

"Today female footballers say 'enough'." Mariasun Quinones, Real Sociedad goalkeeper, said.
