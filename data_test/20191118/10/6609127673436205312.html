TOME THUMB

Fife gran who lost limbs, job and even boyfriend after sepsis wrote autobiography with thumbs

18 Nov 2019, 10:12 by Ruth Warrader

A SEPSIS amputee has finished her autobiography after typing 80,000 words using her stump thumb.

Marguerite Henderson, 56, has taken six months to document her blood poisoning and limb loss hell after sitting hunched at her laptop for 20 minutes at time.

The brave Fife gran also lost both legs to the deadly infection sparked by a paper cut.

She is now searching for a publisher so she can share her survival story Sepsis: Raw and Real with the world.

Margeurite, of Crosshill, said: “I had to be brutally honest with myself and at times I had to stop because looking back was too upsetting and painful.

“I’ve been through so much and found myself fighting to be the person I was, instead of coming to terms who I am today.

I know there are people who will want to read what I’ve been through.

“I may have lost parts of my body, my job and even my boyfriend but the experience brought me so much closer to my family.”

We told how the seamstress spent a week in an induced coma having almost died from the poisoned finger in February 2018.

Surgeons removed her limbs amid fears of a relapse.
