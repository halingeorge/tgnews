Huge fire destroys £15,000,000 worth of super yachts

18 Nov 2019, 10:44

Two yachts worth more than £15,000,000 have been incinerated in a blaze that took almost a whole weekend to put out.

It took over 60 firefighters and three fire boats to get the fire under control after it started early on Saturday in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, US.

Crews were still on the scene on Sunday afternoon extinguishing hot spots and taking down scaffolding which was now collapsing around the cremated boats.

Arlene Meyer and her husband heard ‘three big explosions’ and called emergency services at around 4:30am on Saturday.

She said: ‘I was just so amazed that it went up in flames in one day.’

Local firefighters believe the fire began on one yacht and was spread to the other by winds, leaving both wrecked.

They said the 160-foot Trinity yacht named Lohengrin was worth between £9 million and £12 million ($7-$16 million) while the 110-foot Christianson vessel named Reflections was worth around £4 million to £6 million ($6-£8 million).

Officials believe one of the yachts was being renovated and are investigating whether that had anything to do with the fire.

However the cause is still unknown and firefighters cannot get close enough to investigate thoroughly due to the risk of collapsing scaffolds.

There were no injuries as a result of the blaze.
