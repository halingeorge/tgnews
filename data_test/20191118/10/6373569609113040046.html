Why striker Billy Clarke missed Plymouth Argyle's away game against Forest Green Rovers

Ryan Lowe has also talked about the absence of Will Aimson and Conor Grant from the squad

18 Nov 2019, 10:49 by Chris Errington

Ryan Lowe does not believe the injury to Plymouth Argyle striker Billy Clarke which ruled him out of the 1-0 away League Two win against Forest Green Rovers is a serious one.

Clarke did not travel to Gloucestershire with the rest of the Pilgrims’ squad for their clash against table-topping Forest Green.

The 31-year-old had tweaked his groin during training on Friday, Lowe revealed.

Clarke has made one start and four substitute appearances for the Pilgrims since signing as a free agent last month.

Defender Will Aimson and Conor Grant were also absent from the matchday squad of 18 against Forest Green, but that was purely due to selection.

Even without Clarke, Lowe still had three strikers on the bench in Ryan Taylor, Dom Telford – who both played as second half substitutes – and Zak Rudden.

Lowe said: “Will Aimson and Conor Grant just missed out so they were the two spare men. We took 20 lads.

“Billy Clarke, we left him at home because in shooting at training on Friday he just tweaked his groin.

“He will be alright, he just feels it needs to settle down. Bill is a seasoned pro and wanted to make sure it was right.

“So rather than me putting him on the bench, him sitting there cold and stiffening up, I have have got a squad and we had Zak and Tayls.”
