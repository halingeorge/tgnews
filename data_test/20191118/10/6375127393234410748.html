A commitment

18 Nov 2019, 10:07

WITH or without emergency powers, President Duterte is still committed to pursue with vigor and determination the national government’s highly-ambitious “Build, Build, Build” program. 

In fact, President Duterte is seen to maximize the use of his “available powers” to ensure the full implementation of the government’s flagship projects before he leaves Malacanang in 2022.

The Chief Executive’s priorities, aside from his infrastructure program, are the reduction, if not total eradication of official corruption, ending the “drug menace” and addressing criminality.

Presidential Spokesman Salvador Panelo said he considered as “good idea” the proposal to grant emergency powers to Duterte to speed up the implementation of the infrastructure program.

Last Wednesday, Albay 2nd District Rep. Joey Salceda filed House Bill No. 5456, which seeks to grant special powers to President Duterte to fast-track the BBB program’s implementation.

But Panelo, also the chief presidential counsel, was quick to explain that the proposal is “no longer a priority legislative agenda” of the Duterte administration considering the time constraint.  

Under the 1987 Constitution, the six-year presidency of tough-talking Duterte, the first Mindanaoan to occupy the top political post of the land, is set to end at 12 noon on June 30, 2022.

Panelo described the proposed measure as “belated” considering the fact that “we only have less than three years left in office.”

In the view of his still growing army of supporters and admirers, Duterte is in a position to realize his dream of transforming sleepy barangays in the countryside into flourishing communities.

To achieve this, the Duterte administration, with the support of the Filipino people, should hasten the implementation and completion of projects under the “Build, Build, Build” program.
