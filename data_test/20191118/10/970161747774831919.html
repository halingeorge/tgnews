Kim K reveals she almost lost $10m in Kimono cultural appropriation scandal

18 Nov 2019, 10:16

Kim Kardashian’s cultural appropriation scandal played out on Sunday’s episode of Keeping Up With the Kardashians – where the stay is seen panicking over the offensive name of her shapewear line.

The reality star came under fire after announcing her new line of underwear would be called Kimono – a traditional Japanese dress with symbolic meaning to the country.

As a result, she was called out for appropriating the name and was begged to consider a rethink of the brand that would disrespect the Japanese culture.

Even the Mayor of Kyoto, Japan, penned an open letter to the star asking her to come up with another name.

A panicked Kim shared her business worries with Scott Disick, claiming the change could cost $10million (£7.71million)

‘Who would ever think that hey, I would want to do something that would be disrespectful?’ she told him.

‘They’re saying that I’m cultural appropriating.’

She added that until the official announcement, no one – including the retailers buying the garments – had thought that it was an issue.

Kim admitted later on that it was ‘foolish and ignorant’ of her to think that.

Reading the letter in the confessionals, Kim said: ‘”Kimono is a traditional ethnic dress, and truly symbolizes sense of beauty, spirits and value of Japanese.”’

‘Reading that letter I felt an understanding. I definitely did not want to disrespect a culture,’ she added.

‘That’s not what the brand is even about. It’s about inclusivity, it’s about comfort. It’s not about making people feel uncomfortable.’

Thankfully, after quick thinking from her team, they were able to rebrand without the $10million price tag – and Kim apologised publicly and vowed to change it up, thanking people for making her aware of the issue.

After looking through names including Kim Barely and KKW (Kim Kardashian Wear), she eventually settled on the (seriously much better) name of SKIMS.

In a photo, she declared: ‘My fans and followers are a huge inspiration to me – I’m always listening to their feedback and opinions, and am so grateful they shared their ideas for a new brand name.

After much thought and consideration, I’m excited to announce the launch of SKIMS Solutionwear.’
