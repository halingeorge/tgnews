Ronda Rousey confirms WWE retirement after Total Divas episode

UFC legend Ronda Rousey has previously spoken about quitting WWE and starting a family with husband Travis Browne. Her last wrestling appearance came at Wrestlemania

18 Nov 2019, 10:20 by Alex Smith

Ronda Rousey has hinted that her WWE career is over after her Wrestlemania defeat.

Former UFC champion Rousey hasn't been seen on any WWE show since her loss at the showpiece event at the MetLife Stadium in April.

'Rowdy' suffered a defeat to Becky Lynch in the inaugural all-women main event, which also saw Charlotte Flair involved.

The 32-year-old joined the production in 2018, following the culmination of his MMA career, and became a regular on RAW.

But Rousey promoted the upcoming episode of the reality TV series Total Divas, which will cover her historic match at Mania - claiming that was her "flaming glory."

Rousey  tweeted : “#WrestleMania is finally here and I’m ready to go out in flaming glory!!! Don’t miss the climax of my @WWE career on @totaldivas.”

Ex-Olympian has previously hinted about moving away from the sport and creating a family with husband Travis Browne.

“It just sort of snowballed into, instead of being like, a small detour in my life, became my whole life for an entire year and I completely fell in love with it," she said on her YouTube channel.

"As time went on in the company I ended up getting the title and it became a real possibility that women could be the main event in WrestleMania.

“I decided to stick around and we planned, after WrestleMania, me and my amazing husband here would go off and start trying to start our family.

“As for WWE plans in the future, we want to have a baby first. I don’t know what it’s like to have a baby.

“I could look down at this beautiful child and be like, ‘F*** everything, I don’t care about anything else other than this baby’ and, you’ll never see me again."

Treple H insisted last month that wrestling fans would see Rousey back in the WWE ring "sooner rather than later".

The former WWE world champion confirmed: "She's coming back."
