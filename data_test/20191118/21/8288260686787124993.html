'Makes Bebe move look good' - Manchester United fans react to Aston Villa transfer development

Latest Aston Villa transfer news | Fresh report claims Man Utd have John McGinn and Jack Grealish on their wishlist

18 Nov 2019, 21:06 by Shane Ireland, https://www.facebook.com/ShaneIrelandJournalist

The John McGinn to Manchester United rumour has taken off again after the Aston Villa midfielder was named on an Old Trafford transfer shortlist.

McGinn was first linked with United in the summer after a terrific campaign helping Villa to promotion from the Championship.

It was even claimed at the time that legendary boss Sir Alex Ferguson was pushing for a move having watched the Scotland international’s development closely since he moved south of the border.

No bid was forthcoming then with McGinn going on to put pen to paper on a new and improved five-year contract.

 

But there is fresh speculation that United could attempt to raid Villa Park after McGinn was included on a list of eight players Ole Gunnar Solskjaer's side want to sign.

His midfield team-mate Jack Grealish was also part of the group of targets revealed by ESPN as well as the likes of Borussia Dortmund's Jadon Sancho and Leicester City's James Maddison.

Reacting to the news, one United fan said: "McGinn moved to Villa for £2.9 million a few months ago and now he is a £50m player?! How much more of a laughing stock can Utd make of themselves should we accede to those demands? It would make the Bebe transfer look like good business! Not happening in my honest opinion!"

"He’s not worth £50m, but then United have set the bar," said a second supporter. "Firstly by only seemingly looking at signing ‘young, hungry’ British talent, who inevitably come at a premium price; and then signing Aaron Wan-Bissaka, after only one season in the @premierleague, for £50m, when worth half that."

 

Another added: "Is it only me that thinks Sancho is very overrated? I would take McGinn over him any day."

A fourth said: "Unpopular Opinion - I'd take McGinn over Sancho for our right wing/right sided midfield every day of the week. Sancho is already a superstar who will be expensive and on high wages...I want hungry players who want to play for the badge."
