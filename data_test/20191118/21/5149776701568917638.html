The Nottingham Forest star ranked in Championship's 25 most valuable players

Nottingham Forest latest | Sabri Lamouchi's Forest squad are battling at the top of the Championship table - but how does their squad rate compared to their rivals?

18 Nov 2019, 21:19 by Josh Challies, Josh Murray

Nottingham Forest have put themselves in contention for a genuine shot at promotion out of the Championship this season.

The Reds sit fifth in the table after 16 games, with 28 points to their name from eight wins, four draws and three defeats.

Sabri Lamouchi will be looking to ensure his side continue the momentum they have built during the first third of the campaign, as he pushes to get the best out of his City Ground squad.

But how highly rated is Lamouchi's squad?

The astronomical prices in the transfer market are widely reported and new data has identified which players in the Championship are worth the most money.

Collated by Transfermarkt, the prices reflect the value of each player and would not be the fees they would receive if they were to complete transfers, which is why some of the valuations may not be as you would consider.

Overall, Fulham striker Aleksandar Mitrovic is ranked as the most valuable player with a mouthwatering valuation of £22.5m, putting him £9m ahead of team-mate Tom Cairney in second place.

Despite Stoke City's struggles this season, Jack Butland is joint third with a valuation of £10.8m, with Brentford’s Ollie Watkins, Leeds United’s Helder Costa, Fulham’s Alfie Mawson and Huddersfield Town’s Terence Kongolo all given the same price.

Fulham have the most players in the top 25 with a total of six, while Stoke City and Huddersfield Town both have four, with Leeds United on three and Brentford on two as the only other clubs to have more than one.

Nottingham Forest are joined by Cardiff City, Middlesbrough, Hull City, Blackburn Rovers and Bristol City in having one player in the top 25.
