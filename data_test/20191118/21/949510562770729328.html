Broome County Catholic Charities kicks off annual turkey collection

18 Nov 2019, 21:27 by Dan Conklin

VESTAL (WBNG) — As Thanksgiving approaches, Catholic Charities of Broome County is giving you the chance to ensure everyone in Broome County has food on their table this year.

The annual Thanks-4-Giving Turkey Drive is officially underway, collecting Thanksgiving turkeys, as well as other non-perishable food items, to fight hunger in Broome County this holiday season.

Organizers with Catholic Charities say these holiday baskets can help those in need enjoy a holiday dinner, something many people take for granted.

“We’re trying to fill those baskets now and make sure everyone gets what they need. There’s a lot of people in need in Broome County. There’s a poverty rate that’s quite higher than it is in the rest of New York State, so we just want to make sure we’re doing our part,” explained Mary Whitcombe.

Organizers will be in front of Walmart in Vestal’s Town Square Mall from Monday, November 18th through Friday November 22nd, collecting Thanksgiving turkeys and other non-perishables. They say they hope to collect 3,500 turkeys this year.
