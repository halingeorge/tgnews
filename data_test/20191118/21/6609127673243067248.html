BLAZE HELL

Three people rushed to hospital as fire crews battle second Pollokshields blaze in last week

18 Nov 2019, 21:18 by Justin Bowie

THREE people were taken to hospital after a blaze broke out in the Pollokshields area of Glasgow.

Four fire engines rushed to Melville Street after being called out about 7pm - and crews remain at the scene.

It is the second inferno in Pollokshields after a building was devastated in last week's Albert Drive fire.

A Scottish Fire and Rescue spokesperson said: ""We were alerted at 6.58pm on Monday November 18 to a report of a dwelling fire at Melville Street in Pollokshields, Glasgow.

"Operations control mobilised four appliances where crews were met with a fire in a four storey building.

"Three casualties have been transferred into the care of the Scottish Ambulance Service and firefighters remain in attendance."

A Scottish Fire and Rescue spokesperson confirmed were tackling the blaze earlier.

They said: "We are in attendance at Melville Street.

"We have five appliances at the scene."

Police Scotland confirmed they are also at the blaze.

A spokesperson said: "We were made aware of the incident by the fire service just after 7pm.

"We are assisting at the scene."

Last week we told how a building collapsed in the same area of Glasgow after a horror blaze.

Shocking photos showed rubble and debris as old tenements in Pollokshields were razed to the ground.
