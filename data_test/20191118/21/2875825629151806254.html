Christmas comes early for two Waterford punters who won over €5.3m in Lotto prizes

"This win is a complete game-changer for us. We are renting a house at the moment so the dream of buying a house was always a difficult one for us"

18 Nov 2019, 21:52 by Ian Mangan

Christmas came early for two separate Waterford punters who walked away with over €5.3 million in Lotto and scratch card prizes.

The National Lottery confirmed that the lucky ticket holder who won the whopping €5,260,021 Lotto jackpot on Wednesday, October 23, has now collected their prize.

Their staggering win comes just as the festive season approaches and is sure to take the sting out of this year’s Christmas shopping.

The winning ticket was sold at the SuperValu store at the Kilbarry shopping centre in Waterford City and was the 8th Lotto jackpot of 2019 with over €50 million paid out in jackpot prizes alone this year.

And the Deise good luck didn’t stop there, as another Waterford winner scooped up a tasty win on a scratch card.

Thomas Coughlan from Waterford City got an early Christmas bonus after he won one of the €100,000 top prizes on a Christmas Countdown scratch card.

Lotto bosses confirmed that there is still one top prize remaining on the €10 Christmas scratch card.

Coughlan said he was ‘dumbstruck’ by his massive win which he says is a ‘game-changer’ for him and his fiancée Meghan Simpson and their four-year old son.

The young family have said they plan to spend their winnings on a new home and a wedding in the next 12 months.

He said, “Quite simply, this win is a complete game-changer for us. We are renting a house at the moment so the dream of buying a house was always a difficult one for us.

“Trying to save while renting isn’t ideal but with this win, we are that much closer to getting our forever home.

"And more importantly – we had to sacrifice getting married over buying a house so in the next year, we will have a new home and we will be married, we’re just absolutely thrilled.”

Coughlan, who works as an architect in Waterford City, bought the winning scratch card on his way home from work last Friday at O’Hare’s supermarket on the Dunmore Road.

He added, “It’s something we always do every single Friday – we treat ourselves to a scratch card to celebrate the start to the weekend.
