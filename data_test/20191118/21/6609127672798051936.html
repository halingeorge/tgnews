Scottish Labour election poster boy threw street party celebrating Margaret Thatcher’s death

18 Nov 2019, 21:41 by Chris Musson

A YOB union activist who threw a street party to celebrate Margaret Thatcher’s death is the poster boy of a Scottish Labour election push.

Unite organiser Bryan Simpson, who invited 8,000 people to the morbid bash, fronts a broadcast where he chats to Jeremy Corbyn and Scots leader Richard Leonard.

In the 54-second film, Mr Simpson says: “Only through a Labour government are we going to be able to give workers the power that they need to fight back.”

Mr Simpson led a conga around Glasgow’s George Square on April 8, 2013 — the day ex-PM Mrs Thatcher died aged 87.

The law graduate, from Glasgow, was also given a four-month suspended prison sentence for his part in a street protest over tuition fees in London in 2010.

He was caught on CCTV shouting abuse and tearing off a police medic’s cap, as well as flinging a megaphone towards cops.

It missed and hit another protester in the head.

Scottish Tory MSP Annie Wells said: “This individual is unfit to be the public face of any reputable organisation — let alone a poster boy for a political party ahead of a major election.

“It tells you everything you need to know about Scottish Labour that they’d revert to this level of decision-making.

“Party bosses should have known exactly who he was, what he’d done, and why he’s unsuitable for this purpose.”

Mr Simpson declined to comment.
