Chance to live in popular Mid Devon village close to sought-after school

This four-bed family house in Sampford Peverell has a lot to offer

18 Nov 2019, 21:24 by Tina Penberthy-Hutchings

The Mid Devon village of Sampford Peverell has long been a sought-after area with house hunters, but just what makes it so popular?

With a thriving community, excellent transport links, and walks along the picturesque Grand Western Canal all on the doorstep, the village has a lot to offer.

Even better, if you are a family looking for a home in the catchment area of one of the region’s most sought-after secondary schools, Sampford Peverell is also within the Uffculme School catchment.

At the end of a cul de sac in a small development, No.18 Cornlands is a spacious four-bedroom house just a short walk from the centre of the village.

Boasting some far-reaching countryside views and fitted with solar panels, the detached property offers driveway parking, a single garage, and plenty of living accommodation.

Step inside and you will find an entrance hall, cloakroom, a sitting room opening to the conservatory, and a kitchen and breakfast room.

For more formal dining there is a separate dining room, and completing the ground-floor accommodation is a handy utility room.

The first floor is home to the four double bedrooms, accessed from a central landing which also leads to the family bathroom. Helping to cut the morning bathroom queues, the master bedroom has its own en-suite.

Outside, the rear garden is split into two distinct areas. A large sunny patio sits immediately to the rear of the house.

Bordered by a pretty picket fence, the patio could be a perfect place for summer entertaining with direct access into the kitchen and the conservatory creating a great indoor outdoor feel.

Beyond the patio, a gently sloping lawn area is a great area for children to let off steam or for you to simply relax with a cuppa in the sunshine.

When you want to stretch your legs further, No.18 Cornlands is just a short walk from the Grand Western Canal and the lovely country walks along its towpath.

Head into the village centre and you will discover an active community with a village hall, a primary school, a lovely old church, a post office and store, doctors’ surgery and two pubs.

For those looking for an easy commute, Sampford Peverell is home to Tiverton Parkway railway station, as well as being about two miles from junction 27 of the M5 motorway.

The village also has a good bus service into Tiverton which lies about seven miles to the west.

No.18 Cornlands is on the market for offers in the region of £345,000.
