Poppy Appeal collection tin stolen from Flute and Flagon pub in Solihull

Police have issued pictures of a man and a woman who may have information

18 Nov 2019, 21:11 by birminghammail.co.uk

Police have issued images of a man and woman who may have information about the theft of a Poppy Appeal collection tin from a pub in Solihull town centre.

The box was swiped from the Flute and Flagon off Station Parade, next to the Mell Shopping Centre, at around 7pm on November 4.

Solihull Police Partnership tweeted: “Can you help Police ID this man and woman.

"We’d like to speak to them about the theft of a Poppy charity collection box from The Flute and Flagon, Solihull around 7pm on 4/11/19. Any info.

"Report online https://west-midlands.police.uk Quote ref 20SH/261315U/19.”

A service was held at St Alphege Church, Solihull, on November 11, to remember the fallen from two World Wars and other conflicts.

Other parades and services were held in Shirley, Knowle, Dickens Heath.
