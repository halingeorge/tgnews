You can easily adjust the brightness of your iPad screen – manually or automatically.

To better see your messages, photos, and other apps, you can manually adjust the brightness level at any time in the Control Center.

The Control Center is accessible from any screen by swiping down in the top right-hand corner on iPads running iPadOS 12 or later (or by swiping up from the bottom of the screen on older iPads), and it offers shortcuts to adjust the volume, enable Do Not Disturb mode, and more.

You can also enable auto-brightness in the Settings app; this feature will adjust your screen’s visibility without you ever having to think about it.

This section of Settings is also where you’ll find options such as turning on automatic night shift, a feature that makes it easier to read from your Apple device at night.

Here’s how to adjust your iPad’s brightness.

How to adjust the brightness on an iPad in the Control Center

1. Swipe down anywhere at the top of the right hand side of your iPad screen if your iPad is running iPadOS 12 or later, or swipe up from the bottom of your screen on older iPads.

2. This will open the Control Center, which includes shortcuts for locking your screen orientation and activating “Do Not Disturb.” It also includes adjustment bars for both brightness and volume.

3. Slide your finger up or down on the adjustment option labeled with a sun. The sun’s rays will grow as your screen gets brighter.

How to adjust the brightness on an iPad in Settings

1. Open the Settings app on your iPad.

2. Tap the “Display & Brightness” tab on the left hand side.

3. There you’ll find a slide bar to adjust the brightness of your screen. Slide to the left to make the screen dimmer and slide to the right to make the screen brighter. You’ll also find adjustments here to turn on Night Shift.

How to enable auto-brightness on an iPad

1. Open the Settings app on your iPad.

2. Tap the “General” tab on the left hand side.

3. Tap “Accessibility” from the list of options.

4. You’ll now see all of the Accessibility settings you can change. Tap “Display Accommodations.” If any settings have been activated, it should be labeled “On.”

5. Here you’ll find options to adjust your screen’s color and brightness. Switch the auto-brightness feature on by sliding or tapping the toggle to change from gray to green. Your iPad will note that auto-brightness may affect battery life.
