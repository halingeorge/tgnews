Player Ratings As Spirited Ireland Draw With Denmark In Dublin

18 Nov 2019, 21:43 by Robert Redmond

The Republic of Ireland 1 Denmark 1.

Ireland have failed to qualify directly for Euro 2020 and will need to come through a Nations League play-off next March after failing to beat the old enemy Denmark in Dublin.

Mick McCarthy’s side were aggressive, spirited and committed, and the Danes did not, by any means, set the world alight at the Aviva Stadium, but they could only record a 1-1 draw. Martin Braithwaite opened the scoring before Matt Doherty’s equaliser sparked a grandstand finish to the game. However, unfortunately, Ireland could not find a winner and will now have to go through a play-off in a few months.

Here is how we rated the Ireland players:

Darren Randolph – 6

The Middlesbrough goalkeeper has been Ireland’s most consistent performer for at least two years. Randolph has saved Ireland on several occasions and boosted their chances of qualification by returning from injury for the visit of the Danes. He was rarely tested on Monday night, with the Danes often mirroring Ireland by playing direct.

Matt Doherty – 7

Doherty got his chance at right-back due to Seamus Coleman, Ireland’s captain, being suspended. Earning just his ninth cap, the Wolves wing-back was defensively sound for most of the match, winning his fair share of aerial challenges. In the second half, we saw more of his natural game going forward. He went on one impressive run, taking a couple of Danes out of play before passing to space, and was a calm presence on the ball. A solid display that was almost marred when he switched off for the Danish goal. He redeemed himself with an excellent back-post header to set up a frantic finale to the game.

Shane Duffy – 7

Duffy, who captained Ireland for the first time on Monday night, was the only Irish player to score against the Danes in the team’s five previous matches over the last two years.
