Rylan Clark-Neal to co-host Channel 4's election night coverage

18 Nov 2019, 21:50

Rylan Clark-Neal is trying his hand at politics after being announced as the co-host for Channel 4’s Alternative Election Night.

Continuing his total domination of all TV channels, all genres and all topics, Rylan will join Channel 4 News anchor Krishnan Guru-Murphy on screen for the eight hour long live event while the results come in for December’s General Election.

After his 24 hour karaoke challenge, we’re sure that eight hours is a walk in the park for Rylan.

Also joining the pair on screen will be Canadian stand up Katherine Ryan and Clare Balding, while former MPs Tom Watson and Amber Rudd will provide additional political commentary.

Hoping to bring some fun to what can be a very long and tedious night, the Alternative Election Night promises to be ‘distinctive, fresh and very entertaining, offering up the perfect mix of two political heavyweights who’ve just walked out of front-line politics as well some great comedians and entertainers.’

Excitedly sharing the news on Twitter, 31-year-old Rylan wrote: ‘So yeah…. This X factor reject will be hosting election night. Lols.’

Talk about a glow up.

From X Factor and Celebrity Big Brother contestant to presenting Strictly’s It Takes Two, it’s been quite the year for Rylan.

And the star decided to tie it up by raising a whopping £1 million for Children in Need as he took part in an exhausting karaoke challenge.

During the 24-hour stint, Rylan duetted with the likes of Craig David, Girls Aloud star Kimberley Walsh, Strictly and RuPaul’s Drag Race star Michelle Visage, Frankie Bridge from The Saturdays, M People’s Heather Small and even his own husband Dan Neal.

After completing the challenge at around 9:15am on Wednesday, Rylan collapsed on the floor in tears and was pretty much speechless after belting out Tina Turner’s The Best as his last song.

Rylan, we salute you!
