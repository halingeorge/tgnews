Man charged with murder after body of Edinburgh mum dumped in wheelie bin

Nicola Stevenson, 39, was found dead by a dog walker and a 41-year-old suspect is due to appear in court today.

18 Nov 2019, 11:29 by Dailyrecord.co.uk

A man has been charged with the murder of an Edinburgh mum whose body was found in a wheelie bin at a recreation ground.

The body of 39-year-old Nicola Stevenson was found by a dog walker in the bin at the edge of a recreation ground off in Lewes, East Sussex.

Richard Canlin, 41, of no fixed address, was charged with murder on Sunday and is due to appear at Brighton Magistrates' Court on today.

 

The grim discovery was made at 12.30pm last Wednesday and police launched a full-scale investigation.

Detective Chief Inspector Chris Friday, from the Surrey and Sussex Major Crime Team said: "The investigation teams have been working tirelessly to progress this case and ensure justice is sought for Nicola and her family and friends.

"Despite the charging of a man, we are still desperate to hear from anyone who may be able to add to our investigation timeline.

 

"We are urging anyone who saw Nicola before her body was found to come forward to us as you may hold vital information to aid our ongoing investigation.

"For those of you living in local community to Nicola, you will still see our officers out and about conducting enquiries; please do go and speak to them if you have anything to add."

 

Search and forensic teams have concluded their work at the recreation ground but will continue at Ms Stevenson's home in nearby Stansfield Road.

A 37-year-old man arrested on suspicion of murder on Thursday has been released on police bail until December 12.
