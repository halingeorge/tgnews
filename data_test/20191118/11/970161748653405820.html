Labour promise immediate £6,000 pay rise for every worker

18 Nov 2019, 11:53

Labour has pledged to increase minimum wage to £10-a-hour which they claim would give people an immediate £6,000 pay rise.

The party claims its ‘real’ living wage for all workers over 16 will give approximately 7.5 million people a pay rise, including more than 1.2 million young people.

Workers who currently earn the national minimum wage would get a rise of at least £9,000 more by 2024 than under the Tories, Labour claims.

The party says workers would get an immediate pay rise of between £3,444 and £5,986 under the party’s plans if they win the election on 12 December.

The current minimum wage for people 25 and over is £8.21, while for 16 to 17-year-olds it is £4.35.

Labour says workers aged 25 and over will get an average pay rise of £3,444, while 16 to 18-year-olds will get an average pay rise of £3,497.

It says those aged 18 to 20 will get an average pay rise of £5,986 and 21 to 24-year-olds will get an average pay rise of £4,485.

Labour leader Jeremy Corbyn said: ‘Labour’s Real Living Wage will put more money into the pockets of the workers that need it most and give a boost to the economy.

‘Over the last decade, the Tories have allowed in-work poverty to soar and left millions in insecure jobs.

‘Labour will deliver real change for the many, not the few.’

But the Treasury said the party’s ‘spending splurge’ would ultimately hit workers with a hike in taxes.

Rishi Sunak, Chief Secretary to the Treasury, said: ‘Since 2010, under the Conservatives, the lowest paid workers have £4,500 more in their pockets thanks to increases in the living wage and tax cuts.

‘Whilst we’ve been working to make sure you keep more of the money you earn, Jeremy Corbyn’s Labour would take the equivalent of a month’s extra wages from you after hitting you with higher taxes to pay for their spending splurge.’

Today, Mr Corbyn dismissed ‘nonsense’ claims he is ‘anti-business’ during a speech to the Confederations of British Industry (CBI).

He said a Labour government would bring ‘more investment’ to businesses than they have ‘ever dreamt of’.

The Labour leader said: ‘It’s not anti-business to be against poverty pay.

‘It’s not anti-business to say that the largest corporations should pay their taxes just as smaller companies do.

‘And it’s not anti-business to want prosperity in every part of our country – not only in the financial centres of the City of London.

‘I say this to business too: if a Labour government is elected on 12 December, you’re going to see more investment than you’ve ever dreamt of.

‘You’re going to have the best educated workforce you could ever have hoped for, and you’re going to get the world-leading infrastructure, including full fibre broadband you’ve long, long demanded, year after year at these conferences.’
