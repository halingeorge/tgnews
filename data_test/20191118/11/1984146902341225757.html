Pro-Brexit protesters 'disperse' after police attend Blaydon Roundabout demonstration

Northumbria Police say no crime was committed but that the group dispersed after speaking to officers

18 Nov 2019, 11:43 by Hannah Graham

Demonstrators calling for Britain to leave the EU "dispersed" from their spot on a busy roundabout after being approached by police.

Two men, who appeared to be supporters of the Brexit Party, unfurled a banner on the Blaydon Roundabout, on the A695 in Gateshead, early on Monday.

But by 8.30am the pair and their poster, which read "We voted Leave", had left the area after police were called.

Gateshead mum Josephine Bridger contacted Chronicle Live after seeing officers speaking to the group, branding the intervention "ridiculous".

She claimed police cars which had pulled up near the roundabout were causing more traffic problems than the demonstrators, who were on foot.

She said: "I was taking my son to school and as we approached Blaydon Roundabout we saw there were a couple of men there, standing on the grass with signs protesting for Brexit.

"On my way back there were two police cars there. The two men were just silently protesting, just with a poster. I just couldn't believe it when I saw the police were there, I thought 'have they got nothing better to do?'."

Josephine said she believed the pair didn't pose a safety risk.

She said: "At the end of the day, people have the right to protest and I can't see why the police are getting involved, it's ridiculous.

"I just thought, 'how stupid' - it's uneccesary. They talk about police being under-manned: I'm sorry, but if they can get out to something like this they're not."

The location of the protest divided opinion, however.

On Twitter, one person accused the men of "wasting police time".  And on a local Facebook group, several people expressed safety concerns.

The group's banner bore the Brexit Party's logo, which is shaped like an arrow pointing right - in the opposite direction to the flow of traffic on a rounabout.

One said: "There are ways to protest but not like this. That round about is a nightmare and very busy. They could have caused a nasty accident."

A Northumbria Police spokesperson said “We have received a report of concern for a group of individuals who were believed to be protesting on a roundabout in Blaydon shortly after 7.30am today.

"Officers attended and spoke to the individuals who then dispersed. No criminal offences had been carried out and no one was injured.”

It's understood that the officers did not tell the protesters to leave, but spoke to them and offered "safety advice".

Blaydon is one of the constituencies being targeted by Nigel Farage's Brexit Party in the upcoming election, and is putting forward Michael Robinson as its candidate.
