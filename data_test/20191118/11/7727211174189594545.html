Social media users react after 'Jack Sparrow' fake gun found in bushes

Police were initially concerned by the find - but it ended up sparking plenty of jokes online

18 Nov 2019, 11:56 by Oliver Clay

An imitation firearm sparked concern from police - while others shared a joke about the 'Jack Sparrow' - style pretend weapon.

Police in Widnes posted on its Twitter and Facebook feeds that a community support officer (PCSO) had discovered the apparent flintlock pistol in bushes on Scott Avenue on Saturday.

The gun turned out to be a fake.

The find led to plenty of jokes online, with many residents drawing comparisons with the guns used by Jack Sparrow, the leading character in Disney's Pirates of the Caribbean films.

One commenter said: "Suspect has been sighted captaining an ocean going vessel by the name of the Black Pearl."

Another said: "How quaint, possibly could have been used in a ye olde avo duel! I thought chivalry was dead, how wrong I am."

The item even drew the attention of a poster in North America, who said: "I am from Canada and quality contents (SIC) like this one attracted me.

"You don’t want the peasants and blacksmith to storm the castle."
 

Another person said: "Jesus Christ who hid that? Someone from the Napoleon war in 1803?"

One Facebook user had a more practical idea - and said: "If it’s an imitation fire arm could it not be given to a local theatre group as a prop?

"Seems more worthwhile than destroying it if it poses no danger."
