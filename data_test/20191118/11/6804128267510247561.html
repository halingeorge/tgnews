'So poor!' Harry Redknapp predicts Arsenal and Tottenham Premier League finishes

Harry Redknapp has been talking about the Premier League top-four race

18 Nov 2019, 11:00 by Paul Clarke

Former Tottenham manager Harry Redknapp has provided his verdict on which teams will make the Premier League top four.

Liverpool and Manchester City are widely expected to be the league's top two come next May despite the Reds' nine-point advantage over Pep Guardiola's men at the moment.

But when it comes to the rest of the top six or seven teams, there are many differing opinions on how the 2019/20 campaign is going to pan out.

Redknapp, for one, is backing Chelsea and Leicester City to keep up their form and retain their spots in the top four, but it's bad news for Arsenal and Manchester United, while Spurs are also in a tough position.

Unai Emery is under pressure at Arsenal following a rough start that has the Gunners eight points behind fourth.

United are nine points back, while Spurs are a remarkable eleven points off the top four.

Redknapp told The Sun: "Tottenham have it all on now to get in the top four this year.

"The only thing now in their favour is that Arsenal look so poor. Chelsea are doing great, they’re going to be challenging. Leicester are going to be bang in there.

"But you’re looking at Arsenal — and Manchester United — and wondering if they are going to make it this year. I can see both of those two not making the top four unless there is a big improvement.

"It’s been a disappointing season so far for Spurs. I really thought they would be close to Manchester City and Liverpool and challenging.

"It’s difficult. The players haven’t hit the form they did last year. I’m a big fan of the manager and hopefully they can turn it around."
