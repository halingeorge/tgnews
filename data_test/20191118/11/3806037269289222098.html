END. Re-Engineers Classic Barbour Jackets for FW19

Constructed from a patchwork of different textures.

18 Nov 2019, 11:00 by Jack Stanley

UK-based retailer END. has linked up with heritage outerwear label Barbour for its latest exclusive offering, following capsules with Rick Owens DRKSHDW and Off-White™ earlier this year. The new collection focuses on two classic Barbour pieces — the Ashby wax jacket and Baffle fishing vest — both of which have been re-engineered.

The re-engineering process — which gives the capsule its name — sees pieces constructed from a patchwork of different textures and materials. The Ashby wax jacket is made from a 6oz Sylkoil and 4oz lightweight waxed cotton as well as an 8 wale corduroy, while the fishing vest features a diamond quilting pattern on the front and curved quilting on the reverse, as well as a 4oz and 2oz waxed cotton lining for water resistance. Both pieces are available in “Olive” and “Navy” and are finished with co-branded Tyvek patches.

Take a look at the collection in the gallery above, and expect all pieces to arrive at the END. web store on November 23.
