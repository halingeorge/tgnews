Man gets stuck seven metres in the air while putting up Christmas lights

18 Nov 2019, 11:43

A man trying to put up Christmas lights ended up stranded so long he almost became part of the display.

He was stuck for around two hours on a malfunctioning cherry-picker platform which would not go lower than seven metres off the ground.

The landlady of a pub next door was trying to figure out how to bring him a pint up there, but he was finally rescued.

Firefighters in Market Borough, Leicestershire, came to his aid and brought him back down to the ground yesterday afternoon.

Angela Denison manages the Nags Head pub on Church Street, said: ‘I was thinking poor old Church Street never has it easy when it comes to Christmas illuminations, does the man stuck up there want a pint and it’s a good job it’s not raining.

‘Our customers enjoyed the unexpected entertainment, the ladies loved the arrival of all those fireman, especially when the impressive rescue equipment arrived.’

She said around 20 to 25 people stopped to watch the ‘thrilling rescue’ and ‘seriously embarrassed’ light fitter.

Leicestershire Fire and Rescue received an emergency call about the incident a little after 2pm.

They used an aerial ladder platform to help him get down.

The fire service added the local council is arranging for the cherry picker to be recovered.
