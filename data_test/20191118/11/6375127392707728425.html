Game of death

18 Nov 2019, 11:34 by Theodore P. Jurado

Lions, Knights clash for all the marbles.

SAN BEDA and Letran duke it out for one final time today in a NCAA men’s basketball championship showdown that has surpassed a lot of expectations before a crowd that’s expected to fill every nook and cranny of the Mall of Asia Arena.

In what expected to be a cakewalk for the four-peat seeking Red Lions has turned out to be a protracted battle as the Knights gave their bitter rivals everything they could handle in the series.

Letran stole Game 1, 65-64, before San Beda extended its reign with a series-tying 79-76 victory, setting the stage for the 4 p.m. rubber match.

The Lions are gunning for a record 23rd crown while the Knights are seeking to raise championship banner No. 18.

This will be the fourth straight time that the San Beda-Letran Finals series went into distance.

The Lions won the deciding games in 2012 and 2013, while the Knights emerged triumphant in 2015 in a classic Game 3 that went into overtime.

Newly-minted MVP Calvin Oftana played the hero’s role Game 2, completing a three-point play to put San Beda up, 77-76, with 19.1 seconds left.

Letran tried to complete and celebrate another storybook season, but Bonbon Batiller bungled the potential championship-clinching lay-up in the dwindling seconds.

Clinching a morale-boosting win, coach Boyet Fernandez hopes his squad will use this to fuel the Lions in this no-tomorrow match and not give the Knights an inch.

“I hope we sustain this one. Playing a tough team like Letran that will be hard for us but again I do thank my players, thank the Lord for this,” said Fernandez.

The Lions got a huge break late in the game when Fran Yu, who is scintillating in the semifinals and in the championship round, suffered cramps.

Expect Yu to be in better shape and help Letran regain the throne.

“No one really expected us to be here and go toe-to-toe with them and also give them their first loss after 32 victories,” said Knights coach Bonnie Tan.

San Beda and Lyceum of the Philippines University dispute the juniors title at 1 p.m.
