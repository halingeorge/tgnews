Cakes, spots and Pudsey ears see Mid Devon make hundreds for Children in Need

Events took place on Friday, November 15

18 Nov 2019, 11:28 by Lewis Clarke

Pudsey ears, spotty clothes and lots of cakes have helped people across Mid Devon contribute to a £47.9m Children in Need total.

Events took place on Friday, November 15 where in Tiverton, Park Hill Daycare’s staff and children dressed in brightly coloured clothes and made donations of £1 towards the charity. The team were particularly impressed by Dan’s bright yellow suit.

Bolham Primary school held a non-uniform day and cake sale. Headteacher Denise Woodgate said: “Once again the parents and families have been extremely generous and the children raised over £192 for this year’s event.”

Tidcombe School was a sea of yellow. Children created moveable Pudsey Bears, Pudsey collage and even performing a Pudsey dance. The final amount raised was £158.50.

Hepco dressed down and had a cake sale, and took part in the annual duck race sweepstake and raised in excess of £500.

Exe Valley Leisure Centre rowed the English Channel and back; a total of 83,685.9 metres in total. Each person took twenty minutes per person, with a staff member present.

In Cullompton a coffee morning was held at the Community Centre and there was a service at St Andrew’s Church organised by Sarah Cornish.

She said: “The day has been fantastic. I have loved doing something for Children in Need. It’s a great cause.” 

Willowbank Primary School held activities all week including raffles, filling Pudsey's face with 2p pieces, adopting a Pudsey, naming a Pudsey, a cake sale and a non-uniform day where pupils wore their favourite colour to school. They raised £568.00.

Clyst Hydon Primary School combined their literacy and mathematics skills, added in a bit of technology and baked batches of gluten-free biscuits in the shape of Pudsey Bear.
