Will Young and James Morrison to co-headline Forest Live summer gigs

The pair will be playing Westonbrit Arboretum near Tetbury in Gloucestershire

18 Nov 2019, 11:40 by Jasper King

Will Young and James Morrison have announced they are co-headlining Forest Live, the summer concert series presented by Forestry England

They'll be performing at the Westonbirt Arboretum near Tetbury on Friday 12th June 2020.

Tickets will go on sale this Friday (22 November) at 9am and will cost £49.50 (plus a £5.95 booking fee) via seetickets.com.

The pair have both appeared as Forest Live solo acts in the past and have said they are excited to return to the series, which helps protect the environment.

Commenting on the tour, Will Young said: "Both James and I have fond memories of appearing as part of Forest Live as solo artists in the past. We love the work Forestry England does, so we are glad to be back again in what promises to be a fantastic double-header of a show."

James Morrison commented, "For the last 20 years Will has been at the forefront of British popular culture. That's a massive achievement. I think our sets will complement each other in a special way and I'm really looking forward to our shows together. It'll be a great night out!"

Will Young shot to fame after winning the inaugural series of Pop Idol which shot him to national fame.

He's had a chart-topping career including seven top 5 albums, four reaching the top spot and four number 1 singles such as 'Leave Right Now' and 'Jealousy'.

James Morrison was catapulted into the limelight following the release of his debut album, 'Undiscovered', singer-songwriter, James Morrison was catapulted into the limelight.

The album went to sell over a million copies and his five top 10 singles all rank among the biggest of their era, including the breakthrough smashes 'You Give Me Something' and 'Wonderful World', plus the huge Nelly Furtado collaboration 'Broken Strings'.

The summer concert will see both artists perform individual sets that include material from their latest albums: Will's 'Lexicon' and James's 'You're Stronger Than You Know'.

Going to a Forest Live concert helps look after the nation's forests sustainability, with income from ticket sales helping to create beautiful places and grow trees around forests in the UK.
